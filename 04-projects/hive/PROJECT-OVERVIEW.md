# Hive — Curation Channel

Not a delivery project: an ongoing **curation channel**. Hive is Belron's internal
innovation platform (`hive.belron.com`). COG's job is to **flag external content worth
sharing to Hive**, routed to the right community, with a one-line reason. Armo reviews
the queue and posts the winners manually — COG never auto-posts.

See the running list of flagged items in [[CANDIDATE-QUEUE]].

## Communities (routing targets)

Post candidates are tagged with the community they best fit. Current set (more to be
added later):

- **General Innovation** — broad: automotive industry, windscreen / auto-glass, and general innovation
- **AI Community** — how organisations are adopting AI, GenAI tooling, governance, real enterprise use cases
- **Windscreen Technician Community** — repair/replace techniques, materials, tools, ADAS recalibration, field innovation

## In-remit themes

Scope is **broad** — not bounded to Armo's active projects. In scope:

- **AI adoption in the wild** — how *other companies* approach AI: case studies, rollouts, lessons, org design *(proven winner on Hive — weight this highest)*
- **Anything automotive** — the industry broadly: EVs, ADAS, connected / software-defined vehicles, sensors, smart glass, HUD, auto-glass repair tech and materials
- **Anything tech** — AI, augmented / mixed reality, computer vision, agentic AI, robotics, and other emerging technology
- Customer experience & contact-centre innovation
- Operational / field-service innovation — technician tooling, computer-vision damage assessment, scheduling
- Future of work, ways of working, innovation culture
- Sustainability in operations, auto, or glass

## Quality bar — what makes a *strong* candidate

- **Novel** — tells the community something non-obvious
- **Credible** — named company / primary source / reputable outlet, not a thin listicle
- **Discussable** — a clear "what could this mean for us?" hook
- **Concrete** — a real example, result, or case, not pure punditry
- **Timely** — recent enough to feel current

## Flagging heuristic

Flag as a Hive candidate if it is **on-theme, routable to a community, and clears at
least 2 of {novel, credible, discussable, concrete}**. Otherwise skip silently.

## Exclude / downrank

- Generic AI hype with no specific company or example
- Vendor marketing dressed up as insight
- Already widely shared internally / old news
- Purely Belron-internal project material (belongs in the project file, not Hive)

## Candidate format

When COG flags something, it drops this into [[CANDIDATE-QUEUE]]:

> **[Title]** — *source · link*
> **Community:** AI Community
> **Why:** one-line fit + discussion hook
> *(optional: a 1–2 sentence intro to paste straight into Hive)*
