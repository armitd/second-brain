# Braindump: ServiceNow Is at Belron but Seen as an IT Niche Tool

## Raw Thoughts
We have ServiceNow at Belron, but it's seen as a niche system for IT — not something that could be an MCP server.

## Content Analysis

### Main Themes
1. **ServiceNow deployment gap:** Belron has ServiceNow, which means the AI Control Tower and MCP Server announced at Knowledge 2026 are technically available — but this fact alone doesn't translate into strategic leverage.
2. **Perception as the blocker:** The system is internally framed as an IT-only tool. That framing, not the technology, is what prevents it from being considered as a governance layer for the broader Belron agent ecosystem.
3. **Organisational politics of platform expansion:** Getting ServiceNow repositioned from "IT tool" to "enterprise AI governance platform" is a change management problem as much as a technical one.

### Questions Raised
- Who owns ServiceNow at Belron — IT only, or does the platform team have broader ambitions for it?
- Has the Knowledge 2026 announcement landed with the ServiceNow platform team at Belron? Do they even know the MCP Server is now available in their SKU?
- Is EA positioned to advocate for ServiceNow's expanded role, or would that be seen as overreach into IT's territory?
- Is the perception problem fixable, or would a different governance platform land better with the business stakeholders the MCP Governance project needs to persuade?

### Decisions Contemplated
- Whether to use ServiceNow's MCP Server as the governance layer for Belron's agent ecosystem, or build/procure something purpose-built
- Whether EA should actively work to shift internal perception of ServiceNow, or accept the constraint and route around it

## Strategic Intelligence

### Key Insights
1. **Having the tool is not the same as having the mandate.** ServiceNow's MCP Server is available at Belron, but technical availability means nothing if the platform is seen as IT-scoped. Using it for enterprise AI governance would require a deliberate repositioning campaign.
2. **The perception gap is information.** If business stakeholders don't accept ServiceNow as the governance layer, any solution built on it will face adoption friction regardless of how technically sound it is. This is a real constraint to design around.
3. **There may be an internal ServiceNow champion.** The IT team or ServiceNow platform owner at Belron may be very interested in the Knowledge 2026 announcements — and in expanding ServiceNow's footprint. EA and IT could be natural allies here, if the relationship is right.

### Pattern Recognition
- **Connection to previous thinking:** The MCP Governance project has always had an organisational dimension alongside the technical one. This is a concrete instance of that — the governance layer choice is as much about who will accept it as what it can do.
- **Recurring pattern:** New platform capabilities often exist before the internal narrative catches up. EA is well-placed to accelerate that narrative — but needs to decide whether it wants to.

### Strategic Implications
- If ServiceNow is off the table perceptually, the MCP governance architecture needs a different anchor — options include a purpose-built EA-owned governance layer, or leaning into one of the other vendors (Microsoft Azure AI Gateway, Apigee, or a lightweight open-source approach).
- If the perception is worth challenging, the strongest play is to brief the ServiceNow platform team on Knowledge 2026, let them get excited, and let them make the internal case — rather than EA pushing it top-down.
- The IPO context (from today's daily brief) adds urgency: a governance framework that's credible to investors needs to be built on something the business accepts, not just what technically works.
