# Braindump: Using Hive Signals to Run the CCOTF Community

## Raw Thoughts

Use Hive (Belron's internal innovation platform — hive.belron.com) as the home for the Contact Centre of the Future community. Specifically use the Signals feature.

---

## Content Analysis

### Main Themes

1. **Community over committee:** Running CCOTF as a community (not just a programme team) — engaging contact centre practitioners across Belron's opcos, not just the EA and programme core.
2. **Signals as ground truth:** Using Hive's Signals feature to surface ideas, pain points, and observations from people who actually work in or with Belron's contact centres — across 35 countries.
3. **Internal channel consolidation:** Hive already exists at Belron. Using it avoids fragmenting CCOTF engagement across Teams channels, SharePoint pages, and email.

### Supporting Ideas

- A CCOTF Hive community would give the programme breadth — contact centre managers, ops leads, IT teams, and BPO partners across Autoglass, Carglass, and Safelite all in one place
- Signals submitted by the community could feed directly into architecture decisions — especially the open issues (OI-01 capability boundary, OI-03 current platform knowledge, OI-06 insurer API complexity)
- Community engagement is also a change management move: people who contribute to a programme are more likely to adopt its outputs
- IPO context: demonstrable cross-opco engagement is evidence of programme maturity — not just an architectural artefact but a living community

### Questions Raised

- Who are the natural founding members of the CCOTF community on Hive? (CC ops managers, technology leads, CX/NPS owners per opco?)
- What is a "Signal" in the CCOTF community context? A pain point? A market observation? An idea for improvement? A competitive observation?
- How does the Hive community relate to formal CCOTF programme governance — is it input to the programme, or part of the programme's comms strategy?
- Is there already a Hive community for contact centre topics at Belron, or would this be net new?
- Who moderates it and how often should content be reviewed?

### Decisions Contemplated

- Whether to set up the community immediately and invite broadly, or start with a small group and grow
- Whether Signals is the right Hive feature (vs. a standard community post/discussion format)
- Whether the community should be scoped to CCOTF specifically or broader front-office / CX themes

---

## Strategic Intelligence

### Key Insights

1. **The community IS the change management plan.** CCOTF is a pan-Belron programme that will eventually affect every contact centre agent and manager across 35 countries. Building a community now — before the platform decision — means the people most affected are shaping the direction, not being told about it post-fact. This is the difference between a programme that lands and one that doesn't.

2. **Signals can resolve architecture unknowns.** The CCOTF reference architecture has 9 open issues. Several of them (OI-01 capability boundary, OI-03 current platform, OI-06 insurer connectivity) are unknowns because the EA team doesn't yet have line of sight into each opco. A Signals feed from a cross-opco community is a structured way to surface exactly this kind of ground-level intelligence — faster than formal workshops, and with broader coverage.

3. **Hive as the single source of community truth.** If CCOTF doesn't own a community channel, the conversation will happen anyway — in Teams chats, on email threads, in corridor conversations at CC Week. Owning the Hive community means the programme captures that intelligence and can act on it, rather than losing it to informal channels.

### Pattern Recognition

- **Connection to Architecture Principle AP-09 (multi-opco by default):** A Hive community that spans Autoglass, Carglass, and Safelite is the human counterpart to AP-09 — the architecture must support all opcos; so must the community.
- **Connection to Open Issues (OI-01, OI-03, OI-06):** Signals from community members who know their current platforms and pain points could close multiple open issues without formal workshops.
- **Community as early adopter pipeline:** Members who engage with the CCOTF community on Hive are natural pilot candidates for Phase 1 deployment (Core AI Contact).

### Strategic Implications

- Gives the CCOTF programme a visible home inside Belron — not just a PowerPoint in someone's inbox
- Creates a feedback loop between architecture decisions and operational reality
- Positions EA as a community builder, not just a document producer — relevant to the IPO governance narrative
- Could surface a previously unknown current-state platform (resolving OI-03) if opco members self-identify their stack
