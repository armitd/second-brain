## Strategic Intelligence

### Key Insights
1. **The knowledge base is becoming the agentic-AI substrate for CCOTF.** "RAG-ready" and "machine-readable knowledge document standards for agentic AI" are not KB housekeeping — they are the precondition for AI agents to answer reliably. This is the same lesson as the Ab Initio / Google Cloud piece processed today: *agents are only as good as the data and metadata behind them*. Defining KB document standards early is the high-leverage move.
2. **CCOTF scope spans three layers, not one.** Technology (KB reference architecture, Salesforce), experience (VOC, Qualtrics, Colin Shaw emotional arc), and people (Attensi/L&D enablement). Keeping all three visible avoids a purely-technical framing of the contact centre transformation.
3. **A machine-readable KB standard is a governance artefact, not just a content-ops one.** If knowledge docs feed agentic AI, their structure, provenance and freshness become part of the MCP Governance / agent-trust story — a natural bridge between the CCOTF and MCP Governance projects.

- **Recurring pattern:** knowledge quality / machine-readability keeps surfacing as the gating factor for agentic use — across CCOTF, MCP Governance, and the AIDA data-quality angle.

### Strategic Implications
- The KB reference architecture and a "RAG-ready / machine-readable" document standard should be defined early in CCOTF — they are prerequisites for any agentic contact-centre capability and a shared dependency with MCP Governance.
