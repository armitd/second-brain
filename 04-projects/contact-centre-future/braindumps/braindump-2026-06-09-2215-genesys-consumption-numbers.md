## Content Analysis

### Main Themes

1. **Consumption costs vs. licensing costs** — a critical distinction in CCaaS vendor evaluation. Licensing is the headline number; consumption covers API calls, AI feature usage, voice minutes, digital interactions, storage, and analytics that accumulate on top. The real cost of running Genesys is licensing + consumption combined.
2. **CCOTF vendor evaluation** — this data is needed to make a fair comparison between Genesys and the other CCaaS options (Salesforce Agentforce CC, Microsoft D365, Zoom, Vonage).

## Strategic Intelligence

### Key Insights

1. **Consumption costs are where CCaaS vendor comparisons often fall apart.** Vendors quote low per-seat licensing and bury AI feature costs, API call costs, and voice minute rates in consumption tiers. A full cost model needs: (a) licensing, (b) consumption at current volume, (c) consumption at projected scale.
2. **Genesys-specific consumption categories to capture:**
   - Voice minutes (inbound + outbound)
   - Digital channel interactions (chat, email, SMS, social)
   - AI feature usage — Agent Assist, Virtual Agent (bot sessions), speech analytics, WFM
   - Data storage and recording retention
   - API calls (if custom integrations)
   - Professional services / support tier
3. **The Genesys-Salesforce joint CX Cloud complicates this** — Belron may have consumption split across both platforms. The June 6 brief flagged that 200+ contact centres use the joint Genesys+Salesforce CX Cloud offering. If Belron is on this, consumption may be reported differently.

### Strategic Implications

- This data should be collated before any vendor demos — it shapes the questions to ask
