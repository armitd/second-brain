# Clothes, Clothes, Clothes. Music, Music, Music. Boys, Boys, Boys. — Viv Albertine

## What It's About
Memoir by Viv Albertine, guitarist of The Slits — one of the most important and overlooked bands in punk history. Covers her upbringing, the punk scene, life with Mick Jones, The Slits' chaotic career, and her life after music. Widely regarded as one of the best music memoirs ever written.

## Key Highlights
- Artists mentioned in her orbit: Mengelberg, Lol Coxhill, Fred Frith, Han Bennink — placing her firmly in the experimental/improvised music world beyond punk's boundaries

## Why It Matters
Personal interest — music memoir. Albertine writes with rare honesty about what it actually felt like to be making music in that era, not the mythology. Essential for anyone interested in the post-punk world.
