# Careless People — Sarah Wynn-Williams

## What It's About
A former Facebook global public policy director's memoir of the company's internal culture during its growth years — engagement-at-all-costs product decisions, weak internal guardrails, and leadership's relationship to global politics and government.

## Key Highlights
- Facebook worked with a beauty product company to track when 13-17 year old girls deleted selfies, in order to serve a beauty ad at that emotionally vulnerable moment.
- Despite public denials, Facebook was long aware its research and products optimized engagement at all costs — including "addictive by design" features specifically tailored to exploit vulnerabilities in young users, while concealing the harm.
- An internal memo ("Mapping a Vector Space in Motion") from CTO Andrew Bosworth reportedly urged senior management to move off pure engagement optimization, warning it was irresponsibly shaping behaviour — an acknowledgment from within of the platform's societal influence.
- References a "Track II Dialogue on Artificial Intelligence (AI) in National Security" and the development/application of AI-enabled military systems — Facebook/Meta's adjacency to AI-national-security policy conversations.

## Why It Matters
Meta (parent of Meta AI / LLaMA, tracked on the Competitive Watchlist) is the subject here.
