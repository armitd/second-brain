# DevOps Adoption Strategies

## What It's About
Practical guide to DevOps adoption, covering cultural anti-patterns, value stream mapping, and the shift from monolithic to microservice architectures.

## Key Highlights
- **Over-automation anti-pattern:** Organisations that over-automate focus on tools rather than process. Technology chosen for emotional reasons (what others use) rather than process fit. Results in mass confusion and too many tools requiring adjustment.
- **Tool/process order:** Your tooling should be adjusted for your processes — not the other way round.
- **Value stream mapping:** Three areas — information flow, product flow, and time ladder. Identifies waste and constraint points before introducing automation.
- The most common modernisation route: monolithic → microservice (domain-based, decoupled, reusable).

## Why It Matters
The "tool adjusted for process, not process for tool" principle applies directly to AI agent adoption at Belron — the risk of choosing MCP servers or AI tooling before the underlying processes are understood. Also: the value stream mapping discipline should precede AI automation decisions.
