# High Performance — Jake Humphrey & Damian Hughes

## What It's About
Draws on interviews with elite athletes, coaches, and business leaders to identify the habits and mindsets that separate high performers from everyone else. Based on the High Performance Podcast. Accessible and practical rather than academic.

## Key Highlights
- **You don't have to be a CEO to lead** — high performance is available at any level; it's about mindset not title
- **Seligman's Three Ps of learned helplessness:** Problems framed as *Pervasive* (affects everything), *Permanent* (will always be this way), and *Personal* (my fault) lead to worse life outcomes. High performers reframe as specific, temporary, and external
- Watch for vague language — "they", "everybody", "totally", "completely" — as a signal you're overgeneralising and falling into the pervasive trap
- **Radical responsibility:** High performers take absolute responsibility for their actions, focus on what they can control, and own up when they screw up
- When threatened, the brain shifts into reactive mode — recognising this is the first step to overriding it

## Why It Matters
Relevant to leadership development at EA level. The Three Ps framework is immediately applicable to how teams frame setbacks and why some organisations spiral after failures.
