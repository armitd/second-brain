# Empowered — Josh Bernoff and Ted Schadler

## What It's About
A Forrester-rooted argument that to serve empowered customers you must first empower your employees to solve their problems. (Note: this is the Bernoff/Schadler book, not Cagan's product-management "Empowered".) Only one highlight was captured, so this is a light entry.

## Key Highlights
- Four reinforcing technologies shifted power toward the individual (customer and employee alike) — the "new groundswell": **smart mobile devices** (connected experiences everywhere), **pervasive video**, **cloud computing** (any device can reach information + compute), and **social technology** (people draw power from peers). Taken together they push power toward the individual in both marketplace and workplace.
- The core move: *if you want to succeed with empowered customers, you must empower your employees to solve their problems.*

## Why It Matters
The employee-empowerment-serves-customer-empowerment thesis is a useful lens for [[04-projects/contact-centre-future/PROJECT-OVERVIEW|Contact Centre of the Future]]: it reframes agentic AI in the contact centre as *empowering agents to resolve customer problems faster*, not deflecting or replacing them — the same augmentation-over-automation framing running through the Canaries Dashboard note.
