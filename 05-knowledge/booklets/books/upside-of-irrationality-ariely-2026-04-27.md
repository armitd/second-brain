# The Upside of Irrationality — Dan Ariely

## What It's About
Follow-up to *Predictably Irrational*. Ariely explores how irrational behaviours can sometimes work in our favour — looking at procrastination, adaptation, the IKEA effect, revenge, and how emotions shape decisions. More personal and reflective in tone than its predecessor.

## Key Highlights
- We often avoid doing unpleasant things now (exercising, annoying projects) even when we know the long-term benefit — procrastination is a universal human flaw, not a personal failure
