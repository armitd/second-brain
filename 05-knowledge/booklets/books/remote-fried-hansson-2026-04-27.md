# Remote — Hansson and Fried

## What It's About
37signals' case for remote work, written before it was mainstream. Covers collaboration mechanics, trust-based management, async communication, and why managers who can't trust remote workers probably shouldn't have those workers at all.

## Key Highlights
- "Four hours of overlap to avoid collaboration delays and feel like a team" — the minimum viable synchrony threshold
- Permanent chat room for social cohesion (not work coordination) — primary function is connection, not efficiency
- "If the company is full of people whom nobody trusts to make decisions without layers of managerial review, then the company is full of the wrong people."
- Write well: clarity first, style second; remote work is written work

## Why It Matters
Relevant context for Belron's distributed 34-country operating model. The "four hours of overlap" threshold is a useful constraint for AI-assisted async workflows. The trust/empowerment framing connects to the AI mandate question — if AI is only used where managers feel comfortable, adoption will be low.
