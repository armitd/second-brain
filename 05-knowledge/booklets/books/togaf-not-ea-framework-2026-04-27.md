# TOGAF Is NOT an EA Framework — Smith, Kotusev, Graves et al.

## What It's About
A critique and reframing of TOGAF — arguing it is a methodology/ADM process, not an EA framework proper. Multiple authors examine what enterprise architecture actually is versus what TOGAF delivers.

## Key Highlights
- References Marlies Steenbergen and Martin Van Den Berg's "Building An Enterprise Architecture Practice" as a more practical EA resource
- The ADM (Architecture Development Method) is a process framework, not an EA framework — the distinction matters when scoping what EA should do vs. what TOGAF tells you to do
- Clausewitz: "The first task of any theory is to clarify terms and concepts that are confused" — the EA field has this problem; TOGAF conflates terms

## Why It Matters
Useful for framing what Belron's EA practice actually is versus what it uses TOGAF for. The Clausewitz quote about clarifying terms before solving problems maps directly to MCP Governance work — defining what "agent" means before governing them.
