# Flow — Goulding and Shaughnessy

## What It's About
Flow is an approach to organisational change that centres human interaction — the social interactions of the workday as the site of good decisions. WALLS (large-scale visualisations) replace cubicles and meetings as the focal point for collaborative work design.

## Key Highlights
- "Failure will count against [people] unless experimentation is an incentivised part of company culture."
- "You cannot plan and dictate collaboration, engagement, and enthusiasm. You can only frustrate people with those plans." — on why change-management programs planned with consultants fail
- "Real empowerment means employees take over the workplace in some profound way."
- Leaders set conditions for good social interaction; they harness group intelligence and direct it towards continuous improvement.
- WALLS: large-scale visualisations as common property, managed by employees, with leaders shaping conversations about value.

## Why It Matters
The "experimentation must be incentivised" point is the single most important thing for Belron's AI adoption programme. If AI experimentation is tolerated but failure counts against people in performance review, the programme will stall. Also: the contrast between planning change and enabling it is directly relevant to AI change management.
