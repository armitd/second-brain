# Beyond Belief — Nir Eyal & Julie Li

## What It's About
A framework for using beliefs strategically to sustain motivation. Rejects magical thinking in favour of evidence-open beliefs that are useful rather than merely comforting. Core argument: beliefs are tools, not truths — adopt them because they serve you, discard them when reality contradicts them.

## Key Highlights
- **Motivation triangle**: Behaviour (what to do) + Benefit (why do it) + Belief (conviction that actions will yield results). All three must be present; motivation fails when any one is absent.
- **Beliefs as tools**: "The real question isn't 'Is this belief true?' but 'Does this belief serve me?' The best beliefs are both practical and adjustable — enough conviction to act, enough flexibility to adapt."
- **Fact vs Faith vs Belief**: Fact = verifiable evidence. Faith = conviction without evidence. Belief = firmly held opinion, open to revision. Only beliefs are strategically useful.
- **Perception follows belief**: "We perceive the problems we look to find." If you believe your partner is criticising you, neutral comments transform into attacks.
- **Reality-testing**: A useful belief "holds up to real-world feedback, remains open to revision, and doesn't require ignoring evidence to sustain it."
