# The Other Side of Innovation — Govindarajan and Trimble

## What It's About
The innovation execution problem: organisations are built for efficiency, not innovation. Innovation requires a custom team with a custom model — borrowing from the Performance Engine but separating enough to experiment.

## Key Highlights
- "Business organizations are not built for innovation; they are built for efficiency."
- "The limits to innovation in large organizations have nothing to do with creativity and nothing to do with technology. They have everything to do with management capability."
- **Innovation = idea + leader + team + plan** — creativity is the least constrained resource; execution capability is the constraint
- Innovation and ongoing operations are always and inevitably in conflict — by definition; not a management failure
- Each innovation initiative requires a team with a custom organizational model and a plan revised through rigorous learning (not set in advance)

## Why It Matters
The "management capability, not creativity or technology" insight is directly applicable to Belron's AI adoption: the constraint isn't the models, it isn't the ideas, it's building management capability to run innovation alongside ongoing operations. Also useful for framing the AI CoP — building learning habits around the operational tension.
