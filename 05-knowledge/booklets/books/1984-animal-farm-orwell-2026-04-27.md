# 1984 & Animal Farm — George Orwell

## What It's About
Two of Orwell's most enduring works in one volume. *Animal Farm* (1945) is a political allegory of the Soviet revolution. *Nineteen Eighty-Four* (1949) is the definitive dystopian novel — surveillance, doublethink, and the mechanics of totalitarian control. Still the sharpest description of how propaganda works.

## Key Highlights
- **On keeping the proles passive:** "It was not desirable that the proles should have strong political feelings. All that was required of them was a primitive patriotism which could be appealed to whenever it was necessary to make them accept longer working-hours or shorter rations."
- **The gap between propaganda and reality:** The Party claimed perpetual improvement — more food, better houses, healthier lives — but "not a word of it could ever be proved or disproved." History becomes fantasy.
- **The ideal vs. the real:** The Party's vision was "something huge, terrible, and glittering — a world of steel and concrete"; the reality was "decaying, dingy cities where underfed people shuffled to and fro in leaky shoes"
- Statistics as a weapon: "It was like a single equation with two unknowns. It might very well be that literally every word in the history books, even the things that one accepted without question, was pure fantasy."

## Why It Matters
The highlighted passages focus on propaganda mechanics and the manipulation of information — more relevant now than when written. The gap between stated metrics and lived reality is a pattern worth tracking in any institutional context.
