# Ultra-Processed People — Chris van Tulleken

## What It's About
An examination of ultra-processed food (UPF) using the NOVA classification system. Van Tulleken's core argument: it is the ultra-processing, not the nutritional content, that makes these foods harmful.

## Key Highlights
- "It is the ultra-processing, not the nutritional content, that's the problem." — the NOVA insight reframes the diet debate
- UPF: "designed to create highly profitable (low-cost ingredients, long shelf life, emphatic branding) convenient hyperpalatable products liable to displace freshly prepared dishes"
- The main driver of global obesity increase since the 1980s is the increase in UPF production and consumption
- Insulin hypothesis (Taubes): carbs → insulin spike → fat storage → hunger cycle; van Tulleken presents this fairly but doesn't fully endorse
- "We don't really choose what to eat. We are guided by that internal system." — the homeostatic override mechanism
