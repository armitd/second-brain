# Indian Restaurant Curry at Home Volume 2 — Richard Sayce & Gary Crossley

## What It's About
Practical guide to recreating British Indian Restaurant (BIR) style cooking at home. Focuses on the techniques and ingredients that give restaurant curry its distinctive flavour — particularly the base gravy method — rather than traditional regional Indian cooking.

## Key Highlights
- **Base gravy is the foundation of BIR cooking:** A slow-cooked sauce of onions, carrot, and oil that forms the backbone of restaurant-style dishes. Makes enough for ~16 portions per batch. The recipe is the result of "considerable experimentation" — it's not so much what's in it but what you do with it that matters
- The Mark I base gravy is described as a "safe, all-round sauce" — a Mark II variant offers a more savoury alternative
- BIR cooking relies on technique (caramelisation, high heat) as much as ingredients — the smoky, caramelised flavour comes from cooking method not spice quantity

## Why It Matters
Cooking is a listed personal interest. The base gravy insight is the single most important technique for anyone trying to replicate BIR curry at home — worth putting into practice.
