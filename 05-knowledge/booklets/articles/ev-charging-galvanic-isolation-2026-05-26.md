# We're Charging Our Cars Wrong

## Summary
IEEE Spectrum piece by Wally Rippel and Alan Cocconi arguing that the cost and complexity of today's EV charging stations are themselves a brake on EV adoption. Their proposal: eliminate galvanic isolation in the charger to simplify and cheapen the hardware, on the basis that the cost/safety trade-off currently favours over-engineering. A more affordable, simpler infrastructure would accelerate adoption.

## Key Highlights
- The article's central technical claim is around eliminating **galvanic isolation** in the charging path as the cost-reduction lever.

## Why It Matters
Automotive industry adjacency — Belron's customers are auto owners, and the EV transition reshapes the vehicle mix (different glass, different ADAS profiles, different repair economics). Charging-infrastructure economics are a leading indicator of how fast EV penetration will accelerate, which in turn shapes the ADAS-calibration mix Belron is preparing for.
