# The March of Nines — Kevin Kelly

## Summary
Kelly's concept of "the march of nines" — improving quality by adding more 9s to reliability percentages (99% → 99.9% → 99.99%). Each additional nine is exponentially harder and more expensive than the last. Applied to self-driving cars: achieving human-level safety requires many more nines, will cost billions, and may not reach full autonomy without humans in the loop until 2036 or later.

## Key Highlights
- Each additional nine of reliability requires disproportionately more effort and cost than the last
- Self-driving autonomy requires far more nines of safety than human-level driving to be acceptable
- True autonomy (no human in the loop) may not arrive until 2036 or beyond
- The framework applies to any high-stakes automation context, not just autonomous vehicles

## Why It Matters
A useful mental model for the damage assessment PoC. "Good enough" for a first PoC (say, 90% accuracy on damage classification) is very different from "good enough for production claims decisions" (which might require 99.9%+). The march of nines framing helps set realistic expectations with stakeholders about what the PoC proves vs what production deployment requires.
