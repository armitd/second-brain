# Jevons Paradox — Wikipedia

## Summary
When technological progress increases efficiency in resource use, total consumption of that resource often increases rather than decreases — because lower cost drives higher demand. Originally observed with coal and steam engines; applies directly to AI productivity tools.

## Key Highlights
- Efficiency gains → lower cost per unit → demand increases → total consumption rises
- Counter-intuitive: making something cheaper and easier doesn't reduce its use, it expands it
- AI application: AI making knowledge work faster doesn't reduce the amount of knowledge work — it expands the scope of what gets attempted

## Why It Matters
Useful framing for EA conversations about AI and workforce. "AI will reduce headcount" is the Jevons-naive view. The Jevons-aware view: AI will make each person capable of more, expanding the scope of what teams attempt — which may increase rather than decrease total work volume.
