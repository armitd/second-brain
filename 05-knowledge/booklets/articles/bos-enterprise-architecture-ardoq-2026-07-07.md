# BOS' Enterprise Architecture Project: A Single Source of Truth

## Summary
An Ardoq case study on Bertel O. Steen (BOS), a 120-year-old Norwegian car retail/service group (~USD 2.23bn turnover), standing up its first Enterprise Architecture capability. With Accenture's methodology and Ardoq's Application Portfolio Management best-practice guide, BOS built an As-Is/To-Be single source of truth linking applications to business capabilities, replacing manual Excel/PowerPoint/Visio analysis. The trigger was the automotive industry's shift to a direct-sales model requiring more flexible, scalable, real-time systems.

## Key Highlights
- Automotive direct-sales shift was the strategic driver: legacy sales/after-sales systems had to become flexible, scalable, and real-time — a close parallel to Belron's own operating-model pressures.
- No prior EA team or tool: BOS partnered with Accenture (its existing digitalization partner), who brought an automotive capability model as a starting point, cutting time-to-value dramatically ("3 weeks vs. many more hours in Excel").
- CFO-sponsored: the CFO wanted to understand and control IT costs and see how IT creates business value — EA framed as a finance/value conversation, not just an IT one.
- Deliberately avoided architecture jargon ("business functionality" instead of "business capabilities") to drive business-side adoption; everyone gets read access.
- Expanded from APM into Business Capability Modeling, Application Integration Management, GDPR data processing, and Technical Capability Modeling; now maintains 60+ initiatives in the tool.

## Why It Matters
Two things. First, a directly analogous EA-adoption story from a car retail/service group navigating a direct-sales transition — useful reference material for framing Belron EA value to business and finance stakeholders (the CFO-sponsorship and jargon-avoidance angles especially). Second, **competitive intel**: this is an **Ardoq** case study, and Ardoq is a direct competitor to LeanIX (Belron's EA repository). Worth noting how Ardoq positions APM, capability modeling, and "single source of truth" against the SAP LeanIX story.
