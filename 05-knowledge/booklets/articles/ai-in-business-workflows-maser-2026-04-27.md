# What It Actually Takes to Incorporate AI Into Business Workflows

## Summary
Practical Inc. article on AI adoption in enterprise contexts. Three prerequisites: high-quality organised data, buy-in at all levels, and starting with small pilots. The core argument is that AI tools are only as effective as the humans who implement them — organisational and behavioural foundations matter more than the technology choice.

## Key Highlights
- "The AI conversation is no longer hypothetical — it's here, and it's finding its way into everything from records management to customer service queries"
- Unlocking full value demands "thoughtful collaboration between people and technology"
- Tools are only as effective as the humans who implement and interact with them
- "Putting people first, rather than platforms, positions your team, your data, and your business for long-term success"

## Why It Matters
Directly relevant to Belron AI adoption framing. The "people first, platforms second" framing is useful when presenting damage assessment PoC or CCOTF to stakeholders who are sceptical about AI deployment speed.
