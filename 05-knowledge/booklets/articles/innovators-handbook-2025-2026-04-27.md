# The Innovator's Handbook 2025

## Summary
A compiled handbook on innovation strategy for enterprise leaders, featuring contributions including Brian Solis (ServiceNow) and Gina (Innovation Council framework). Core thesis: AI is the dominant force in technological innovation and companies must choose to disrupt or be disrupted. Covers a three-stage innovation pipeline (Discovery → Incubation → Acceleration) and the role of an Innovation Council as governance mechanism.

## Key Highlights
- **Three-stage pipeline:** Discovery (identifying opportunities in 5–10 year domains) → Incubation (testing market potential and feasibility) → Acceleration (scaling validated ideas)
- **Innovation Council:** Senior leaders distinct from operational leadership, focused on long-term health over immediate business needs
- **Practical starting steps:** Define clear innovation language; start with orphan projects (neglected ideas with potential); build momentum with early wins; align around domains of innovation intent
- "It's not about the number of ideas but the quality and potential of opportunities within your portfolio"
- Emphasises persistence: treat innovation as a long-term commitment, not a one-off initiative

## Why It Matters
The Innovation Council model is a useful structural reference for how EA could position itself during Belron's transition period — a governance body focused on long-term strategic direction rather than operational execution. The "orphan projects" concept also resonates: the damage assessment PoC and CCOTF are both somewhat orphaned initiatives that need a home.
