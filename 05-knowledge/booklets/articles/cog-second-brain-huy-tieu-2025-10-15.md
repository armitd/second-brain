# I Finally Built a Second Brain That I Actually Use (6th Attempt)

## Quick Summary
The origin article for COG — the system you're currently using. Huy Tieu describes five failed PKM attempts (Notion, Obsidian with plugins, custom React app) and what finally worked: removing manual organisation entirely by letting Claude AI handle it. The insight is simple: the overhead of organising thoughts is what kills every PKM system.

## Key Insights
- **The core failure mode of PKM systems:** Manual organisation overhead overwhelms users despite strong initial enthusiasm. Every system fails not because of the tool, but because of the human tax of filing and tagging.
- **COG's solution:** Automate all organisation. `/braindump` captures unstructured thoughts, Claude classifies and cross-references automatically. Zero manual filing.
- **What works vs. what failed:** Started with 25 commands, reduced to 4 core ones. Perfectionist categorisation systems failed. Scheduled processing (daily/weekly/monthly) beat continuous automation.

## Why This Matters
This is the design rationale behind the system you use every day. Understanding the author's intent — especially the "reduce friction to zero" philosophy — helps you get more out of it. The key insight: COG isn't meant to be a perfect knowledge system, it's meant to be one you actually use.

**Results after 3 months:** 120+ braindumps, 5 strategic frameworks generated, zero manual organisation, $10–15/month via Claude API.
