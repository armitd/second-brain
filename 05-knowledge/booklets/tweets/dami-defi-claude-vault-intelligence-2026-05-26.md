# Thread: Claude as Intelligence Layer on Obsidian — 4-Layer Vault System

## Summary
Dami-Defi documents an 8-week experiment connecting Claude to an Obsidian vault as an active intelligence layer rather than a filing system. The core insight: organising notes by *type* (observation, reaction, pattern, question, number, reference) rather than *topic* allows Claude to surface cross-domain connections that topic-based filing hides. The system compounds over time — at 8 weeks Claude knows recurring unresolved questions and contradictions the user has forgotten.

## Key Points
- **The distinction that matters:** "The difference between an organised vault and an intelligent one is the difference between a library and a researcher." Organisation finds what you know exists; intelligence surfaces what you forgot and connects what you never linked.
- **Four layers:** (1) Capture via Readwise + Whisper transcription + Telegram bot — zero friction; (2) N8N as router — formats captures, organises inbox, triggers daily Claude synthesis at 6am; (3) Obsidian as memory — notes organised by *type* not *topic*; (4) Claude as cognitive partner — reasons across accumulated context, not individual queries.
- **CLAUDE.md is the critical file:** Teaches Claude your role, projects, thinking style, and what you want surfaced. Without specifics, outputs are generic. The template includes: Who I Am, What I'm Building, How This Vault Works (note types), My Thinking Style, What I Want From You, Hard Rules.
- **Daily synthesis prompt:** Passes last 7 days of captures to Claude at 6am. Returns 4 sections: Connections (non-obvious cross-note links), Patterns (theme across 3+ notes), Contradictions (conflicting saved positions, surfaced without resolution), Open Questions (recurring unresolved questions).
- **Compounding is real and measurable:** Week 2 — occasionally useful; Week 4 — genuinely surprising connections; Week 8 — has updated three significant positions because Claude surfaced direct contradictions between notes written weeks apart.

## Key Template: The CLAUDE.md Structure

```
# Vault Intelligence System
## Who I Am
My name is [name]. I work at the intersection of [domains].
My audience is [describe them specifically].
## What I Am Building
[One paragraph on current projects and goals]
## How This Vault Works
Notes organised by type not topic.
- Observations: things noticed, unpolished
- Reactions: honest gut response
- Patterns: same principle in two domains
- Questions: things genuinely not known
- Numbers: real data points with source
- References: saved content for future use
## My Thinking Style
[Reasoning approach, what connections excite you, recurring questions]
## What I Want From You
Surface connections I missed.
Find contradictions between notes weeks apart.
Flag questions that keep appearing without resolution.
Never summarise. Always synthesise.
## Hard Rules
Do not produce generic insight.
Every output must contain at least one connection traceable to a specific note.
```

## Why It Matters
The "type not topic" organisational principle and the CLAUDE.md specificity rules are directly applicable to how COG is structured. The contradictions section is the most practically useful feature — surfacing positions that have silently shifted without the user noticing.
