# Thread: Rory Sutherland on the Double-Income Household Trap

## Summary
Sutherland's observation that the shift to dual-income households began as an option but became an obligation. The primary beneficiaries were governments (double the tax base) and property owners (house prices rose to absorb two salaries). Families lost ~35 hours of discretionary leisure time per week with no corresponding rise in living standards.

## Key Points
- What begins as liberation can become a new form of constraint — the dual-income norm is a textbook example
- Research (American Time Use Survey, OECD) shows leisure time fell sharply for dual-earner families with no rise in subjective wellbeing
- The financial gain was largely captured by rising house prices and higher taxes, not retained by families
- "A reminder to look carefully at changes that society presents as inevitable progress"
