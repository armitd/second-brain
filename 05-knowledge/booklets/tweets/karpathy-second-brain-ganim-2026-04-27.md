# Thread: Karpathy's Second Brain Clearly Explained — Corey Ganim

## Summary
A practical how-to guide based on Karpathy's LLM Wiki pattern (see [[karpathy-llm-wiki-original-post-2026-04-27]]). Three-folder system: `raw/` (unorganised dump), `wiki/` (LLM-maintained), `outputs/` (generated answers). One schema file (CLAUDE.md/AGENTS.md) tells the AI what to organise and how.

## Key Points
- **Three folders only:** `raw/` (junk drawer — never organise manually), `wiki/` (AI writes and maintains), `outputs/` (AI-generated answers). No other structure needed
- **Schema file is everything:** CLAUDE.md at root tells the AI the topic, organisation rules, wiki standards. Without it, AI guesses. With it, every output is structured correctly
- **Fill raw/ without organising:** Copy-paste articles, export notes, paste meeting notes. AI's job to organise, not yours
- **Compounding loop:** Once wiki has 10+ articles, ask questions against it. Save answers back into the wiki. Every question makes the next answer better
- **Monthly health check:** Ask AI to find contradictions, missing explanations, unsourced claims, gap articles. Catches errors before they compound
- **You don't need Obsidian:** A folder of `.md` files and a good schema file outperforms a complex tool stack 90% of the time

## Why It Matters
Practical implementation guide for the same pattern COG is built on. Good reference for explaining the LLM Wiki approach to others.
