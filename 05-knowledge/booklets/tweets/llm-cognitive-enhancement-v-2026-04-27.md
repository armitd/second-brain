# Thread: Using LLMs for Cognitive Enhancement — Recursive Thought Spirals, Thread Maps, Meta Questions

## Summary
V (@internetvin) describes how voice-based LLM interaction bridges unstructured and structured thinking in real time. Uses MacWhisper to transcribe audio → paste into Claude → repeat. Develops three specific techniques: Recursive Thought Spirals, Thread Maps, and Meta Questions. Essay-style thread, more philosophical than tactical.

## Key Points

- **Voice as primary input:** Speaking aloud produces richer, more unstructured thoughts than typing. Using MacWhisper to transcribe → Claude to structure creates a real-time bridge between raw cognition and organised ideas.

- **Recursive Thought Spirals:** Rather than following Claude's questions, react to specific lines in Claude's response, record your reaction, send it back. Each structured response from Claude triggers new unstructured thoughts. The spiral deepens the exploration beyond what either human or AI could achieve alone.

- **Thread Maps:** When exploring a complex topic, ask Claude to create a thread map — a structured overview of all parallel lines of thinking that have emerged. Acts as a cognitive compass. Update it as understanding develops.

- **Meta Questions:** Ask Claude to reflect on your own thinking patterns ("What patterns do you notice in how I approach problems?"). Claude can observe and name your cognitive habits — useful for becoming more conscious of how you think.

- **Core insight:** LLMs don't just answer questions; they can model the structure of your thinking back at you, revealing patterns you wouldn't notice from inside your own head.

## Why It Matters
The voice → transcribe → Claude → react loop is directly relevant to how braindumps work in COG. The Thread Map technique is a useful prompt pattern for long, sprawling braindumps — could be added to the braindump skill as an option.
