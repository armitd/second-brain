# Thread: Karpathy Fireside Chat — Sequoia Ascent 2026

## Summary
Karpathy's April 2026 Sequoia keynote sets out three strategic themes: (1) LLMs enable genuinely new categories of product, not just speed-ups; (2) LLM "jaggedness" is explained by training data verifiability and economics — labs optimise for high-revenue domains, leaving others underdeveloped; (3) the agent-native economy decomposes products into sensors, actuators, and logic — human taste/judgement remains the ceiling, not the floor. The framing: "vibe coding raised the floor; agentic engineering raises the ceiling."

## Key Points
- **LLMs as new horizons, not just accelerators:** Examples — menugen (full LLM app, no classical code), .md skills replacing .sh scripts (LLM as interpreter of intent), LLM knowledge bases (computation over unstructured data, previously impossible)
- **Jaggedness explained:** LLM performance is uneven because (a) verifiability of a domain determines whether RL training can reinforce good outputs, and (b) economics — frontier labs train hard on high-TAM domains, leaving niche domains under-served. If your use case is "on the rails" of the training distribution, you fly; if off-road, you're in the jungle
- **Agent-native economy:** Products and services decompose into sensors, actuators, and logic (split across classical/neural computing). Making information "maximally legible to LLMs" is the key engineering challenge. Agentic engineering is a new skill set — different from both classical engineering and prompt engineering
- **Vibe coding raised the floor; agentic engineering raises the ceiling** — Karpathy says he's "never felt more behind as a programmer" despite founding the vibe coding wave

## Why It Matters
The jaggedness model is directly applicable to Belron's AI projects — AI Damage Assessment is a high-verifiability domain (damage is or isn't there; ADAS calibration is measurable) which means it sits "on the rails." Contact Centre language understanding is less verifiable and more susceptible to jaggedness. The sensor/actuator/logic decomposition is a useful architecture framing for MCP Governance — MCP servers are actuators; LLMs are logic; enterprise data is the sensor layer.
