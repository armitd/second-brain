# Thread: NotebookLM + Claude — 8-Prompt Research System

## Summary
The core argument: use **NotebookLM as the librarian** (ingest, index, retrieve) and **Claude as the analyst** (reason, synthesise, decide). NotebookLM handles the 50 PDFs; Claude handles the hard thinking. Eight prompts cover the full research lifecycle — from synthesis to bias-checking to weekly knowledge audit.

## Key Points

### The Division of Labour
- **NotebookLM:** Ingests sources, generates summaries, produces audio overviews, handles source retrieval
- **Claude:** Takes NotebookLM outputs and does what NotebookLM can't — reasons, finds contradictions, forms theses, makes decisions

### The 8 Prompts

1. **Research Synthesis** — Pass NotebookLM extracts to Claude; ask for the 3 buried insights, the collective argument across sources, contradictions to resolve, the blind spot, the 1 insight that changes your decision, and a 3-sentence stakeholder summary

2. **Defensible Thesis Builder** — "Build me a research thesis: core thesis in one sentence, 3 strongest evidence points for it, 2 against it (steelman), smart skeptic argument, confidence level (60/80/95%), data point needed to flip position, 3-paragraph executive summary"

3. **Mental Model Extractor** — From any book via NotebookLM: extract the 1 core mental model, the 3-5 step framework hidden inside, the contrarian belief at the heart, when the model works and when it doesn't

4. **Meeting Transcript to Decisions** — Pass transcript through NotebookLM, then ask Claude: the 3 decisions actually made, the decisions avoided, soft commitments people made without realising, action items with owners and deadlines, unresolved tensions

5. **Audio Overview → Action** — After a NotebookLM audio overview, ask Claude to surface the single most actionable insight, a specific experiment this week, follow-up deep-dive question, publishable content hook, and the mental model to remember forever

6. **30-Day Expertise Sprint** — NotebookLM as textbook, Claude as tutor: 10 foundational documents to ingest, 5 leading thinkers to study, weekly reading + synthesis schedule, Claude prompts to run after each session, weekly knowledge test

7. **Source Bias and BS Detector** — Author's likely bias (institutional, ideological, financial), strongest/weakest claim, convenient omissions, methodology issues, 3 triangulation sources, confidence rating 1-10

8. **Weekly Knowledge Audit** — Sources ingested, hours spent, decisions supported, insights to remember, content not worth the time, where you're consuming instead of thinking, "knowledge debt" topics you keep avoiding

## Why It Matters
Directly applicable to the research workflow supporting Belron EA projects — especially for processing the growing number of research reports, whitepapers, and strategic analyses that flow in. The braindump → auto-research → NotebookLM pattern is worth formalising.
