# Thread: Why I Don't Use Scrum in Remote Teams — Async 5-Question Framework

## Summary
Sergio Pereira argues that Scrum adds ~8 hours of meetings per sprint with minimal value in remote teams. His alternative: a 5-question async framework (Why / What / How / Who / When) written in a shared doc before work starts. Most Big Tech companies quietly use Plan → Build → Ship rather than Scrum ceremonies.

## Key Points
- **Scrum overhead:** ~8hrs of meetings per sprint (planning, standup, review, retro) that often don't produce proportional value
- **5-question async framework:** Why (problem), What (solution), How (approach), Who (owners), When (milestones) — written async in a collaborative doc
- **Big Tech reality:** Plan → Build → Ship is the dominant model at scale, not ceremony-heavy Scrum
- **Remote context:** Synchronous ceremonies designed for co-located teams don't translate well to distributed work

## Why It Matters
Useful reference for thinking about how Belron teams structure sprints and whether ceremony overhead is justified. The async doc approach maps well to distributed enterprise contexts.
