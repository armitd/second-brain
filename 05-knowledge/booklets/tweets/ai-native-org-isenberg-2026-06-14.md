# Thread: What Does It Actually Mean to Be AI Native?

**Author:** Greg Isenberg (@gregisenberg) — Jun 2026

## Summary
A 9-point framework defining what "AI native" actually means for organisations. The core argument: AI native is not about using ChatGPT, it's about restructuring the organisation so agents can execute while humans handle strategy and taste. The moat is the system, not the tools.

## Key Points
1. **3-layer org**: People for strategy and taste → agents for execution → shared context layer that makes the company readable to agents
2. **AI eats the middle**: You used to spend 80% of your day on execution. Now agents do that. Your job is the bookends: what to do and judging quality
3. **Everyone is a manager now**: Your output is your agents' output. If they produce garbage, you set them up wrong
4. **ChatGPT ≠ AI native**: "That's like having a website and calling yourself a tech company"
5. **People first, tools second**: Companies that skip to tools without changing how people work — that's why it fails
6. **Making your company "readable" to agents is the real work**: Every process, decision, piece of knowledge must exist in a format an agent can consume. Most companies are nowhere close
7. **Speed without signal = expensive chaos**: The system must move fast AND know if it's moving in the right direction
8. **Skill chain compounds**: Skills build on skills. The more you invest in them, the more your company compounds
9. **The moat is the system**: Your competitor can copy your tools. They can't copy your system (agents + rich context + compounding weekly)

## Why It Matters
This is the clearest articulation of what Belron's AI journey is actually aiming for — not tool adoption but systemic transformation. Point 6 ("making the company readable to agents") directly maps to what COG's second brain architecture does at the personal level and what an enterprise knowledge graph would do at the org level.

Point 9 (moat = system) is a compelling framing for the IPO narrative: Belron's AI advantage isn't individual tools, it's the compounding system being built around them.
