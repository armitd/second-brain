# Thread: NotebookLM Mind Maps — Auto-Generated, Every Node Is a Live Query

## Summary
Steven Johnson (author of "Where Good Ideas Come From") describes why NotebookLM's auto-generated mind maps converted him from a long-time mind map sceptic. Two problems with traditional mind maps: laborious to build, and once built they're passive — visualising is not the same as exploring. NotebookLM solves both: maps are generated automatically from your sources, and every node launches a full NotebookLM query with one click.

## Key Points
- **Auto-generated from sources:** no manual linking or tagging. Upload sources → mind map of core concepts appears
- **Every node = live query:** clicking a node launches a full NotebookLM question about that concept, with citations back to source material. Visualisation + drill-down in one step
- **Recommended use:** upload the last 20 documents you've written or read → generate a map to see "where your head has been" — an involuntary overview of your recent thinking
- **Source:** NotebookLM product team feature announcement

## Why It Matters
The "upload last 20 docs → see a map of where your head has been" use case is a useful periodic review tool. Could complement COG's weekly check-in or braindump review — a visual layer over the knowledge base rather than purely text.
