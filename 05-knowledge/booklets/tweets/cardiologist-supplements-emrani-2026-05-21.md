# Thread: Cardiologist's 7 Daily Supplements

## Summary
Afshine Emrani MD (cardiologist, FACC) shares 7 supplements he takes daily — backed by clinical practice rather than marketing. Filed for personal health reference.

## The 7 Supplements

| Supplement | Dose & Timing | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Creatine** | 5g in water, morning | Energy production for all cells including heart muscle; muscle strength with age; cognitive benefits |
| **Glycine** | 5g before bed | Lowers core body temperature → better sleep onset and duration; rebuilds collagen; supports liver and gut |
| **Magnesium glycinate** | 400–500mg before bed | 75% of people are deficient; addresses stress, night cramps, poor sleep, elevated blood pressure |
| **Vitamin D3 + K2** | Together (dose not specified) | D3 alone can deposit calcium in arteries; K2 redirects it to bones — they must be taken together |
| **CoQ10 (ubiquinol form)** | 100–300mg with breakfast | Essential for heart muscle energy (100,000 beats/day); depleted by statins and ageing |
| **Omega-3 fish oil** | 1,000–2,000mg EPA+DHA | Must carry NSF or USP certification — most shelf products are rancid |

## Key Cautions
- D3 without K2 can calcify arteries — always take together
- Most fish oil on pharmacy shelves is rancid — check for NSF/USP certification
- Magnesium glycinate specifically (not oxide); the ubiquinol form of CoQ10 specifically
