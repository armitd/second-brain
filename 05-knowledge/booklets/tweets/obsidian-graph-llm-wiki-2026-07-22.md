# Thread: The Obsidian graph as a self-maintaining AI wiki

## Summary
Frames the Obsidian graph not as a decorative note map but as a self-maintaining AI wiki built on Karpathy's "LLM wiki" pattern: the model reads each new source, extracts claims, updates old pages, creates entity files, links concepts, and flags where new info breaks older notes. With Claude Code + Obsidian, a single clipped article can expand into 8–20 markdown files (source summary, concept pages, people, tools, comparisons, open questions, running index).

## Key Points
- Most people use a chatbot as a disposable memory slot — one PDF, three questions, close the tab, context gone by tomorrow.
- Karpathy's LLM-wiki pattern turns saved sources into a growing linked archive instead of a graveyard of bookmarks.
- After ~60 sources it stops being "note-taking" and becomes a private research machine that remembers your old thinking and gives cited answers from your own archive.

## Why It Matters
This is the exact pattern COG's `CLAUDE.md` already names as its raw-sources convention ("Karpathy's LLM Wiki pattern"). Useful as external validation of the vault's design and as a **Hive**-shareable articulation of why a linked second brain beats a chat tab.
