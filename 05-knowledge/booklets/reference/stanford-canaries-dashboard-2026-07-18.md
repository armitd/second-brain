# Canaries Dashboard: AI Economic Indicators

## Quick Summary
The Stanford Digital Economy Lab (with ADP Research) tracks real-time employment trends across occupations by their exposure to generative AI, using ADP payroll data covering ~4.6 million workers. It is an early-warning system ("canaries in the coal mine") for which parts of the labour market are being reshaped by AI adoption since ChatGPT's November 2022 launch. Updated monthly.

## Key Insights
- **Early-career workers take the first hit:** ages 22–25 in high-AI-exposure roles (software development, customer service) show noticeable employment declines, while less-exposed roles (e.g. home health aides) grow. The effect persists and deepens for the youngest cohort.
- **Automation vs augmentation is the fault line:** occupations where AI *automates* the work show employment declines or muted increases; occupations where AI *augments* the worker show weaker/negligible employment correlation. This distinction, not exposure alone, predicts impact.
- **The aggregate signal is still modest but diverging:** overall employment differences across exposure groups remain small, but the gap widens as exposure rises — consistent with an early-stage effect concentrated at the entry level rather than a broad displacement (yet).

## Content Highlights
- Data spine: a 5-year balanced panel of ~25,000 firms on ADP payroll, over 730 occupations, matched via the BLS SOC Crosswalk; analysis weighted by occupation rather than raw employment counts.
- Finding: "employment growth is slowest in the most AI-exposed occupations," though differences across exposure groups remain modest in aggregate.
- Finding: automation-focused AI usage correlates with employment declines or "more muted increases"; augmentation-focused usage shows weaker employment correlations.
