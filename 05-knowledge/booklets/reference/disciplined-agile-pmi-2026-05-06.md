# Disciplined Agile (DA) — PMI Toolkit

## Quick Summary

Disciplined Agile (DA) is PMI's decision-making toolkit and knowledge base for enterprise agility. Unlike prescriptive frameworks (SAFe, Scrum), DA does not tell you *what* process to follow — it helps organisations **choose their way of working (WoW)** based on context. It is a hybrid toolkit that draws on hundreds of agile, lean, and traditional practices and asks teams to select the combination that fits their situation.

## Key Insights

- **"Choose Your WoW" is the central idea:** DA explicitly rejects one-size-fits-all methodology. Rather than mandating a single lifecycle or ceremony set, it provides a decision framework for picking practices based on team size, context, risk, and organisational maturity. This is fundamentally different from SAFe (prescriptive, top-down) or Scrum (team-level, limited scope).

- **Four-layer architecture:** Foundation (mindset, principles, choosing WoW) → Disciplined DevOps (DevOps + security + data management) → Disciplined Agile Delivery (solution delivery) → Disciplined Agile Enterprise (organisational agility, sense-and-respond capability). The layers build on each other — teams start at the Foundation and progress.

- **The DA Mindset is explicit:** Eight core agile principles + seven promises + eight guidelines. All three layers work together: principles say *why*, promises say *how we will behave*, guidelines give practical application. This is more structured than "agile values and principles" but less rigid than SAFe's frameworks.

## DA vs SAFe vs Scrum

| | Disciplined Agile (DA) | SAFe | Scrum |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Approach** | Bottom-up, context-sensitive toolkit | Top-down, prescriptive framework | Team-level, iterative process |
| **Best for** | Organisations wanting to evolve/customise their agile approach | Large organisations needing structure and governance alignment | Small, self-organising teams |
| **Flexibility** | High — teams choose from practice options | Low — structure is defined | Medium — team-level freedom, no enterprise guidance |
| **Governance** | Context-driven | Standardised and centralised | Minimal |
| **Scope** | Individual teams → whole enterprise | Enterprise-wide | Team-level |

## Toolkit Architecture

### Four Views
1. **Mindset** — Principles, promises, guidelines, philosophies
2. **People** — Roles, responsibilities, team and org structures
3. **Flow** — Process lifecycle, how work moves end-to-end
4. **Practices** — Specific techniques drawn from agile/lean/traditional sources

### Four Layers
1. **Foundation** — Conceptual underpinnings: mindset, lean/agile fundamentals, choosing WoW
2. **Disciplined DevOps** — DevOps extended with Security and Data Management for enterprise-class delivery
3. **Disciplined Agile Delivery** — End-to-end solution delivery guidance
4. **Disciplined Agile Enterprise (DAE)** — Organisational agility: culture, structure, sense-and-respond capability

## Why This Matters

For an Enterprise Architect, DA's "Choose Your WoW" framing is directly applicable to contexts where one-size-fits-all governance fails. The same philosophy applies to MCP governance (choose the right policy surface for the context, not the maximum possible control) and to advising delivery teams on which agile practices actually suit their project type. The DA Enterprise layer is relevant when thinking about how Belron scales AI delivery capability across business units — DA offers a framework for that conversation that doesn't default to "mandate SAFe."

The contrast with SAFe is worth understanding: Belron may already use SAFe elements, and knowing where DA offers a lighter-touch alternative is useful for EA advisory conversations.
