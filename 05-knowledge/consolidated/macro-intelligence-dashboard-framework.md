# Macro Intelligence Dashboard Framework

## Framework Overview
A lightweight, time-efficient system for reading global macro conditions using publicly available market signals — anchored to S&P 500 ratios as the primary data source, with a leading indicator layer (VIX) and a structural stress layer (DXY, Gold, Oil). Designed for a professional who needs a 10-minute monthly read on world order conditions, not a full macro analyst capability.

## The Core Insight

**S&P 500 in isolation is a narrow signal. S&P 500 as a ratio is a world order tool.**

The index number tells you US corporate health. The ratio — S&P vs CSI 300, vs MSCI EM, vs EuroStoxx — tells you about the balance of power between the US-led system and its alternatives. Sector rotation within the S&P tells you what smart money is pricing in geopolitically before it hits headlines.

## Core Principles

### Principle 1: VIX Is the Only Leading Indicator — Check It First

**Statement:** VIX is the only forward-looking signal in the macro dashboard. Every other signal is lagging or concurrent. VIX spikes before or as geopolitical and macro shocks land — making it the most time-sensitive and actionable signal to watch.

**VIX threshold table:**

| VIX Level | What It Means | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **< 15** | Complacent — markets pricing in calm | Good IPO/investment conditions |
| **15–25** | Normal — watch direction of travel | No action; monitor trend |
| **> 25** | Stress — smart money pricing in risk | IPO window under pressure; review timing |
| **> 35** | Crisis — risk-off mode | Window effectively closed |

**For the Belron IPO window (H2 2026):** VIX is the single most actionable number. A sustained move above 25 in Q3 2026 shifts the IPO timing calculus. Check weekly; treat movement toward 25 as an early warning.

**Confidence:** High — VIX as leading indicator is well-established in market analysis; Belron IPO connection is interpretive but grounded in the H2 2026 target

---

### Principle 2: S&P Ratios Reveal the World Order Shift

**Statement:** Comparing the S&P against other major indices shows the relative performance of the US-led global order vs. alternatives. No single comparison is definitive — together they form a direction-of-travel picture.

**The three ratios (check monthly):**

| Comparison | What It Tells You |
|---|---|
| **S&P 500 vs MSCI EM** | US corporate system vs emerging markets — West-led order outperforming, or multipolar world catching up? |
| **S&P 500 vs CSI 300 (China)** | US-China power balance as measured by investor capital allocation — the single highest-signal comparison for the AI/tech race |
| **S&P 500 vs EuroStoxx 50** | US vs European economic trajectory — divergence signals geopolitical or economic fracture |

**Confidence:** High — comparative index analysis is standard macro methodology

---

### Principle 3: Structural Stress Signals Confirm or Challenge the Ratio Story

**Statement:** The structural stress signals (DXY, Gold, Oil) provide context for why ratios are moving in a particular direction. They're slower-moving than VIX and don't need weekly monitoring — monthly is sufficient.

| Signal | What It Tells You |
|---|---|
| **DXY (Dollar Index)** | Reserve currency health. Rising = capital fleeing to USD; still dollar-dominant. Falling = risk-on OR dollar system being challenged. |
| **Gold / USD ratio** | Trust in the fiat system. Gold rising vs USD = inflation hedge OR confidence loss in dollar system. |
| **Oil (Brent Crude)** | Energy geopolitics. Sustained high = commodity powers (Russia, Saudi, Iran) have leverage. Low = US/Western supply dominance. |

**Confidence:** High — DXY, Gold, Oil as macro stress signals is standard practice

---

### Principle 4: Sector Rotation Is the Geopolitical Early Warning System

**Statement:** Which sectors are outperforming within the S&P tells you what smart money is pricing in geopolitically — often before news coverage reflects it. Defence stocks rising inside a flat market is more informative than the S&P level itself.

| Sector Signal | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Defence ETFs (ITA, XAR) outperforming** | Geopolitical tension premium — conflict risk being priced in |
| **Semiconductors (NVDA, AMD, SOXX) outperforming** | AI/tech race intensifying — capital flowing to tech supremacy |
| **Energy (XOM, CVX) outperforming** | Commodity power shifting; supply chain or geopolitical pressure |
| **Financials outperforming** | Dollar system healthy; credit and capital flowing normally |
| **Gold miners (GDX) outperforming** | Dollar system under stress; hedging behaviour |

**The AI race signal:** NVDA is ~6% of the S&P. Its performance IS the US AI supremacy signal. If NVDA falls while CSI 300 tech rises, that is a world order signal worth paying attention to.

**Confidence:** High — sector rotation as macro signal is well-established; AI race = semiconductor connection is specific to current market context (high confidence in current relevance, medium confidence on duration)

## Applications

### Application 1: Monthly 10-Minute World Order Check

**Cadence:** Monthly (first Sunday of the month)
**Tool:** TradingView free plan — build a multi-chart watchlist

**Sequence:**
1. Check VIX: threshold position?
2. Check S&P vs CSI 300: who gained this month?
3. Check S&P vs MSCI EM: emerging market convergence or divergence?
4. Check DXY: dollar direction?
5. Spot-check sector rotation: defence, semiconductors, gold miners vs broad market

**Time:** 10 minutes maximum

## Boundaries & Limitations

**This framework works when:**
- You need a quick directional read, not a precise forecast
- You're monitoring world order trends, not trading
- You're using it to frame strategic context for professional decisions

**This framework does NOT work when:**
- You need precise economic forecasting
- You're making investment decisions (this is not investment advice)
- You're trying to predict short-term market movements

**What S&P misses entirely:**
- China's domestic trajectory (CSI 300 partially compensates but is opaque)
- EU political cohesion (EuroStoxx is an economic proxy, not a political one)
- Commodity power shifts (underrepresented in S&P)
- BRICS alternative reserve currency development (invisible in market signals)
