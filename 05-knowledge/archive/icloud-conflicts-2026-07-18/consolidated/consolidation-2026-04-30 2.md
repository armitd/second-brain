### Theme 1: EA Must Reposition Before Agents Make Its Current Value Redundant
**Frequency:** 3 professional braindumps; referenced in 5+ daily briefs

**Core argument (three sources, independently):**
- **Byttow:** "AI absorbed the process layer." Coordination overhead is being automated. What remains is judgment. EA functions whose value is process-based will be made redundant and visible by AI — in the same way AI makes goal-clarity gaps visible.
- **Shopify memo (Tobi Lutke):** AI usage is now a baseline expectation, not optional. Before asking for headcount or a new process, demonstrate why AI-first doesn't work. The organisation that sets this standard within Belron is the one that shapes the transition.
- **Agarwal:** "The dividing line isn't generational — it's dispositional." Builder's disposition predicts adaptability. 10x output gap confirmed. Constraints are dissolving for those with clarity.

**Framework implications:** EA Effectiveness Principle 5 added. Professional Transition Principle 5 + Use Case 4 added.

### Theme 3: Governance as Intent-Setting — Applied to Both EA and MCP
**Frequency:** 2 braindumps; 3 daily briefs

**The unifying framing:**
Byttow's argument about process vs. judgment applies directly to governance. Governance that operates as friction (review gates, standards compliance) is exactly the kind of process layer being absorbed. Governance as intent-setting (defining what goals agents are authorised to pursue, with what tools, under what constraints) is durable.

**Applied:**
- To EA Effectiveness: Principle 5 repositions EA's governance role from friction to intent-setting
- To MCP Governance: Principle 5 added — the IFTTT MCP blast-radius case study is the concrete example

**Pattern:** The same repositioning argument applies across three frameworks simultaneously. This is a cross-cutting principle, not a domain-specific one.
