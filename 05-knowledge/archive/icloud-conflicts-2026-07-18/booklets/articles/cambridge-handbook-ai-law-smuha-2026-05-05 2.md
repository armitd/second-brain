# The Cambridge Handbook of the Law, Ethics and Policy of Artificial Intelligence

## Summary
An academic handbook covering the ethical, legal, and policy dimensions of AI — with particular focus on responsibility, regulation, and the EU AI Act. The EU AI Act's high-risk AI provisions come into force in 2026, making this a timely reference for enterprise AI governance. The handbook interrogates whether current regulatory frameworks will genuinely protect people or merely create a compliance appearance.

## Key Highlights
- The EU AI Act targets high-risk AI systems with mandatory requirements starting 2026 — enterprises deploying AI in regulated domains (health, safety, employment, critical infrastructure) face legal obligations, not just best-practice guidance
- Responsibility attribution in AI systems remains legally contested — who is liable when an autonomous system causes harm is an open question the handbook addresses at length
- The handbook frames AI ethics not as a checklist but as ongoing governance practice — relevant for MCP Governance framing
- AI policy is fragmenting across jurisdictions: EU (risk-based regulation), US (sector-specific), China (algorithm registry model) — enterprise deployments spanning geographies face multiple overlapping frameworks

## Why It Matters
Directly relevant to MCP Governance: as Belron deploys AI agents (CCOTF, AI Damage Assessment) the EU AI Act's 2026 provisions apply. Understanding the legal responsibility framework for high-risk AI is the governance foundation, not an optional extra. This handbook provides the academic rigour behind what the regulation requires.
