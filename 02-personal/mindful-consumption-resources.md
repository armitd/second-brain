# Resources: Enjoying This One, Not Chasing the Next

Curated for the specific issue: **always onto the next one** (coffee, beer, etc.), and the anticipation stretching into *obtaining* it ("is there a queue?", "is the bar busy?"). Ordered from "understand the mechanism" → "the expert on this exact thing" → "the savouring half" → "guided/practical".

---

## 1. Understand the mechanism (why this happens)

Your pattern is a known one in neuroscience: **wanting ≠ liking** (Kent Berridge's research). Dopamine drives *wanting* (anticipation), and the surge is strongest in the *approach* phase — before you have the thing. Once it arrives, the system quiets and the actual *liking* is real but shorter and milder than the craving that preceded it. So the brain is literally built to chase the next thing more than to enjoy the current one. Naming this is itself a wedge.

- **"You can want things you don't like…"** — Psyche (clear, credible explainer): https://psyche.co/ideas/you-can-want-things-you-dont-like-and-like-things-you-dont-want
- **"Why Do We Want Things We Don't Like?"** — Psychology Today: https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/everyday-neuroscience/202503/why-do-we-want-things-we-dont-like
- **"Anticipation Feels Better Than the Reward"** — short piece on desire: https://www.coolthoughts.in/anticipation-feels-better-than-the-reward-a-look-at-human-desire

## 2. The expert on your exact issue — Dr Judson (Jud) Brewer

Psychiatrist at Brown, specialises in **craving as a habit loop** (trigger → behaviour → reward) and how mindfulness creates a "wedge of awareness" that breaks it. This is the single closest match to "always wanting the next one" — and his work on *anxiety/worry as a habit* maps directly onto your "is there a queue?" planning reflex.

- **Book — *The Craving Mind*** (why we get hooked, from cigarettes to smartphones to love): https://drjud.com/read-the-craving-mind-book/
- **Book — *Unwinding Anxiety*** — treats worry/planning-ahead as a habit loop; this is the one for the *obtaining/logistics* half of your pattern
- **"How Mindfulness Works to Break Bad Habits"** — Mindful.org overview of his framework: https://www.mindful.org/how-mindfulness-works-break-bad-habits/
- **"Is Anxiety a Habit?"** — interview, on worry as anticipatory habit: https://www.1440.org/blog/is-anxiety-a-habit-talking-with-dr-judson-brewer
- **Apps:** *Unwinding Anxiety* and *Eat Right Now* (his craving/mindful-eating programmes) — see drjud.com
- **Technique to search for by name: "urge surfing"** — riding the wave of a craving without acting on it, watching it rise and pass. Purpose-built for "I want the next one."

## 3. The savouring half (enjoying *this* one)

Attention gets you present; **savouring** gets you to actually enjoy it. Two well-evidenced sources:

- **Rick Hanson — "Taking in the Good" / *Hardwiring Happiness*** — deliberately stay with a good experience for 5, 10, 20 seconds so it lands. Perfect for the first-sip practice.
  - Practice: https://greatergood.berkeley.edu/article/item/taking_in_the_good
  - Book: https://rickhanson.com/writings/books/hardwiring-happiness/
- **Fred Bryant — *Savoring: A New Model of Positive Experience*** — the foundational academic work on savouring: https://www.routledge.com/Savoring-A-New-Model-of-Positive-Experience/Bryant-Veroff/p/book/9780805851205
- **"10 Steps to Savoring the Good Things in Life"** — Greater Good, Berkeley (free, practical): https://greatergood.berkeley.edu/article/item/10_steps_to_savoring_the_good_things_in_life

## 4. Guided & practical — for consumption specifically

- **Coffee meditation** — a practical script (engage the senses, first sip full attention). Note: many of these live on coffee-brand blogs, so they're practical rather than authoritative, but the method is sound. Mindful.org is the more credible home: https://www.mindful.org
- **Ten Percent Happier** (app) — skeptic-friendly, no woo; has a **Mindful Eating** course that adapts directly to drinking. Good fit if spiritual language puts you off.
- **Insight Timer** (app) — huge free library (200k+ sessions). Search **"urge surfing"**, **"mindful eating"**, **"savouring"**, **"RAIN"** to find guided versions of the exact techniques above: https://insighttimer.com
- **Waking Up** (Sam Harris) — more philosophical, strong on present-moment/"there is only now"; good for the "arrive and see" mindset.
- **App roundup** (to compare) — Zapier's best meditation apps: https://zapier.com/blog/best-meditation-app/

## Techniques to search for by name (across any app)
- **Urge surfing** — for "I want the next one"
- **Mindful eating / drinking** (the raisin exercise, adapted to coffee/beer) — for savouring this one
- **RAIN** (Recognise, Allow, Investigate, Nurture) — for meeting a craving
- **Taking in the good** (Hanson) — the savouring rep
- **Noting / labelling "planning"** — for the "is there a queue?" reflex

---

## My pick if you only try three
1. **Read/skim *The Craving Mind* (or the Mindful.org overview)** — understand the loop; it reframes the whole thing.
2. **Learn "urge surfing"** (free guided version on Insight Timer) — the direct tool for chasing the next one.
3. **Do Hanson's "taking in the good" on your first coffee each day** — 20 seconds of deliberate savouring; the enjoy-this-one rep.

*Sources are web-found and cited above. The neuroscience and the Brewer/Hanson/Bryant work are credible; the coffee-blog scripts are practical but commercial. Not clinical advice — if the "can't stop at one" ever feels compulsive, worth a word with a GP.*
