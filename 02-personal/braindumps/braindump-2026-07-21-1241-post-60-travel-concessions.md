# Braindump: Post-60 Travel to the Office — Senior Concessions Setup

## Raw Thoughts
> Preserved as captured.

**Post 60 travel to the Office.**

### Senior Travel Concessions – Setup Checklist (London resident, occasional Egham travel)

**What to get**
1. **60+ London Oyster photocard** — for London-only travel (most days)
2. **Senior Railcard** (£30/1yr or £70/3yr) — for Egham travel days
3. **A physical Oyster card** to link the Senior Railcard to (separate from your 60+ Oyster; discount does NOT work on contactless/Apple Pay/Google Pay) — for pure in-London journeys only
4. **South Western Railway (SWR) app** — for Egham journeys specifically (Egham is outside the Oyster zone)

**How to use them**
- **London-only days:** tap in/out with the 60+ Oyster photocard — free travel on Tube, buses, DLR, trams, most National Rail within London.
- **Egham days (1–2x/week):** DO NOT just tap in/out (Oyster or contactless). Egham station is **outside the Oyster PAYG zone**, and plain contactless tap-in/out there does **not** automatically apply your Senior Railcard discount — you'd be charged full fare. Instead, **buy an Off-Peak ticket with your Senior Railcard applied** via the SWR app (or a station ticket machine/office) before you travel, to get the proper 1/3 discount.

**Setup steps (do once)**
1. Apply for the 60+ Oyster photocard via the TfL website (from age 60, or up to 2 weeks before your 60th birthday).
2. Buy a Senior Railcard online (from age 60, or up to 14 days before your 60th birthday).
3. Get/use a separate physical Oyster card for PAYG (London-only journeys).
4. Take the Senior Railcard + that physical Oyster card **in person** to any Tube, Elizabeth line, or London Overground station.
5. Ask staff to **link the Senior Railcard discount** to the Oyster card at a ticket machine — this cannot be done online.
6. Discount lasts until the Railcard expires; re-link each time you renew.
7. **Download the SWR app** (App Store / Google Play).
8. **Sign up for an SWR account** in the app.
9. **Add your Senior Railcard details** to your SWR app account once so it applies automatically to future ticket purchases.
10. For each Egham trip: buy your Off-Peak ticket in the app before travelling (railcard discount applied automatically).

**Key rules to remember**
- **No discount during weekday morning peak** (approx. 06:30–09:30) for Network Railcard area journeys (covers the London-zone leg towards Egham).
- Fully discounted at all other times, including all day weekends and bank holidays.
- **Egham-specific**: always buy a ticket in advance (app, machine, or ticket office) with the Railcard applied — never rely on tapping in/out for this leg.

**Bonus (optional)**
- **National Express Senior Coachcard** (£15 one-off): 1/3 off standard/flexible coach fares — leisure day trips, not the commute.

**When State Pension age is reached**
- 60+ Oyster automatically upgrades to the **Freedom Pass** (free travel across London + free local bus travel across England).

## Content Analysis

### Main Themes
1. **Two travel patterns, two tools:** London-only (most days) = 60+ Oyster photocard, free. Egham (1–2x/week) = Senior Railcard via the SWR app. Keeping them separate in your head is the whole trick.
2. **The Egham discount trap:** the single most important, easily-missed point — Egham is outside the Oyster zone, and tapping contactless/Oyster there charges **full fare** with no railcard discount. You must buy the ticket in advance with the railcard applied.
3. **Timing tied to your 60th:** both the 60+ Oyster and the Senior Railcard can be set up ~2 weeks before your birthday, so this is a "do just before 60" admin job.

### The one thing not to get wrong
> **On Egham days, buy an Off-Peak ticket in the SWR app with the Senior Railcard applied — never just tap in/out.** Tapping = full fare. Everything else here is convenience; this one is money.

### Questions Raised
- When exactly is your 60th? The application windows (2 weeks / 14 days prior) key off it — see [[04-projects/60th-birthday-party/PROJECT-OVERVIEW]].
- Are your Egham travel days ever in the morning peak (06:30–09:30)? If so the railcard discount doesn't apply on that leg — worth checking your typical train times before assuming the saving.
- 1yr (£30) vs 3yr (£70) Senior Railcard — 3yr pays off if you keep commuting to Egham; quick payback either way at 1/3 off.

## Strategic Intelligence

### Key Insights
1. **The convenience default (contactless everywhere) is the expensive mistake here.** For once, tapping your phone is the *wrong* move — the Egham leg needs a pre-bought railcard ticket. Muscle memory will cost you; make "SWR app before I leave" the Egham-day ritual.
2. **This is a set-up-once, save-forever job.** A couple of hours of admin around your 60th locks in free London travel + 1/3 off Egham indefinitely (until Freedom Pass makes London free entirely at State Pension age).
3. **Peak-time exclusion is the asterisk.** If office days force early trains, factor that the discount vanishes 06:30–09:30 — might nudge your travel time later where flexible.
