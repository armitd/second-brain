# Braindump: Cottage Garden Planting Scheme

## Raw Thoughts
Planning a cottage garden planting scheme. Reference: Old Castle Cottage guide (Part 3) — how to plan and execute a cottage garden.

## Reference: Key Takeaways from Old Castle Cottage Guide

### Design Principles
- **Informal edges:** Use a garden hose to mark natural, flowing bed lines rather than rigid shapes
- **Layer by height:** Low-growing plants at edges → medium-height in middle → tall at the back
- **Colour palette:** Pinks, purples, and whites for cohesion — a restrained palette avoids visual noise
- **Vertical interest:** Climbing roses on structures to draw the eye upward
- **Scale:** "Anyone can create a cottage garden using a few simple principles" — no large budget or space required

### Soil Preparation
- Amend heavily with compost — aim for a rich, crumbly base
- Options for clearing: remove sod entirely, or use no-dig method with cardboard + compost overlay
- Fill individual planting holes with specialist planting amendment

### Planting Technique
- Dig holes matching pot size (a garden auger speeds this up significantly)
- Space to allow for mature plant spread and spillage — don't plant to current size

## Strategic Insights

### Key Insights
1. **The three-tier height rule is the most transferable principle** — even if I don't use any of these specific plants, low-mid-back layering will make the bed work
2. **Colour restraint matters more than plant selection** — a coherent palette of 2-3 colours will look better than a wider mix of "nice" plants
3. **Compost investment upfront saves replanting later** — perennials will sit for years; getting the soil right before planting is much easier than amending around established plants
