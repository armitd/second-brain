## About Hebes

Hebes are evergreen flowering shrubs native to New Zealand. In the UK they're popular for low-maintenance structure, year-round foliage, and long flowering seasons. April is a good time to plant — ground is warming, frost risk is dropping, and they have the whole growing season to establish before winter.

---

## Spring Planting Guide

### Timing
- **Best window:** Mid-April to May in most of the UK
- **Frost risk:** Hebes dislike hard frost, especially in the first winter. Planting in spring gives them the longest possible establishment period before their first cold snap
- **Avoid:** Planting in waterlogged or frozen ground

### Site & Soil
- **Sun:** Full sun to partial shade. More sun = more flowers
- **Soil:** Well-drained is the key requirement. Hebes hate waterlogged roots — this is the most common cause of failure
- **Soil prep:** Dig in some grit if the soil is heavy clay. They don't need rich soil — too fertile and they grow soft and less hardy
- **Coastal gardens:** Hebes are excellent coastal plants — salt-tolerant and wind-resistant

### Planting
1. Dig a hole roughly twice the width of the root ball
2. Loosen roots gently if pot-bound
3. Plant at the same depth as in the pot — don't bury the stem
4. Backfill and firm in well
5. Water thoroughly
6. Mulch around (not touching) the stem to retain moisture and suppress weeds

### Spacing
- Small/compact varieties (e.g., 'Pewter Dome', 'Red Edge'): 45–60cm apart
- Medium varieties (e.g., 'Great Orme', 'Midsummer Beauty'): 60–90cm apart
- Larger varieties: 90cm–1m+

## Varieties Worth Knowing

| Variety | Size | Hardiness | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **'Pewter Dome'** | Small (60cm) | Very hardy | Grey-green foliage, white flowers. Reliable |
| **'Red Edge'** | Small (50cm) | Very hardy | Red-edged leaves, lilac flowers. Good winter colour |
| **'Great Orme'** | Medium (1m) | Hardy | Long spikes of pink flowers, July–October |
| **'Autumn Glory'** | Medium (60cm) | Hardy | Purple flowers late season. Long flowering |
| **'Midsummer Beauty'** | Medium-large (1.2m) | Moderate | Lavender flowers. Sheltered spot needed |
| **'Wiri Mist'** | Small-medium (75cm) | Hardy | White flowers, neat habit |
| **'Caledonia'** | Small (60cm) | Hardy | Violet flowers, reliable |

**General rule:** Small-leaved hebes = hardier. Large-leaved hebes = less frost tolerant, need a sheltered spot.

## Aftercare

- **Watering:** Water regularly for the first season, especially in dry spells. Once established they're fairly drought tolerant
- **Feeding:** A light balanced feed in spring (not high nitrogen — promotes soft growth). Generally they don't need heavy feeding
- **Pruning:** Light trim after flowering to keep shape. Don't cut back into old wood — hebes don't regenerate well from bare stems. Remove any frost-damaged growth in spring once the risk has passed
- **Winter protection:** In the first winter, a light fleece over tender varieties during hard frosts is worthwhile. After that, established plants handle typical UK winters
