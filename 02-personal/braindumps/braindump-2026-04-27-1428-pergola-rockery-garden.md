# Braindump: Pergola & Rockery — Bottom of Garden

## Raw Thoughts
How to install a pergola at the bottom of the garden - also should we install a rockery?

## Content Analysis

### Main Themes
1. **Pergola Installation:** Considering adding a pergola structure at the bottom of the garden — planning, method, and execution.
2. **Rockery Decision:** Evaluating whether a rockery would complement or replace part of the garden plan.
3. **Garden Design Integration:** Both ideas likely stem from wanting to improve the bottom of the garden into a more purposeful, attractive space.

### Supporting Ideas
- Bottom of garden often underused — a pergola creates a destination point and adds vertical interest
- Pergola can serve as a shaded seating/dining area, or support climbing plants (wisteria, roses, clematis)
- A rockery would suit a sloped or uneven bottom of garden, and reduces ongoing lawn maintenance
- The two features could coexist: rockery leading up to or framing a pergola
- Both are relatively DIY-able with some planning; pergola may need footings depending on size

### Questions Raised
- What is the ground like at the bottom of the garden — level, sloped, soft soil?
- What size pergola — small intimate seating area or larger entertaining space?
- Is the pergola free-standing or attached to a wall/fence?
- What's the primary use — shade, climbing plants, dining, all of the above?
- Is a rockery to replace lawn, add visual interest, or deal with a slope?
- Budget for each: DIY vs. hired landscaper?
- Planning permission needed? (UK rules: most garden structures under 2.5m at eaves are permitted development)

### Decisions Contemplated
- **Pergola vs. no pergola:** Likely leaning toward yes given the framing of the question
- **Rockery alongside pergola:** Open question — may depend on available space and ground conditions
- **DIY vs. professional install:** Pergola is very achievable DIY; rockery is physically heavy work

## Strategic Intelligence

### Key Insights
1. **Sequencing matters:** Install pergola footings/posts before any rockery work — groundwork for the pergola could disturb a freshly laid rockery
2. **Rockery suits low-maintenance goals:** If the bottom of the garden is hard to mow or water, a rockery solves that problem structurally
3. **Pergola adds year-round value:** With the right climbing plants and outdoor lighting, a pergola transforms into a usable outdoor room even in UK weather

### Strategic Implications
- A pergola + rockery combination could significantly increase garden usability and kerb appeal
- Worth doing a rough sketch of the space before committing — measure the bottom of the garden to understand what fits
- If planning to sell the property in the next few years, both features add value; if long-term home, optimise for personal enjoyment
