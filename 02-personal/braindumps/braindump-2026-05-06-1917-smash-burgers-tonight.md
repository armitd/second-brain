### Key Things to Get Right

**The technique:**
- Use 80/20 beef mince — fat content is essential for the crust and flavour. Don't use lean mince.
- Portion into loose 80–100g balls — don't pack them tight.
- Get the pan (or griddle) extremely hot before the beef goes in — cast iron or heavy stainless, nothing non-stick.
- Smash hard and flat immediately on contact — use a burger press or heavy spatula; hold for 10 seconds. The smash has to happen in the first few seconds before the crust forms.
- Don't touch again until a deep brown crust has formed on the underside — usually 2–3 minutes.
- Add American cheese (or similar high-melt processed cheese) immediately after the flip so it melts fully.
- Double patty is the classic smash format — two thin smashed patties per burger, cheese on each.

**The bun:**
- Brioche buns — soft, slightly sweet, toasted on the flat of the pan in the burger fat.
- Size matters: smaller buns (slider-size) work best for double smash stack proportions.

**The sauce:**
- Smash sauce = mayo + American mustard + ketchup + finely diced pickles (or pickle juice) + pinch of garlic powder. Mix in advance.
- **Ratios:** 3 tbsp mayo · 1 tsp American mustard · 1 tsp ketchup · 1 tbsp finely diced dill pickles · pinch of garlic powder
- Optional additions: pinch of onion powder, dash of smoked paprika
- Mix 20–30 minutes ahead and let it sit — flavours knit together
- Apply to both bun halves, not just one.

**The build (bottom to top):**
- Bottom bun (sauced) → patty + cheese → patty + cheese → pickles → shredded iceberg → onion (raw or griddled) → top bun (sauced)
