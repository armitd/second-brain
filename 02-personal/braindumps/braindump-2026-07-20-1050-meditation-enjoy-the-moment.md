# Braindump: Meditation Methods to Enjoy the Moment

## Raw Thoughts
Meditation methods to help enjoy the moment - and not keep thinking about what's coming.

## Content Analysis

### Main Themes
1. **Present-moment attention:** The core want is to be *here*, in the experience actually happening, rather than mentally living in the next thing.
2. **Future-tripping / anticipatory thinking:** The specific obstacle is the mind running ahead to "what's coming" — planning, anticipating, pre-living. Not general anxiety, more a habit of forward-projection that pulls you out of now.
3. **Savouring vs monitoring:** Enjoying the moment isn't just noticing it — it's letting yourself feel the good of it. Two different muscles: attention (be here) and savouring (enjoy being here).

### The Methods (practical, low-friction)

**Anchor practices — pull attention back to now**
- **Breath anchor (the basic one):** Attention rests on the breath. When the mind wanders to "what's next", notice it, silently label it, and come back. The *coming back* is the rep, not the staying. 5–10 mins.
- **5-4-3-2-1 grounding:** Name 5 things you see, 4 you hear, 3 you feel, 2 you smell, 1 you taste. Drops you into the senses instantly — good in the moment (a walk, a coffee, the garden), no formal sit needed.
- **Body scan:** Slowly move attention head-to-toe. Trains sustained present attention and settles the "planning" mind. Good before sleep.

**Noting — defuse the future-thoughts specifically**
- **Labelling:** When the mind jumps ahead, silently note "planning" or "future". Naming it loosens its grip — you see the thought instead of being inside it. This directly targets "what's coming".
- **"Not now, later":** A gentle mental note that the future thought is real but doesn't need attention *right now*. Optionally park it (jot it down) so the mind trusts it's captured and lets go.

**Savouring — the enjoy half**
- **One conscious pleasure a day:** Pick one ordinary good thing (first sip of coffee, sun on your face, a chord on the guitar) and give it your full attention for 30 seconds. Deliberate savouring.
- **STOP (micro-practice):** Stop · Take a breath · Observe (what's here right now) · Proceed. 20 seconds, done anywhere, several times a day. Turns "enjoy the moment" into a habit rather than an aspiration.

**Everyday / informal**
- **Single-tasking:** Do one thing at a time and let it be the whole thing (eat without the phone, walk without a podcast sometimes). Present-moment training disguised as normal life.
- **Habit anchors:** Attach a breath-and-notice to something you already do daily (kettle boiling, at a red light, before opening the laptop).

### Questions Raised
- Do you want a **formal daily sit** (build the muscle), **in-the-moment micro-practices** (use it when it matters), or both? They reinforce each other.
- Guided vs unguided? A guided app lowers the activation energy at the start.
- What's the actual trigger for the future-thinking — evenings? Downtime? Specific worries? Knowing the trigger tells you where to place the practice.

## Strategic Intelligence

### Key Insights
1. **The goal isn't a quiet mind, it's a quick return.** The mind *will* run to what's coming — that's normal. The skill is noticing sooner and coming back gently. Every return is a rep; there's no failing at it.
2. **Attention and savouring are separate skills.** You can be present and still not enjoy it. Build both: anchor practices for presence, savouring practices for enjoyment.
3. **Micro beats marathon for this specific problem.** "What's coming" hijacks you in ordinary moments, so the fix that works is tiny practices *in* those moments (STOP, 5-4-3-2-1), not only a 20-minute morning sit.
