2. **Duplication risk:** Overlapping capabilities often mean duplicated investment, inconsistent outcomes, and confusion over ownership
3. **Governance question:** Overlaps usually surface a deeper question: who owns what, and who decides?

## Strategic Intelligence

### Key Insights

1. **Capability overlap is an EA problem with an EA solution.** The right response is a capability map — a single view of what capabilities exist, who owns them, and how they relate. Without that map, the overlap conversation happens in meetings but never gets resolved.

2. **Overlap often signals a governance gap, not a design flaw.** CCOTF may have built capabilities because the front-office's version didn't meet its needs — or because there was no clear owner. Understanding *why* the overlap happened is as important as documenting *what* overlaps.

3. **This is a high-value visibility opportunity.** Mapping and resolving a capability overlap between two significant parts of the business is meaningful EA work — visible, tangible, and directly connected to business outcomes (reduced duplication, clearer ownership, better customer experience).
