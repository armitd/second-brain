# Braindump: Budgeting Starts July — Roadmaps Needed

## Raw Thoughts
Budgeting starts in July — need to get roadmaps in place.

---

## Content Analysis

### What This Means
Budgeting starting in July 2026 means roadmaps need to be ready **before** July — likely by mid-June to allow review cycles and stakeholder input before budget submissions. That's approximately 10 weeks from now.

Roadmaps feed budgets. No roadmap = no justified budget ask. For an EA function, this is the moment where the architecture work becomes a financial conversation — capability investments, technology initiatives, and programme dependencies all need to be costed and sequenced.

### Questions Raised
- Which roadmaps need to be ready? (Technology roadmap? Capability roadmap? Per-programme? Per-opco?)
- Who is the audience — central IT leadership, Belron board, opco finance teams?
- What format is expected — PowerPoint, a tool like LeanIX, a structured document?
- Are these roadmaps EA-owned, or does EA input into roadmaps owned by others (delivery teams, product teams)?
- What initiatives from this week's thinking need to be on a roadmap — CCOTF, damage assessment, Nordics project, agentic AI governance?
- Is there an existing roadmap that needs updating, or is this a greenfield exercise?

### Supporting Ideas
- The roadmap is the EA function's most important budget-cycle artefact — it justifies investment in terms of capability and business outcome, not just technology
- Roadmaps that aren't connected to business capabilities (from the BCM work) or the customer journey are much harder to defend in a budget conversation
- LeanIX roadmap views can feed directly into budget conversations — another reason to get the Fact Sheets populated before July
- The Wardley Map of Belron's capabilities (from this week) is a natural input — it shows which capabilities need investment (custom/emerging) vs. which should be cost-reduced (commodity)

---

## Strategic Intelligence

### Key Insights

1. **10 weeks is tight if roadmaps don't exist yet.** The sequencing is: understand the landscape (Belron business understanding framework, Phase 1 complete by end of April) → identify the initiatives (May) → build and validate roadmaps (May–June) → present for budget input (June). Every week of delay in the understanding phase compresses the roadmap phase.

2. **Roadmaps without capability grounding won't survive budget scrutiny.** A technology roadmap that says "upgrade the booking system" is weak. One that says "invest in the Customer Booking capability to support CCOTF and reduce insurance partner friction" is defensible. The BCM work from April 7 needs to be the foundation.

3. **The EA roadmap should include the agentic AI governance work.** MCP/A2A governance, the LeanIX implementation, the emerging technology deliverables — these need budget if they're going to happen. The roadmap is how that happens.

4. **This connects directly to the 5-year plan.** The roadmap is the 1-year view of the 5-year plan. If the 5-year plan doesn't exist yet (April 8 braindump — still TBD), the roadmap risks being reactive rather than strategic.
