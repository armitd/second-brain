#### Option A: Agentic AI — MCP, A2A, and What It Means for Belron

**The topic:** The shift from AI as assistant to AI as autonomous actor in enterprise workflows. MCP (97M installs, Linux Foundation governance) and A2A (Google, 50+ partners) as the protocol standards that make multi-agent enterprise systems governable.

**Why it's strong:**
- Well-researched this week — the A2A/MCP braindump is a near-complete research paper already
- Directly relevant to Belron's systems (booking → dispatch → calibration → claims is a natural multi-agent workflow)
- EA owns this governance territory — this positions the function as forward-thinking
- Genuinely emerging: 40% of enterprise apps will embed agents by year-end (Gartner)
- External validation from daily briefs: OpenAI $122B, NVIDIA agent platform, Okta for AI Agents all landed this week

**Presentation angle:** "The Agent Economy — What It Means for Belron's Architecture"
**Paper angle:** "MCP and A2A: A Framework for Governing Agentic AI in Enterprise"
**Briefing angle:** One-pager for leadership — what decisions need to be made now before agents proliferate ad hoc

---

#### Option B: AI Damage Assessment — The Build Case

**The topic:** How AI and computer vision can transform the customer damage assessment experience — from customer photographs windscreen → instant repair/replace recommendation → seamless booking.

**Why it's strong:**
- Directly tied to Belron's business (not abstract)
- The market scan from this morning provides the vendor landscape
- Tractable at $1B shows the market is real and moving fast
- The build window argument (now to 2028 before it commoditises) creates urgency
- Combines machine vision, foundation models, UX, and data strategy in one narrative

**Presentation angle:** "AI Damage Assessment — The Opportunity and the Build Case"
**Paper angle:** "Building a Customer-Facing AI Damage Assessment Solution: Architecture, Vendors, and Data Strategy"
**Briefing angle:** For a senior sponsor — what is the opportunity, what would it cost, and what's the decision?

---

#### Option C: LeanIX and the EA Repository — Making Architecture Visible

**The topic:** How a modern EA management platform (LeanIX) transforms the EA function from a documentation service into a living, queryable source of truth — with AI features (Inventory Builder, Joule copilot) that make it accessible to non-EA stakeholders.

**Why it's strong:**
- Concrete, tool-grounded, immediately actionable
- Directly tied to the EA function establishment work
- LeanIX's new AI features (Inventory Builder, Joule) are genuinely new and interesting
- Narrower in scope — probably the easiest to execute well

**Less strong because:** More internally focused; less likely to generate broad interest beyond EA peers

### Recommendation

**Option A (Agentic AI / MCP + A2A) is the strongest topic** for a three-part deliverable set:
- It's the most forward-looking and strategically significant
- The research is already done (A2A/MCP braindump)
- It positions EA as the function that owns the governance of the next major technology wave
- It will still be relevant in 12–18 months
- It has the broadest audience: leadership, IT peers, opco partners, business stakeholders

**Option B** is a strong second — more grounded in the Belron business, but requires more research and depends on whether the damage assessment project has internal momentum.
