3. **Your three active projects are BA in disguise** — each of them is already a BA problem that hasn't been framed that way yet:
   - **AI Damage Assessment PoC** = the "Vehicle Assessment" capability, currently human-performed, being transformed by AI. BA frames this as a capability transformation with technology as the enabler.
   - **Contact Centre of the Future** = the "Customer Engagement" value stream being redesigned. BA gives you the value stream map to show what's being automated, augmented, or eliminated.
   - **MCP Governance** = the "IT Governance" capability area — BA connects it to business policies, compliance requirements, and enterprise risk appetite rather than just technology standards.

### TOGAF BA Concepts — Application to Belron

**Business Capabilities** (what the business does, independent of how):
- Core Belron capabilities likely include: Customer Acquisition, Appointment Management, Vehicle Assessment, Glass Repair, Glass Replacement, ADAS Calibration, Claims Management, Technician Dispatch, Customer Communication
- Capability heat-mapping against AI readiness would immediately surface which capabilities (like Vehicle Assessment and Customer Communication) are priority transformation targets

**Value Streams** (how value flows to the customer):
- Primary value stream: Customer reports damage → Assessment → Appointment → Repair/Replace → Satisfaction
- Each step in this stream is a BA analysis unit — where are the hand-offs? Where does value leak? Where does wait time accumulate?
- CCOTF lives in the first two steps; AI Damage Assessment lives in the second step

**Business Motivation Model** (strategy → tactics → directives):
- Belron's strategic intent (market leader in vehicle glass, expanding into ADAS) → tactical objectives (faster claims, higher first-visit fix rates) → directives (AI investment, ADAS capability build) maps cleanly onto the motivation model
- This is the BA layer that lets you connect an AI PoC to a board-level objective without it feeling like a stretch

**Organisation Map** (who does what):
- Cross-OpCo complexity (Autoglass UK, Carglass France/Germany, Safelite US) means BA organisational mapping would surface where capability is duplicated vs. where it's shared — relevant for any AI rollout strategy

**Business Services** (what capabilities expose to whom):
- Distinguishes what Belron does internally from what it exposes to customers, insurers, and OEMs — useful for defining where AI agents should sit in CCOTF and AI Damage Assessment

### Pattern Recognition
- **Connection to EA practice:** BA is the missing link between strategy documents and architecture decisions that currently happens informally in EA work — making it explicit creates reusable artefacts
- **Connection to AI projects:** Each AI project proposal would be stronger with a BA backing — a capability model shows the gap being closed; a value stream shows the friction being removed
- **LeanIX alignment:** LeanIX has Business Architecture features (value streams, capability maps) that are likely underused — BA course knowledge maps directly to LeanIX practice

### Strategic Implications
- The practical payoff is in joint working sessions: being able to sit with a BA reviewing a capability model or value stream and ask the right questions — "how does this capability change with AI augmentation?" or "where does this value stream create architecture constraints?" — rather than being a passive consumer
- Producing a Belron capability model as a learning artefact is less valuable than engaging with the BAs' existing artefacts and contributing an EA/technology perspective to them
- The TOGAF BA certification (if pursued) would differentiate — most EAs have TOGAF Architecture Framework but not the BA specialisation
