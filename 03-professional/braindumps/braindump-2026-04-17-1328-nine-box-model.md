### Supporting Ideas
- The 9-box model is a performance-vs-potential matrix — a 3×3 grid placing employees on two axes: current performance (x) and future potential (y)
- Originally developed by McKinsey and GE in the 1970s as part of GE's talent management system under Jack Welch
- Common use cases: succession planning, high-potential identification, talent development prioritisation, leadership pipeline assessment
- As an EA at Belron, the 9-box is likely appearing in leadership or people strategy conversations — particularly relevant in the context of a potential IPO, where leadership bench strength becomes a formal due diligence area
