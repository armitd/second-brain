## Framework: What the S&P 500 Actually Measures (and Doesn't)

### What the S&P 500 IS a good proxy for
- **US corporate earnings power** — the 500 largest US companies; their combined health reflects the US-centric global economy
- **Global revenue exposure** — S&P companies derive 40-60%+ of revenue outside the US (especially tech, industrials, energy); the index reflects global corporate health through a US lens
- **Capital flows into USD assets** — rising S&P = global capital flowing into USD-denominated assets = dollar system confidence
- **Tech/AI sector supremacy** — NVDA, MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN, META are 30%+ of the S&P; their performance is a direct proxy for who is winning the AI/tech race

### What the S&P 500 MISSES
- **China's trajectory** — China is not in the S&P. A rising S&P tells you nothing about whether China is gaining or losing ground.
- **European stability** — the S&P gives you no signal on EU cohesion, EUR stability, or whether Europe is diverging from the US
- **Commodity power shifts** — oil/gas geopolitics are underrepresented relative to their strategic importance
- **Emerging market multipolar shift** — whether the dollar system is being challenged by BRICS alternatives is invisible in S&P data

## The Dashboard: S&P + 5 Comparative Signals

### Tier 0: Leading Indicator — Check This First (check weekly)

VIX is the only forward-looking signal in the dashboard. Everything else is a lagging or concurrent measure. VIX spikes *before* or *as* geopolitical and macro shocks land — which makes it the most time-sensitive signal to watch.

| VIX Level | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **< 15** | Complacent — markets pricing in calm; good IPO conditions |
| **15–25** | Normal range — watch for direction of travel |
| **> 25** | Stress — smart money is pricing in risk; IPO window under pressure |
| **> 35** | Crisis — window effectively closed; capital in risk-off mode |

**For the Belron IPO specifically:** VIX is the single most actionable number. A sustained move above 25 in Q3 2026 would shift the IPO timing calculus. Check it weekly; treat any move toward 25 as an early warning.

### Tier 1: The World Order Ratio (check monthly)

| Comparison | What It Tells You |
|---|---|
| **S&P 500 vs MSCI EM** | US corporate system vs emerging markets — is the West-led order outperforming or the multipolar world catching up? |
| **S&P 500 vs CSI 300 (China)** | US-China power balance as measured by investor capital allocation |
| **S&P 500 vs EuroStoxx 50** | US vs European economic trajectory — divergence signals geopolitical or economic fracture |

### Tier 2: Structural Stress Signals (check monthly)

| Signal | What It Tells You |
|---|---|
| **DXY (Dollar Index)** | Reserve currency health. DXY rising = capital fleeing to USD = world order under stress but still USD-dominant. DXY falling = either risk-on OR dollar system being challenged. |
| **Gold / USD ratio** | Trust in the fiat system. Gold rising vs USD = either inflation hedge OR loss of confidence in dollar system. |
| **Oil price (Brent Crude)** | Energy geopolitics. Sustained high oil = commodity powers (Russia, Saudi Arabia, Iran) have leverage. Low oil = US/Western supply dominance. |

### Tier 3: Sector Rotation Within the S&P (check monthly)

| Sector Rotation | What It Signals |
|---|---|
| **Defense ETFs (ITA, XAR) outperforming** | Geopolitical tension premium — investors pricing in conflict risk |
| **Energy (XOM, CVX) outperforming** | Commodity power shifting; supply chain or geopolitical pressure on energy |
| **Semiconductors (NVDA, AMD, SOXX) outperforming** | AI/tech race intensifying; capital flowing to the companies that define tech supremacy |
| **Financials outperforming** | Dollar system healthy; credit and capital flowing normally |
| **Gold miners (GDX) outperforming** | Dollar system under stress; hedging behaviour |
