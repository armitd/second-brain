# Braindump: Business Capability Modelling — Internal Education Strategy

## Raw Thoughts

Start thinking about business capability modelling. How to create material for the rest of the company to understand the topic.

---

## Content Analysis

### Main Themes
1. **Business Capability Modelling as a discipline:** Getting clarity on what BCM is, how it works, and why it matters — foundational groundwork before educating others
2. **Internal enablement:** Creating materials that make an abstract EA concept accessible to a non-EA audience across different roles and levels
3. **Communication and translation:** The challenge of bridging the gap between EA vocabulary and business language
4. **Organisational change readiness:** Getting the company to engage with capability thinking as part of how they discuss strategy and investment

### Supporting Ideas
- BCM defines *what* the business does (capabilities) independent of *how* it does it (processes, systems, org structure) — that distinction is the core message to land
- Different audiences need different angles: executives need the strategic investment lens; middle management need the operational clarity lens; IT teams need the systems-to-capability mapping lens
- Windscreen repair/replace business has a relatively bounded capability set — this is actually an advantage for a first map (simpler than a conglomerate)
- The risk of BCM materials is they become too theoretical — concrete examples from the company's own business are essential

### Questions Raised
- Who is the primary audience to start with — leadership, middle management, or IT?
- What's the business problem BCM is solving right now? (The "why now" narrative matters for adoption)
- Does the company already have any strategic planning language that BCM can attach to (e.g. OKRs, transformation programmes)?
- What format will land best — workshops, a one-pager, a slide deck, a wiki page?

### Decisions Contemplated
- Where to start: top-down (executive briefing first) vs. bottom-up (build the map quietly, then socialise)
- Whether to use an established framework explicitly (TOGAF, BIZBOK) or keep it framework-agnostic for accessibility
- How much detail to go into vs. keeping it high-level for the first pass

## Strategic Intelligence

### Key Insights
1. **BCM is a shared language tool, not just an EA artefact.** The value isn't the diagram — it's getting business stakeholders to think and talk in capability terms. Materials should teach the *thinking*, not just explain the *concept*.
2. **Start with the map, not the theory.** Showing people a draft capability map of their own company creates immediate engagement. Abstract explanations of BCM without a tangible artefact tend to lose people quickly.
3. **The "what vs. how" distinction is the one thing to make stick.** Every piece of communication material should reinforce this single idea — capabilities describe what the business does, regardless of who does it, what system supports it, or how the org is structured.
4. **The windscreen/auto glass context is a gift.** It's a service business with clear, grounded capabilities (e.g. damage assessment, scheduling, glass sourcing, installation, calibration, customer management, claims processing). This makes the abstraction concrete fast.
5. **Framing matters more than methodology.** Don't lead with "enterprise architecture" or "TOGAF." Lead with business outcomes: "a clearer picture of what we do, where we invest, and where there are gaps."

### Pattern Recognition
- **Connection to previous thinking:** Aligns with today's brief — the shift in EA from documentation to decision governance. BCM is the foundational layer that enables that shift.
- **Recurring pattern:** The gap between EA theory and business comprehension is a perennial EA challenge. The answer is always the same: use the company's own language and examples, not framework vocabulary.

### Strategic Implications
- A well-socialised BCM capability map positions the EA function as a strategic partner, not a technical support function
- The materials created here could become a repeatable asset — used for onboarding, strategic planning cycles, and investment prioritisation conversations
- Getting BCM adopted as a common language raises the ceiling for what EA can achieve in the business
