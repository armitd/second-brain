# Braindump: Open Semantic Interchange (OSI) Standard Layer

## Raw Thoughts
Research Open Semantic Interchange Standard Layer

## Context (quick web lookup, not full research)
The Open Semantic Interchange (OSI) is an industry-wide initiative to standardise how semantic metadata — metrics, definitions, business logic — is exchanged across analytics, AI, and BI platforms. The goal is a vendor-neutral, single source of truth so the same metric definition works consistently across dashboards, notebooks, and ML/AI models, instead of being redefined per tool.

- **Led by:** Snowflake, with backing from Collibra and other industry players; 14+ new participants joined as of mid-2026
- **Status:** v1.0 specification released 27 January 2026. Phase 1 (spec finalisation, reference implementations, governance) is largely complete. Phase 2 (Q2–Q4 2026) targets native support across 50+ platforms, domain-specific extensions, and pilot programmes
- **Recent activity:** A Financial Services Semantic Working Group held its first formal meeting in June 2026, with practitioners from banking, insurance, asset management, and market infrastructure
- **Repo/spec:** [open-semantic-interchange/OSI on GitHub](https://github.com/open-semantic-interchange/OSI)
