## Background: Salesforce Data Landscape (orientation notes)

**Salesforce Customer 360** is the overarching brand — the idea that all Salesforce products connect to give a unified view of the customer across sales, service, marketing, and commerce.

**Salesforce Data Cloud** (formerly Salesforce CDP / Genie) is the underlying real-time customer data platform:
- Ingests data from Salesforce products + external sources
- Creates a unified, real-time customer profile
- Powers personalisation, AI (Einstein), and cross-channel engagement
- Now the data backbone of the entire Customer 360 suite

**"Data 360"** may refer to:
- A Salesforce marketing/positioning term for their unified data story
- A specific offering or bundle in their current portfolio
- An evolution of Data Cloud branding (Salesforce rebrands frequently)

**Relevance for Belron specifically (Service Cloud + Marketing Cloud already live):**

Belron is already paying for two Salesforce clouds. Data Cloud is Salesforce's pitch to connect them:

- **Service Cloud + Data Cloud:** Contact centre agents get a real-time unified customer profile during a call — full history of bookings, repairs, claims, marketing interactions, lifetime value. This is directly CCOTF-relevant. Without Data Cloud, Service Cloud agents only see what's in Service Cloud.
- **Marketing Cloud + Data Cloud:** Personalised, triggered marketing journeys based on real customer behaviour across all channels — not just email history but service interactions, ADAS calibration records, etc.
- **Einstein AI + Data Cloud:** Real-time AI predictions (next best action, churn risk, upsell propensity) fire against the unified profile. Salesforce's AI story is now entirely built on Data Cloud as the substrate.

**The "Data 360 / Customer 360" framing:** Salesforce positions this as completing the picture — with Service Cloud, Marketing Cloud, and Data Cloud together, every customer touchpoint is unified and actionable in real time. For Belron's customer journey (book → service → calibration → follow-up), this is a genuine capability uplift, not just a platform sale.

**The honest challenge:** Data Cloud licensing is significant — it's priced on data volumes and activations, not per-seat. Organisations that bought it and underused it found it expensive for the value. The value is only real if the data from Service Cloud and Marketing Cloud is actually connected and the use cases (agent 360 view, triggered journeys) are built out.
