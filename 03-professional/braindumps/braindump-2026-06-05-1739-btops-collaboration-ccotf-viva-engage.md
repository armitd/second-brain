## Content Analysis

### Main Themes

1. **BT&Ops collaboration gap** — there's a felt need to improve how Business Technology and Operations collaborate. This is a relationship/ways-of-working question, not a technology one — the question is what's getting in the way and what could make it better.

2. **CCOTF community platform** — two connected thoughts: (a) create a Microsoft Teams team for CCOTF as the programme's collaboration space, and (b) whether Teams + SharePoint can replicate the community/social feel of Viva Engage ("Contact Centre Hive" concept from the June 2 daybook).

3. **Book holidays** — personal action item, already captured as a task.

### Questions Raised

- What specifically is the BT&Ops collaboration friction? Is it about visibility, decision-making, communication rhythm, or tooling?
- Who should be involved in defining how BT&Ops collaborate better — is this a bilateral conversation, or a wider team discussion?
- Can Teams + SharePoint genuinely replicate Viva Engage for a community? Or is Viva Engage the right tool and the question is whether to use it?
- What does the CCOTF Teams team need — channels, tabs, a planner board, a SharePoint site? What's the MVP?

### Decisions Contemplated

- **Community platform for CCOTF:** Teams team (structured programme space) vs. Viva Engage community (open social/community feel). These serve different purposes — a Teams team is for the programme delivery group; Viva Engage is for a wider contact centre practitioner community.
- **BT&Ops collaboration:** Not yet at decision stage — diagnosis comes first.

---

## Strategic Intelligence

### Key Insights

1. **Teams and Viva Engage answer different questions.** A CCOTF Teams team is for the programme — the core group doing the work, with channels for workstreams, a SharePoint site for documents, Planner for tasks. Viva Engage is for the broader community — the "Contact Centre Hive" concept where contact centre practitioners across opcos share knowledge, surface problems, and build a community of practice. Both are probably needed, not either/or.

2. **BT&Ops collaboration is a recurring pattern.** If this came up as a daybook thought, it's likely something that surfaced in a meeting or interaction. The question "how can we collaborate better" usually signals either: unclear ownership at the BT/Ops boundary, or a communication rhythm that isn't working. Worth diagnosing before proposing a solution.

3. **The CCOTF Teams team is an early quick win.** Creating the team, setting up channels (Discovery, Vendor Evaluation, Comms, Governance), and adding the right people is a low-effort deliverable that signals the programme is real and organised. Do this first.

### Strategic Implications

- The BT&Ops collaboration question may connect to the EA/SA/TA operating model braindump — if EAs are in BT and SAs are in Ops (or vice versa), unclear handoffs create collaboration friction
- The CCOTF community platform decision should be documented as an early programme decision — it sets the precedent for how the programme communicates internally and with opcos
