Don't invent AI governance. Inherit a model that already works.

“If you are working in LeanIX, then it makes sense to govern in LeanIX.”

The LeanIX governance model — the discipline we already run over the application estate — maps cleanly onto AI. Same register, same ownership, same lifecycle. Applied to use cases, models and agents.

What LeanIX governs today
Authoritative register of applications
A named, accountable owner per asset
Defined lifecycle: Plan → Active → Retire
Portfolio risk & cost heatmaps
Owner self-service: fact-sheet subscriptions keep data fresh

Applied to the AI estate
One inventory of AI use cases, models & agents
An accountable owner per AI system
Lifecycle: PoC → Pilot → Production → Retire
AI risk classification & spend visibility
AI owners self-certify their own systems

Why this, why now

01 Cheaper to inherit than invent AI governance is being stood up from scratch. Adopting a proven pattern beats running a second, disconnected AI registry.
02 A pre-IPO asset "We know every AI system, its owner and its risk posture" is a strong story for IPO-grade due diligence.
03 Dependencies stop hiding Registered as fact sheets, AI sits in the same landscape as the apps and integrations it actually touches.
The ask

Pilot it small. Register today's AI PoCs — starting with AI Damage Assessment — as LeanIX fact sheets. Guardrails that let you scale safely and pass scrutiny, not a gate that slows you down.
