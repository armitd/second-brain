- Summary: A Likert scale is a survey tool that measures people's levels of agreement or disagreement with statements. ## Highlights
- A **Likert scale** ([/ˈlɪkərt/](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English) [*LIK-ərt*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key),[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Likert_scale/#cite_note-1)[[note 1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Likert_scale/#cite_note-2)) is a [psychometric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychometrics) scale named after its inventor, American social psychologist [Rensis Likert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rensis_Likert),[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Likert_scale/#cite_note-3) which is commonly used in research [questionnaires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Questionnaire). It is the most widely used approach to scaling responses in survey research, such that the term (or more fully the **Likert-type scale**) is often used interchangeably with *[rating scale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rating_scale)*, although there are other types of rating scales.
