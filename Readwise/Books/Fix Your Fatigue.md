## Highlights
- Mitochondria are the power plants of our cells. Oxidation describes the reaction of a substance with oxygen. During respiration, food is oxidized to produce energy. In cellular respiration, glucose (a sugar from the food we eat) is oxidized by oxygen (from the air we breathe), producing carbon dioxide, water and energy to fuel our bodies. Mitochondria oxidize carbohydrates, fats and amino acids and convert them into super special molecules called adenosine triphosphate or ATP.
