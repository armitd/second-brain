## Highlights
### Chapter 5: Avoiding Cultural Anti-Patterns in DevOps
- Organizations that over-automate will typically focus on the actual tools rather than the process itself. Technology in this scenario is often based on emotions, such as other organizations using similar tools or their colleagues from a conference using them
- This situation just ends up in mass confusion for everyone involved. The result is too many tools and processes being used that must be adjusted for the tooling, when in fact your tooling should be adjusted for the processes.
- One of the most common routes to modernization is to move away from existing monolithic applications to a microservice-based architecture and design patterns. This pattern represents a domain-based architecture where services are decoupled from one another and can be reused for multiple purposes.
