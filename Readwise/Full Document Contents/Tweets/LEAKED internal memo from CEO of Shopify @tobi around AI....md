10 quick takeaways i have after reading it:

  
2. a subtle but huge reframe: “hire an AI before you hire a human.”
  
4. AI is now a baseline expectation at shopify. hiring filters will probably favor ai-fluent candidates at shopify and other companies.
  
6. AI agents are now treated like teammates, not tools.

  
- prompting is now a core skill. top performers will be top prompters.

  
- AI usage is now measured. kinda wild. probably a business idea there to build the lattice for AI usage.

  
- AI-first prototyping is the new standard. shipping speed will probably 10x even at a $100B company like Shopify.

  
- org charts blur, headcount planning now includes bots, not just bodies.

  
- AI literacy is the new coding literacy. prompting, contextualizing, or evaluating ai output is become mandatory.

  
- AI is now a core layer in the software stack. not a plugin. not an add-on. ai sits beside infra, backend, frontend, and design. the best teams will be the ones who treat it like infrastructure.

  
- tobi’s memo screams one thing: more impact per person. shopify is early to this, but i bet this will hit every major company over the next 12-24 months.
