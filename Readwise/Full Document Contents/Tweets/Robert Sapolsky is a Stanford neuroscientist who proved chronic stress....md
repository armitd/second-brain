## Full Document
Robert Sapolsky is a Stanford neuroscientist who proved chronic stress is the silent killer doctors ignore.

On Chris Williamson's podcast, he revealed 10 "normal" habits you do every day that wreck your sleep, mood, and nervous system:

1) Replay conversations in your head

---

Sapolsky's exact words: "ruminating about that weird thing you said to a teacher 15 years ago."

Your brain doesn't know the conversation isn't happening right now. It triggers the same cortisol surge as the original threat.

Notice it. Name it. Redirect.

---

2) Worry about scenarios that haven't happened

Sapolsky's signature line: a zebra reacts to a lion for 30 seconds, then grazes peacefully. A human worries about an imagined lion for 30 years.

When you catch yourself: ask "is this happening right now?" 

Your browser does not support the video tag.

---

3) Doomscroll the news

Sapolsky: "You read about what's happening in Ukraine or Bangladesh and you activate the same 150-million-year-old stress circuitry that evolved for outrunning predators."

One news window per day. Same for social. 

---

4) Binge tragic or violent content

Sapolsky: humans uniquely activate a full stress response watching a screen or reading a novel.

Your nervous system can't tell fiction from reality — it spikes cortisol either way.

Audit what you consume after 7 PM. 

Your browser does not support the video tag.

---

5) Try to control what you can't

Sapolsky proved: lack of control AND trying to control uncontrollable things both spike chronic cortisol.

Either way, your nervous system stays in survival mode.

Surrender one thing per day. Practice the difference. 

Your browser does not support the video tag.

---

6) Skip social connection because you're "too busy"

Sapolsky's research: lack of social support is one of the 5 amplifiers that turns stress chronic.

Baboons with strong bonds had lower cortisol, longer lives, stronger immunity.

Schedule connection like a meeting. 

---

7) Save stress elimination for the weekend

Sapolsky's exact words: "It's not stuff you save for the weekend. It's something you've got to set time out for every day."

20 minutes daily beats 3 hours on Saturday.

Block it like a meeting. 

---

8) Force yourself to meditate when you hate it

Sapolsky: "If I spent 20 minutes meditating I would have a stroke by this weekend. Make sure it's something you actually enjoy."

If your stress practice is stressing you, it's the wrong tool.

Try walks. Music. Hobbies. 

---

9) Constantly chase the next dopamine hit

Sapolsky: "We're the species that wants and wants and wants."

Dopamine isn't about reward — it's about anticipation.

Whatever felt great yesterday feels insufficient tomorrow.

Notice when you're chasing. Pause.

10) Stay in environments that drain you

Sapolsky's baboon research: low-ranking baboons in unstable troops had high cortisol, shorter telomeres, and shorter lives — even when fed and safe.

Toxic workplaces do the same to humans.

Audit who drains you most.

TLDR — 10 habits keeping you in chronic stress:

1) Stop replaying conversations

2) No hypotheticals

3) Limit news

4) Cut tragic content

5) Surrender control

6) Schedule social

7) Daily practice

8) Make it enjoyable

9) Stop chasing dopamine

10) Audit environment
