#### BEFORE ANYTHING ELSE: THE PHILOSOPHY SHIFT

Most people use Obsidian as a filing cabinet. They store things. They feel productive. Nothing comes out. 

The setups in this list treat Obsidian as a production system - a place where raw material (notes, ideas, research, conversations) gets processed into output (articles, pitches, products, decisions). Every setup here has one test: does it help something come out of the vault, or does it just help something go in? 

If it only helps things go in, it is organisation. If it helps things come out, it is a money-making machine. 

#### CATEGORY 1: CAPTURE SETUPS

*The front door of your vault. If this is broken, everything downstream is broken.* 

**Setup 1: The Three-Inbox System**

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HKSKrzhaoAALVrM.jpg) 

Most vaults fail at capture because everything goes into one undifferentiated pile. Create three separate inbox notes instead: 

* **📥 Inbox - Ideas - half-formed thoughts, sparks, questions**
* **📥 Inbox - Reference - things you want to save for later**
* **📥 Inbox - Actions - things you need to done**

Process each inbox once a week. Not daily - weekly. The friction of processing daily kills the habit. Weekly keeps it sustainable. 

**Why it makes money:** Ideas that used to get lost in a 400-item inbox now get found, developed, and turned into content and offers. 

**Setup 2: The Voice-to-Vault Pipeline**

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HKSLwCgaUAAOMXE.jpg) 

Your best ideas do not arrive at your desk. They arrive in the shower, on a walk, in the middle of a conversation. Most of them disappear. 

Use your phone's voice memo app → auto-transcribe → paste into your Inbox-Ideas note. On iPhone, the built-in Voice Memos app transcribes automatically. On Android, use Google Recorder. 

If you have Gemma 4 12B running locally or access to Claude, run the transcription through this cleanup prompt weekly: 

***Here are my raw voice memo transcriptions from this week.***

Clean up the grammar, remove filler words, and 

break each idea into its own bullet point.

Keep every idea even if it sounds rough. 

**Why it makes money:** The ideas that became products and articles for me were almost always voice memos. The ones I typed, I filtered before I wrote them down. Voice memos capture the unfiltered ones. 

**Setup 3: The Meeting Intelligence Note** 

Create a template for every meeting you take. One note per meeting, with this structure: 

***# Meeting: [Person/Company] - [Date]***

***## Context***

[One sentence about who this person is and why you met]

***## What They Said (verbatim quotes where possible)***

-

***## What I Noticed (subtext, body language if in-person, hesitations)***

-

***## Their Real Problem (your interpretation, not what they said)***

-

***## Opportunities This Surfaces***

-

***## Next Action*** 

***The "What I Noticed" and "Their Real Problem" sections are what most note-takers skip. They are where the money is.*** 

**Why it makes money:** Client and prospect intelligence captured this way has directly shaped offers, pricing conversations, and content angles that generated revenue. 

**Setup 4: The "Stolen From the Internet" Note** 

*Create one note titled 💡 Stolen Ideas and use it as a running collection of frameworks, phrases, approaches, and ideas you encounter that you want to adapt. Not copy - adapt.* 

*When you see a great email subject line, a compelling product framing, a narrative structure that worked - add it here with a note about why it worked.* 

*Review this note before any creative work.* 

**Why it makes money:** Creativity is pattern recognition. A curated library of what works is a cheat code for faster, better output. 

**Setup 5: The Client Language Bank** 

Create a note titled 🗣️ Client Language and use it to capture the exact words your clients and audience use. Not paraphrases - verbatim phrases from emails, calls, DMs, comments. 

***# Client Language Bank***

***## How they describe their problem***

- "I have all these notes but I can't find anything"

- "I feel like I'm always starting from scratch"

- "My vault feels like a junk drawer"

***## How they describe the outcome they want***

- "I want to feel like my notes are actually working for me"

- "I want to turn my thinking into something"

***## Words they use for the solution***

- "second brain"

- "thinking system"

- "connected notes" 

**Why it makes money:** This language goes directly into your sales copy, content hooks, and offer positioning. Words your clients use convert better than words you invent. 

#### CATEGORY 2: PROCESSING SETUPS

*This is where most vaults die. Notes go in, nothing ever gets worked on.* 

**Setup 6: The Weekly Processing Ritual**

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HKSPEbDaUAAe6kY.jpg) 

Block 60 minutes every Sunday. Three-phase process: 

* **Phase 1 (20 min):** Process the three inboxes. Move each item to its permanent home or delete it.
* **Phase 2 (20 min):** Open your five most recently edited notes. Ask: "Is there anything here ready to become output?" If yes, tag it #ready.
* **Phase 3 (20 min):** Review your #ready tags. Pick one. Turn it into something - a post, a pitch, a draft, a plan.

The third phase is the one that generates revenue. Most Obsidian users never do it. 

**Why it makes money:** A note tagged #ready that never gets opened is identical in value to a note you never wrote. The processing ritual forces output. 

**Setup 7: The Maturity Scale** 

Tag every note with a maturity level so you always know where it stands: 

* ### seed - raw capture, not yet developed
* ### growing - has been worked on, not yet complete
* ### ready - could become output now
* ### published - has already been turned into something

When you open your vault, filter by #growing to see what needs attention, #ready to see what is waiting to become revenue. 

**Why it makes money:** It makes the gap between "notes I have" and "output I produced" visible. Visible gaps get closed. 

**Setup 8: The Fleeting Note Killer** 

Fleeting notes - the ones you capture in the moment - have a lifespan. After 7 days, a fleeting note that has not been processed is almost certainly noise. 

Set a weekly ritual: every Sunday, open all notes created more than 7 days ago that are still tagged #seed and have not been touched. Delete them or promote them. No middle ground. 

The inbox is not an archive. Notes that are not worth processing are not worth keeping. 

**Why it makes money:** A leaner vault is easier to navigate. Easier to navigate means you actually find the things worth developing. 

**Setup 9: The AI Processing Pass (Local or Cloud)** 

Once a week, open your five most important #growing notes and run this prompt in Claude: 

Here is a note I have been developing: [paste]

Tell me:

1. What is this note actually arguing? 

 (Not what I said it was about - what it actually argues)

2. What is missing that would make this complete?

3. Is this one idea or two ideas that should be separate notes?

4. What would I title this if it became a published piece? 

This diagnostic pass on your best notes surfaces what is ready and what still needs work. 

**Why it makes money:** It accelerates the development cycle of your best ideas from weeks to days. 

**Setup 10: The Connection Finder Template** 

When you are working on a note and want to surface connections to other ideas in your vault, use this prompt structure: 

Here is Note A: [paste]

Here is Note B: [paste]

What is the conceptual thread that connects these?

Is there a third idea that would bridge them?

What would the title of that bridge note be? 

Create the bridge note. Link all three. Your vault's knowledge graph just got denser. 

**Why it makes money:** Content that draws unexpected connections between ideas performs significantly better than content that covers familiar ground in familiar ways. 

#### CATEGORY 3: OUTPUT SETUPS

*Where notes become things people pay for.* 

**Setup 11: The Content Assembly Line**

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HKSPj2MaEAAkp9A.jpg) 

Create a folder called 🏭 Content Pipeline with four subfolders: 

* 1 - Ideas (your #ready notes live here)
* 2 - Drafts (actively being written)
* 3 - Review (finished, awaiting final check)
* 4 - Published (done and distributed)

Move each piece of content through the pipeline. Never work on something without knowing exactly where it lives in the process. 

**Why it makes money:** Visual clarity on what is in progress prevents the "I have a hundred ideas but nothing gets published" problem. 

**Setup 12: The Evergreen Content Library** 

Create a note called 🌲 Evergreen Library - a curated list of your best performing and most timeless content with links to each piece. 

Every time you publish something that performs well, add it here with: 

* Title and link
* The core insight (one sentence)
* Best repurposing angles you have not tried yet

Before you start new content, check this library. Repurposing one great piece into five formats is more efficient than writing five new pieces. 

**Why it makes money:** Your best content is sitting in a folder somewhere earning nothing. This library keeps it in active circulation. 

**Setup 13: The Personal Framework Builder** 

Over time, you develop ways of thinking about problems that are distinctly yours. These are frameworks - and frameworks are the intellectual property that justifies premium pricing. 

Create a folder called 🔧 My Frameworks. When you notice yourself explaining the same concept in the same way repeatedly, capture it: 

### Framework: [Name]

#### What it explains

[The problem or situation it helps with]

#### The model

[The framework itself - steps, components, categories]

#### The key insight

[The non-obvious thing this framework reveals]

#### Examples

[2-3 real instances where this framework applied] 

**Why it makes money:** Frameworks become course modules, consulting methodologies, workshop content, and book chapters. They are the units from which products are built. 

**Setup 14: The "PKM to Product" Pipeline** 

This is the setup that most directly connects Obsidian to revenue. 

Create a note called 💰 Product Ideas structured as: 

### Product Ideas

#### Based on frameworks I already have

* [Framework name] → could become [product type]

#### Based on questions I get asked repeatedly

* "How do you [x]?" → [potential course / guide / template]

#### Based on problems I solved for myself

* I solved [problem] by [approach] → [potential product]

#### Based on content that over-performed

* [Post/article] got unusual engagement →  audience wants more of [topic]

Review this monthly. The next product is almost always already in your vault - waiting to be recognised. 

**Why it makes money:** This is the direct line between your thinking system and your revenue. The question is not "what should I create?" - it is "what have I already figured out that someone would pay to learn?" 

**Setup 15: The Pitch Generator** 

Every note with a #ready tag is a potential pitch. When you are ready to monetise an idea, open the note and run: 

Here is an idea I have been developing: [paste note]

Write three pitch versions for this concept:

1. A cold email pitch (5 sentences, specific client application)

2. A social media hook (first two lines that stop a scroll)

3. A product description (what it is, who it is for, 

 what they get, what changes for them)

My audience: [describe your audience]

My positioning: [how you are known/positioned] 

**Why it makes money:** A developed idea with no pitch attached is still invisible. This closes the last gap between thinking and revenue.

**Why it makes money:** Social proof is a direct revenue driver and most service providers leave it scattered across their inbox.

**Setup 23: The Question Bank** 

Create a note called ❓ Questions I Get Asked. Every time someone asks you the same question twice - in a client call, a DM, a comment, an email - add it here. 

When your question bank hits 20 entries, you have the content calendar for the next three months. When it hits 50, you have the outline for a course or book. 

**Why it makes money:** Repeated questions are demand signals. Demand signals are business intelligence. 

**Setup 24: The Failure Archive**

**Why it makes money:** Your failure archive is your most differentiated content. "Here is what everyone tells you to do and why it didn't work for me" is more valuable to most audiences than "here is what you should do." 

#### CATEGORY 6: AUTOMATION AND AI SETUPS

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HKSQYXFbUAAhL8u.jpg) 

*For multiplying the output of your vault without multiplying the time you put in.* 

**Setup 25: The AI Thinking Partner Conversation** 

This is the setup I use most. Inside a Claude Project with your vault's key notes uploaded as knowledge, keep a running conversation called 🤖 Thinking Partner: 

Never start a new conversation here - continue the same one. Over weeks, it accumulates context about your thinking, your projects, and your in-progress ideas. The longer the conversation, the more useful Claude becomes as a collaborator. 

When you are stuck, paste your note and ask: "What is this note actually trying to say?" 

**Why it makes money:** Ideas develop faster in dialogue than in isolation. This is the dialogue partner that never gets tired of your half-formed thinking. 

**Setup 26: The Automated Weekly Synthesis** 

If you have Claude Pro, set up a Routine that runs every Sunday evening: 

Read the notes I paste below from this week. 

These are my raw working notes.

Give me:

1. The most important insight from this week's notes

2. The idea with the most commercial potential

3. One connection between two ideas I probably haven't noticed

4. The one note most ready to become published content

Notes: [paste your week's best notes] 

**Why it makes money:** The synthesis you might spend an hour doing manually takes 90 seconds with this prompt. 

**Setup 27: The Local AI Privacy Layer** 

For any notes that should never leave your machine - client briefs, sensitive strategy, personal material - connect Ollama to Obsidian via Local LLM Helper. 

Keep two Claude workflows: cloud (via Projects) for non-sensitive work where you want frontier intelligence, local (via Ollama) for anything private. 

Tag sensitive notes with #local-only so you always know which vault to work in. 

**Why it makes money:** Privacy creates the psychological safety to think more openly. Better thinking produces better output. 

**Setup 28: The Content Multiplier Workflow** 

Every time you publish a piece of content, open Obsidian and run this workflow: 

1. Create a note titled [Post Name] - Repurposing Log
2. Paste the original content
3. Run the one-click repurposing prompt (from the 27 Automations article)
4. Save all four repurposed versions in the same note
5. Tag with publish dates as you use each version

One piece of content. Four formats. One vault note tracking all of it. 

**Why it makes money:** Consistent repurposing multiplies the reach of every piece you create without multiplying the creative effort. 

**Setup 29: The Daily Output Log** 

Create a note called 📊 Output Log with this structure: 

#### [Date]

* Published: [link]
* Sent: [email/proposal/pitch]
* Created: [in-progress]
* Ideas developed: [note titles]

Fill it in for 60 seconds at the end of each working day. 

**Why it makes money:** What gets measured gets managed. Seeing a week where "Published: 0" stares back at you is more motivating than any productivity framework.

#### THE ONE SETUP THAT TIES THEM ALL TOGETHER

Every setup in this list is useful individually. But there is one meta-setup that multiplies all of them. 

**Create a note called 🧠 My System** and use it to document your own vault workflow. How you capture. How you process. What your tags mean. How you decide what becomes output. What your weekly ritual looks like. 

Not because you will forget - but because the act of documenting your system forces you to understand it. 

And a system you understand is a system you improve. A system you improve compounds. 

The vault does not make money. Your thinking makes money. 

The vault is the system that captures, develops, and deploys your thinking at a scale and speed you cannot achieve in your head alone
