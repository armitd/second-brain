Cornell notes are simply one of [the best ways to take notes.](https://www.goodnotes.com/blog/how-to-get-good-grades)

Why?

They’re easy to create, help you understand material better as you take them, and are incredibly easy to use to review.

Here’s how you can start taking Cornell notes too.

### What are Cornell Notes?

Cornell note-taking is a popular note-taking method for taking, organizing, and summarizing notes. This method typically results in better overall comprehension.

This note-taking method was invented by Professor Walter Paul of Cornell University in the 1950s.

### **How to Take Cornell Notes**

It requires very little preparation which makes it ideal for note-taking in class. The page is divided into 4 — or sometimes only 3 — different sections:

1. One block at the top of the page
2. Two columns
3. One block at the bottom of the page

![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/622072bb15578473cfe8ef5a/66a0ce8b872d465d063e2ee2_IMG_0069.jpg)
Taking Cornell notes is straightforward: all actual notes from the lecture go into the main note-taking column.

The smaller column on the left side is for keywords and questions — you can think of this section for recording hints and prompts about the material.

At the bottom of the page, write a brief summary of the content on the page.

![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/622072bb15578473cfe8ef5a/66a0ce99e47e489d108664fd_IMG_0070.jpg)
### **Why Is Cornell Note-Taking So Effective?**

* **Active summarizing.** By actively summarizing a topic, you’ll gain a better understanding of your class notes
* **Intentional note-taking.** Cornell notes encourage focus and intentionality when taking notes. Rather than writing everything down, you have to write key points and often reflect on the material
* **Revision-ready notes.** When it comes time to revisit your notes, they’re formatted well for revision: you can use the questions to test your knowledge, summaries to refresh your memory, or your main notes for necessary detail

To learn more, read our article on [science-backed study techniques](https://www.goodnotes.com/blog/how-to-study-tips), curated by a Harvard student.

### Tips to Take Better Cornell Notes

1. Use the left column to write **questions** that you can use to quiz yourself on when revising. (See our article on [active recall!](https://www.goodnotes.com/blog/active-recall-studying))
2. Sometimes you may reach the end of a piece of paper, before you’re actually finished taking your notes. Even if the notes aren’t complete, **don’t forgo the summary**. Write the summary based on the existing points on the page.
3. Cornell notes are written to be easy to review. So make sure to review them!
