#### Top 10 Emerging Consulting Industry Trends [2026]

##### 1. Digital Transformation & Automation: McKinsey’s Lilli Reclaims 30% of Research Time

The digital transformation consulting market is projected to grow from [USD 268.46 billion](https://www.marketresearchfuture.com/reports/digital-transformation-consulting-market-22794) in 2025 to [USD 510.50 billion](https://www.marketresearchfuture.com/reports/digital-transformation-consulting-market-22794) in 2034, exhibiting a CAGR of [7.4%](https://www.marketresearchfuture.com/reports/digital-transformation-consulting-market-22794). According to Forbes, [94%](https://forbytes.com/blog/top-most-impressive-cases-when-technology-helped-businesses/) of firms worldwide are already making the shift toward digital transformation.

![Digital_Transformation_Consulting_Market](https://www.startus-insights.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Digital_Transformation_Consulting_Market_1.webp)Credit: [Market Research Future](https://www.marketresearchfuture.com/reports/digital-transformation-consulting-market-22794)
Consulting firms offer digital transformation strategies to clients and also embed AI-driven automation within their own operations. This dual approach strengthens internal productivity and expands the value of client-facing services.

Moreover, PwC’s 2025 CEO survey found that [56%](https://www.pwc.com/bm/en/press-releases/pwc-2025-global-ceo-survey.html) of CEOs reported efficiency gains from generative AI adoption, with [34%](https://www.pwc.com/bm/en/press-releases/pwc-2025-global-ceo-survey.html) noting profitability improvements and [32%](https://www.pwc.com/bm/en/press-releases/pwc-2025-global-ceo-survey.html) seeing revenue increases. Post-pandemic resilience drives growth, as businesses expand automation efforts and also increase Internet of Things (IoT) budgets to ensure continuity and agility.

Consulting businesses translate these pressures into major client outcomes. They enable faster processes and cost savings through automation strategies. McKinsey projects that generative technologies are capable of providing [USD 2.6-4.4 trillion](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/mckinsey-digital/our-insights/the-economic-potential-of-generative-ai-the-next-productivity-frontier) in annual productivity gains.

Another notable case is McKinsey’s in-house platform *[Lilli,](https://www.mckinsey.com/about-us/new-at-mckinsey-blog/meet-lilli-our-generative-ai-tool)* which reclaimed [30%](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/mckinsey-digital/how-we-help-clients/rewiring-the-way-mckinsey-works-with-lilli) of consultants’ research time and allowed the firm to shift one-quarter of its engagements toward outcome-based delivery.

Consulting firms deploy advanced technologies to drive this change. They apply AI to offer predictive analytics and workflow augmentation. For example, [EY](https://www.ey.com/en_gl) invested in its [EY.ai Agentic Platform](https://www.ey.com/en_gl/newsroom/2025/03/ey-launching-ey-ai-agentic-platform-created-with-nvidia-ai-to-drive-multi-sector-transformation-starting-with-tax-risk-and-finance-domains), developed with [Nvidia](https://www.nvidia.com/en-in/), to embed digital agents in tax, risk, and finance functions.

Robotic process automation (RPA) complements this push, with the global RPA market projected to grow to [USD 30.85 billion](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/robotic-process-automation-rpa-market) by 2030. For instance, a rapidly growing bank in India, where the loan disbursement process was sped up by [78%](https://nividous.com/resources/case-studies/ibm-business-automation-workflow-speeds-loan-disbursement-process/) using [IBM](https://www.ibm.com/in-en) business automation workflow solutions.

Cloud infrastructure also serves as a core enabler of consulting-led digital transformation. This solution allows firms to guide clients through rapid migrations, achieve near-zero downtime, and lower operating costs. Accenture’s 2024 [acquisition](https://newsroom.accenture.com/news/2024/accenture-completes-acquisition-of-intellera-consulting) of [Intellera Consulting](https://www.intelleraconsulting.com/), for example, added [1400](https://newsroom.accenture.com/news/2024/accenture-completes-acquisition-of-intellera-consulting) experts to support Italy’s public sector. This move enabled near-zero downtime migrations and reduced operating costs.

Also, consultants extend digital transformation into real-time operations through connected devices and edge systems. Deloitte, for instance, integrates [IoT-based digital twins and hazard detection](https://www.deloitte.com/global/en/about/press-room/ai-for-infrastructure-resilience.html) into client projects to reduce climate-related infrastructure losses by up to [AUD 705 billion](https://www.theaustralian.com.au/business/tech-journal/ai-can-futureproof-infrastructure-against-natural-disasters/news-story/88111708ad28ef2a068bf4cfa83ad7b1) annually.

Moreover, data intelligence tools strengthen consultancy offerings by enabling firms to provide predictive modeling, risk assessment, and real-time decision support for clients. [Impetus Technologies](https://www.impetus.com/), for example, secured over a [USD 350 million](https://www.impetus.com/press-release/kedaara-capital-announces-a-350mn-strategic-investment-in-impetus-technologies-empowering-the-next-chapter-of-our-data-and-ai-journey/) partnership with [Kedaara Capital](https://kedaara.com/) to scale predictive modeling and risk management services.

Additionally, cybersecurity allows consulting firms to integrate zero-trust frameworks, automated compliance monitoring, and fraud detection directly into client transformation programs.

##### 2. ESG & Sustainability Focus: Starbucks Saves USD 60 M Annually via Sustainability Consultation

Regulatory pressure drives rapid growth in ESG and sustainability consulting, as businesses worldwide navigate evolving frameworks such as the EU’s [Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD)](https://finance.ec.europa.eu/capital-markets-union-and-financial-markets/company-reporting-and-auditing/company-reporting/corporate-sustainability-reporting_en) and the [SEC’s climate disclosure requirements](https://www.sec.gov/newsroom/press-releases/2024-31).

Consulting firms step in to guide clients through compliance reporting, risk management, and disclosure frameworks. A 2021 Federated Hermes survey found that [88%](https://www.hermes-investment.com/uk/en/institutions/press/responsible-capitalism-survey/) of institutional investors consider ESG factors more important than traditional financial metrics when evaluating the long-term attractiveness of a company.

The global ESG assets under management were around [USD 30 trillion](https://www.cfainstitute.org/insights/articles/finance-industry-esg-impact) in 2022. This segment is projected to exceed [USD 40 trillion](https://www.cfainstitute.org/insights/articles/finance-industry-esg-impact) by 2030, representing more than [25%](https://www.cfainstitute.org/insights/articles/finance-industry-esg-impact) of the anticipated [USD 140 trillion](https://www.cfainstitute.org/insights/articles/finance-industry-esg-impact) total global AUM at that time. These market forces establish ESG consulting as a critical service for strategy, competitiveness, and long-term corporate resilience.

Companies engaging ESG or sustainability consultants, especially for building retrofits or process upgrades, achieve substantial reductions in resource consumption. For instance, [Starbucks](https://about.starbucks.com/), guided by sustainability consultants and third-party experts, expanded its [Greener Stores program](https://about.starbucks.com/press/2024/starbucks-announces-6000-greener-stores-worldwide/) and reduced energy and water use by [30%](https://about.starbucks.com/press/2024/starbucks-announces-6000-greener-stores-worldwide/) across 6000 verified stores. This initiative saved [USD 60 million](https://about.starbucks.com/press/2024/starbucks-announces-6000-greener-stores-worldwide/) annually. By April 2025, Starbucks had verified [over 9000 Greener Stores](https://about.starbucks.com/stories/2025/starbucks-verifies-over-9000-stores-for-sustainable-practices/) globally.

Consulting firms also allow clients to tap sustainable finance markets, where global green bond issuance reached [USD 700 billion](https://www.bis.org/publ/qtrpdf/r_qt2503d.htm) in 2024. Likewise, McKinsey partnered with [TPG](https://www.tpg.com/) to launch *[Rubicon Carbon](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/sustainability/how-we-help-clients/carbon-management)*, a platform that manages carbon credits and enables enterprises to scale their decarbonization strategies.

Consultancy firms apply AI and ML to automate ESG reporting, validate emissions data, and forecast sustainability outcomes. McKinsey’s *[Catalyst Zero](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/industrials-and-electronics/how-we-help-clients/accelerating-decarbonization-across-the-farming-supply-chain)* platform, for example, allows industrial client [AGCO](https://www.agcocorp.com/int/en/home/) to build automated decarbonization cost curves and design net-zero roadmaps.

Blockchain provides tamper-proof audit trails that enable consultants to verify carbon credits, track supplier compliance, and ensure accurate sustainability reporting.

IoT sensors expand consulting impact by enabling real-time monitoring of energy, water, and waste. Siemens, for instance, deployed [IoT-driven smart building solutions](https://press.siemens.com/global/en/pressrelease/siemens-launches-connect-box-smart-iot-solution-manage-smaller-buildings) that minimize energy consumption across commercial properties.

Also, digital twin technologies allow consultants to model climate scenarios, energy transition pathways, and resource optimization strategies before implementation. A notable case is Unilever’s collaboration with digital consultants to apply [AI and digital twin modeling](https://www.cio.inc/unilevers-ai-push-from-shop-floor-to-culture-core-part-1-a-29133) in supply chain logistics. This collaboration ensures cleaner sourcing practices and renewable energy use across operations.

Partnerships and acquisitions strengthen consulting firms’ ESG capabilities. Accenture invested [USD 6.6 billion](https://investor.accenture.com/~/media/Files/A/Accenture-IR-V3/home/accenture-fiscal-2024-annual-report.pdf) across 46 strategic acquisitions in fiscal 2024, with sustainability services as a central focus. These acquisitions allow the firm to expand advisory capacity, integrate specialized ESG expertise, and provide end-to-end sustainability consulting solutions for its clients.

##### 3. Personalized Client Services: Personalization Offers 10–25% Revenue Lift

According to Twilio Segment’s State of Personalization Report 2024, [89%](https://segment.com/state-of-personalization-report/) of marketing decision-makers believe personalization is crucial to their business’s success over the next three years. Consulting businesses respond by replacing standardized methodologies with adaptive, data-driven engagements that directly address client contexts.

Organizations excelling at personalization typically see a [10-15%](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/growth-marketing-and-sales/our-insights/the-value-of-getting-personalization-right-or-wrong-is-multiplying) revenue lift and, in some cases, up to [25%](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/growth-marketing-and-sales/our-insights/the-value-of-getting-personalization-right-or-wrong-is-multiplying). Leading, fast-growing companies derive about [40%](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/growth-marketing-and-sales/our-insights/the-value-of-getting-personalization-right-or-wrong-is-multiplying) more of their revenue from personalization efforts compared to their peers.

These results translate into enhanced client retention, premium pricing opportunities, and stronger market positioning. For example, Deloitte partnered with [Daimler Trucks Asia](https://asia.daimlertruck.com/en/) to implement [cognitive quality management solutions](https://www.deloitte.com/global/en/services/consulting/case-studies/daimler-truck-manufacturing.html) that predicted and addressed issues using tailored data insights. This instance demonstrates how personalization improves operational outcomes and client trust.

Cloud computing and data platforms give consultants the infrastructure to unify data across touchpoints. Accenture’s [cloud alliances](https://newsroom.accenture.com/news/2024/accenture-and-google-cloud-advance-ai-adoption-and-cybersecurity-with-fortune-500-companies) with Microsoft and Google illustrate how businesses allow clients to integrate personalization globally and ensure governance and security.

Accenture also continues investing in cloud and AI partnerships to operationalize personalization at a global scale. This reflects how large consulting businesses embed personalization into digital innovation programs.

Data analytics and business intelligence allow consultants to convert raw data into actionable insights. For instance, [BCG X](https://www.bcg.com/) partnered with [Rio Tinto](https://www.riotinto.com/) to build an [AI-driven scheduling platform](https://www.bcg.com/x/mark-your-moment/how-an-iron-ore-producer-modernized-mining-operations-with-ai) that replaces outdated systems and automates complex decisions across mining, rail, and port operations. The solution, tailored for a team of fifty schedulers, more than doubled their productivity and delivered rapid ROI.

Customer relationship management (CRM) and client experience platforms increase consulting impact by centralizing client histories and interaction records. [PwC’s collaboration with Salesforce](https://www.pwc.com/us/en/technology/alliances/salesforce/generative-ai/contextual-personalization-at-scale.html) highlights how consultants enable consistent, context-aware personalization across channels.

Likewise, IoT and real-time data collection extend personalization into physical operations. Consulting firms use these insights to tailor maintenance schedules, optimize resource allocation, and adapt solutions to each client’s operational needs.

According to Management Consulted, job postings for top consulting firms, including the Big Four, fell by [over 50%](https://managementconsulted.com/consultant-hiring-delayed-2023/) year-over-year. The economic strain is compounded by post-pandemic overcapacity.

During the crisis years, consultancies expanded to meet surging digital innovation demand. Yet as attrition rates dropped to historic lows, many firms were left with excess staffing levels that could not be sustained.

Alongside these structural corrections, technological disruption accelerates the workforce reduction trend. The rapid rise of AI, hyperautomation, and intelligent process automation (IPA) took over many tasks that were once performed by junior consultants. These include market research and compliance reporting.

According to a McKinsey State of AI survey, [71%](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai) of organizations report that their enterprises regularly use generative AI in at least one business function, up from [65%](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai) in early 2024. These require fewer human resources.

Despite these pressures, workforce restructuring delivers notable benefits. By freeing up resources for technology adoption, businesses improve profitability. They also maintain service quality. Service innovation advances as automation augments delivery. For example, NLP tools analyze documents in minutes. They convert research-heavy processes into near-instant insights.

Similarly, AI-powered chatbots provide round-the-clock client support. This enables value delivery even as headcount contracts.

The rapid changes are evident across leading firms. McKinsey eliminated [5000 roles](https://cxomedia.in/2025/05/28/mckinsey-lays-off-5000-employees-in-global-workforce-overhaul/) between 2023 and 2025. It began with [1400 back-office positions](https://cxomedia.in/2025/05/28/mckinsey-lays-off-5000-employees-in-global-workforce-overhaul/), yet continues to hire specialists in AI and digital innovation.

Moreover, Accenture described its 2025 restructuring as rewiring five decades of operating models. It embedded [AI throughout service delivery](https://hr.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/industry/accenture-to-promote-15000-employees-in-india-and-50000-globally-in-june-2025/121350169) and simultaneously promoted [50K](https://hr.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/industry/accenture-to-promote-15000-employees-in-india-and-50000-globally-in-june-2025/121350169) employees worldwide.

Cloud computing reduces overhead and supports remote collaboration. [IBM Consulting](https://www.ibm.com/consulting) illustrates this by using [Microsoft Azure OpenAI Service](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/products/ai-foundry/models/openai) to [automate procurement workflows](https://newsroom.ibm.com/2023-08-17-IBM-Consulting-Collaborates-with-Microsoft-to-Help-Companies-Accelerate-Adoption-of-Generative-AI) for enterprise clients.

RPA aligns repetitive tasks like data entry and compliance checks. AI and ML enhance predictive analytics and automate research-heavy activities. For example, Accenture’s [AI-integrated talent platform](https://eightfold.ai/customers/customer-stories/ernst-young-ey-makes-skills-based-work-a-reality-with-ai/) through [eightfold.ai](https://eightfold.ai/) tripled client productivity and cut employee turnover by [26%](https://eightfold.ai/customers/customer-stories/ernst-young-ey-makes-skills-based-work-a-reality-with-ai/). This shows how AI-powered skills mapping strengthens recruitment and workforce retention.

###### **SUMMON enables Staff-less Hotel Management**

The *SUMMON* system also oversees recruitment, training, shift planning, accounting, reporting, and facility management. It consolidates tasks that traditionally rely on large teams.

By digitizing hotel operations, *SUMMON* lowers personnel costs and addresses chronic staffing shortages. It also maintains consistent hospitality standards.
