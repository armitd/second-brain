Today, Google unveiled a new ChatGPT-like chatbot named [Bard](https://www.cnn.com/2023/02/06/tech/google-bard-chatgpt-rival/index.html), as the company races to catch up in the wake of ChatGPT’s massive viral success (growing [faster](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/chatgpt-chatbot-tiktok-ai-artificial-intelligence/#:~:text=Currently%2C%20ChatGPT%20averages%2013%20million,a%20day%2C%20according%20to%20UBS.) than TikTok, apparently). In a [blog post](https://blog.google/technology/ai/bard-google-ai-search-updates/), CEO Sundar Pichai said Bard is now open to “trusted testers,” with plans to make it available to the public “in the coming weeks.”

#### Microsoft to hold in-person Redmond event tomorrow

Meanwhile, just minutes after Google’s announcement, Microsoft shared an announcement that it will be holding an in-person event at its Redmond headquarters tomorrow at 1 pm ET.

![](https://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Screen-Shot-2023-02-06-at-4.54.47-PM.png?resize=1098%2C1336&is-pending-load=1)
Microsoft is expected to unveil its long-awaited integration of [generative AI](https://venturebeat.com/2022/06/17/what-is-generative-artificial-intelligence-ai/) from OpenAI into its search engine Bing — which supposedly will be powered by the brand-new GPT-4.

Those who [spied](https://www.windowscentral.com/microsoft/major-leak-reveals-revolutionary-new-version-of-microsoft-bing-powered-by-chatgpt-4-ai) screenshots of the new integration noted the presence of a chat box rather than a search bar.
