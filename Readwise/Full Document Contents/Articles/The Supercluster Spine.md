#####  Roland Harwood 🌍

######  Compulsive Connector | Climate Tech | Ethical AI | Smart Cities | Physicist | Failed Astronaut | Piano Player | Dad of 3 | TCK\* | On The Edge

Last week I attended five major conferences in London and Paris - [London Tech Week](https://londontechweek.com/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block), [London Design Biennale](https://uk.linkedin.com/company/london-design-biennale?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention)  , [Viva Technology](https://vivatechnology.com/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block), [NVIDIA’s GTC](https://www.nvidia.com/en-eu/gtc/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) and the [Smart Data Forum](https://www.smartdataforum.org/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) - which gave me a chance to compare and contrast two of Europe’s most vibrant innovation ecosystems. 

It was a bit of a whirlwind, and pretty exhausting to be honest, yet the energy across both cities was palpable. Yet despite their proximity (at just over 2 hours apart by train) are London and Paris competing too much for attention and resources?

####  Better Together

According to the recent [Dealroom 2025 Global Tech Ecosystem Index](https://dealroom.co/tech-ecosystem-index-2025?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block), Paris has recently overtaken London as Europe’s leading tech ecosystem by startup enterprise value growth - surging 5.3× since 2017 versus London’s 4.2× - while London continues to lead in funding, attracting $11.3 billion last year compared to Paris’s $7.8 billion. 

However this report also triggered a wave of articles saying that [Paris named as Europe’s leading tech ecosystem](https://www.reuters.com/technology/paris-named-europes-leading-tech-ecosystem-beating-london-2025-05-21/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) - framing it as a zero-sum win. At the same time the recent [Tech Nation 2025 report](https://report.technation.io/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) demonstrates that UK tech sector has reached a combined market valuation of $1.2 trillion in 2025, reaffirming its position as the number one tech ecosystem in Europe.

> “The UK has the 3rd largest AI ecosystem in the world (after the US and China), supports over 2 million jobs and grew 30x faster than the rest of the economy last year.” [Poppy Gustafsson](https://www.linkedin.com/in/poppy-gustafsson-41464ba0/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block)

This dynamic competition between Paris and London is a prime example of how rival hubs push each other to innovate faster and scale smarter through collaboration across adjacent fields as [Laura Citron OBE](https://www.linkedin.com/article/edit/7315650583695118336/#?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) has said, articulating clearly how this kind of zero-sum thinking is based on a series [myths that needs busting](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7336312912413708288/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block).

Technology Clusters grow not only through competition but through collaboration across adjacent fields and places. London’s deep expertise and Paris’s robust ecosystem illustrate this beautifully. And the tech clusters around these two global cities aren’t silos - they’re ecosystems nourished by close connection, competition, and collaboration. 

Both cities lead in AI talent, repeat founders, and deep tech, consistently pushing each other to rise. This rivalry drives talent, funding, and innovation flows across borders - boosting both ecosystems as they aim higher. 

So it’s no coincidence that London Tech Week and Viva Tech occur in the same week with many people I met travelling between the two. The rivalry and partnership between Paris and London isn’t just good - it fuels and strengthens both. 

####  Two Unstoppable Forces

At all of these events I witnessed lively debates around whether AI and Climate Tech are competing for resources and attention as well? The only conclusion I could draw is that they are both increasingly intertwined - not as competing sectors but complementary forces accelerating innovation across all industries.

> “This is an extraordinary moment. AI is a great equaliser. The new programming language is ‘human’ - just ask nicely! And it’s growing at 1,000,000x every decade so you just need to get on this rocket ship.” [Jensen Huang](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jenhsunhuang/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) 🚀

In both Paris and London, this synergy is evident. London’s unmatched blend of talent, policy, investment, and creative tech sets a gold standard (DeepMind ’s AI Lab, AI Sector Deal, ARIA etc). Paris, leveraging strong government support and infrastructure (Station F, AI Action Summit, La French Tech etc) has rapidly scaled its attractiveness.

####  Compulsively Connecting

As Community Lead for [Project Hafnia](https://hafnia.milestonesys.com/joinwaitlist?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) - a cutting-edge AI model training initiative by [Milestone Systems](https://dk.linkedin.com/company/milestone-systems?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention) in partnership with [NVIDIA](https://www.linkedin.com/company/nvidia?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention) - I’ve seen first-hand how AI model training based on real world data can drive new innovations across cities. For instance [our recent partnerships with the city of Genoa](https://www.milestonesys.com/company/news/press-releases/project-hafnia-urban-infrastructure-genoa/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) in Italy, and a successful collaboration with [Dubuque in Iowa](https://cities-today.com/industry/milestones-video-technology-powers-next-gen-ai-for-smart-cities/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block), are a testament to this cross-border, cross-sector collaboration, driving scalable impact.

As Co-Founder of the [Climate Tech Supercluster](https://www.climatetechsupercluster.com/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block), a collaboration between [Cambridge Cleantech](https://uk.linkedin.com/company/cambridge-cleantech?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention) , [Oxfordshire Greentech](https://uk.linkedin.com/company/oxfordshire-greentech?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention) and [Liminal | B Corp™](https://uk.linkedin.com/company/we-are-liminal?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention) , we are working hard to unite diverse stakeholders across 5 countries and 4 sectors fostering collaboration that accelerates innovation in decarbonisation and climate resilience. By bridging startups, corporates, and governments, we create a dynamic ecosystem where healthy rivalry and shared ambition fuel Europe’s leadership in climate technology.

As Co-Convener of the [Innovate UK Net Zero Living Programme](https://www.linkedin.com/showcase/netzeroliving/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention) programme we are working with [Urban Foresight](https://uk.linkedin.com/company/urban-foresight?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention) and [Forum for the Future](https://uk.linkedin.com/company/forum-for-the-future_2?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention) to help to coordinate efforts across 59 towns and cities across the UK into a living laboratories for sustainability innovation. This test bed enables real-world piloting of new solutions, from smart energy systems to circular economy models, ensuring that groundbreaking ideas translate into practical, scalable impact for cities striving to meet their net zero goals.

####  The New Palo Alto

The Big Tech companies from Palo Alto were mostly not visible this week (with the notable exception of [NVIDIA](https://www.linkedin.com/company/nvidia?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention) in Paris) and I think that was both a deliberate choice and sign that things are changing. With uncertainty increasingly clouding the US technology landscape in particular - from political turbulence to tightening regulations - more entrepreneurs, academics and investors are turning their gaze more seriously to Europe which is quietly growing in confidence. 

What they’re finding is a new global centre of gravity: a dense, collaborative corridor of innovation stretching from London to Paris, as described by  [Saul Klein](https://uk.linkedin.com/in/saulklein?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention)  , co-founder of [LocalGlobe](https://uk.linkedin.com/company/localglobe?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention) , as the spine of [the New Palo Alto](https://dealroom.co/guides/npa?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) which also encompass the major innovation hubs of Oxford, Cambridge, Manchester, Edinburgh, Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Brussels, Cologne and many more. 

> "As the tech industry faces increased scrutiny, we have an opportunity to offer an alternative model of innovation. By building thoroughbreds to be sustainable, transparent companies, we can begin the work of sharing the benefits of innovation more equally."  [Saul Klein](https://uk.linkedin.com/in/saulklein?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention)  

Just as some of the most iconic US cities take their names from the ancient cities of Europe - New York and New Orleans - [New Palo Alto pays homage to its namesake while signalling a new path forward](https://www.wired.com/story/europe-is-the-new-palo-alto/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block).

When climate ambition meets AI excellence, and when cities like Paris and London drive a shared, scalable future, you get something bigger than the sum of its parts. If you're looking for the next wave of world-changing tech, it's being built along and around the spine of this global supercluster — all within a short distance of these two great cities.
