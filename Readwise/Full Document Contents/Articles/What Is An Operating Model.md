- Document Note: Background: My manager asked our team to extract practical, actionable steps from McKinsey’s "What Is An Operating Model?" that we can apply to improve how we deliver work and organize capabilities.
   🍽️ Define the critical value streams and align capabilities to them 
   Identify the 3–5 core end-to-end value streams that deliver your highest-priority outcomes, map the required capabilities for each stream, and reorganize teams, roles, and processes around those streams rather than functions.
   🧭 Clarify decision rights, governance, and operating principles 
   Document who decides what, at what level, and how (including escalation paths), set clear operating principles and metrics, and make decision rights visible so teams can act autonomously without rework.
   ⚙️ Build modular capabilities with enabling technology and people practices 
   Modularize capabilities so they can be reused across value streams, invest in the enabling tech/platforms and common processes that make reuse possible, and tie skills, incentives, and operating rhythms to the new model.
   1) Which of the 12 operating-model elements is currently the weakest or most misaligned with our strategy, and what measurable outcomes (clarity, speed, skills, commitment) would improving that element deliver?
   2) What trade-offs would a redesign require (e.g., structure vs. speed, centralized governance vs. local autonomy), and how would those trade-offs affect our ability to respond to disruption and scale value creation?
   3) Do we have the leadership behaviors, talent, footprint, and reward systems needed to sustain the redesign, and what specific investments or changes in people practices are required to make the new model stick?
- Summary: An operating model is the system that turns strategy into results. McKinsey identifies 12 linked elements—like purpose, structure, processes, technology, and talent—that shape that system. Good design and people-focused changes make organizations faster, clearer, and more capable of sustained performance.
