What I believe about how to make money from AI:

1. SaaS pricing used to be kinda boring. But the old playbook doesn't work for AI products.

AI companies can't afford to make pricing a 'future me' decision. The best LLMs are still fairly expensive. Token volume is exploding. And the top 10% of your customers might account for 70-80% of your costs!

And AI startups can't copy the big players. Even giants like Salesforce, OpenAI & Cursor are scrambling to adapt pricing models for AI.

2. Buying models are evolving from "owning" to "hiring". And how you charge impacts how much customers will pay.

Consider how you paid for your last car ride. You might've bought a car (on-prem), leased a car (subscription), rented one (usage-based), called an Uber (outcome-based), or hired a chauffer (service-as-software). The old models don't fully go away. But customers get more options & the market expands.

Each phase unlocked pricing power. We didn't pay less for SaaS; we paid less upfront & more over time if products delivered.

Success-based pricing models can capture 25%+ of the economic value the products create because a) they take on risk & b) they tap into new budgets.

3. There are more flavors of AI pricing than you think. Study them.

And avoid flat fees or seat-based pricing unless you have the margins to support them.

4. The big $$ opportunity is value-based pricing. AI credits are a bridge.

Remember MoviePass? They've been resurrected w/ higher prices & a new credit model. AI companies are doing the exact same thing.

Hybrid pricing models -- usually a mix of per-seat pricing + AI credits -- have exploded from 27% to 41% adoption. These are great for now. But your buyer doesn't want to manage 20+ vendors that all sell credits.

5. Build with pricing agility in mind right now.

Your pricing will need to change. Plan for it.

What that means: a) someone needs to own it, b) you need a modern tech stack w/ the flexibility to adjust models & c) you need to talk to your customers about pricing & value.

---

The TL;DR: the AI boom will be the AI bust unless we can make turn AI products into great AI businesses. Pricing is your path to AI growth.
