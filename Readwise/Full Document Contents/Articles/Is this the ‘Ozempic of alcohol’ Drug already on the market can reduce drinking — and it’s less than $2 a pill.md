Thirst-zepatide?

A cheap pill is being billed as the вҖңOzempic of alcoholвҖқ for its ability to reduce appetites for booze, the way GLP-1 drugs curb food cravings.

Naltrexone вҖ” which costs about $1.60 a pill when purchased in bulk вҖ” decreases the urge for alcohol by blocking opioid receptors in the brain, reducing the dopamine-reward effect that people get from drinking.

Essentially, alcohol no longer has the same satisfying feeling, which helps diminish the desire for it.

Naltrexone has been approved by the Food and Drug Administration to treat alcohol use disorder since 1994 and opiate dependence since 1984.

[Nearly 28 million Americans](https://www.niaaa.nih.gov/alcohols-effects-health/alcohol-topics/alcohol-facts-and-statistics/alcohol-use-disorder-aud-united-states-age-groups-and-demographic-characteristics) struggle with alcohol use disorder.

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention estimates [17% of American adults](https://www.cdc.gov/alcohol/excessive-drinking-data/?CDC_AAref_Val=https://www.cdc.gov/alcohol/data-stats.htm) binge drink, [with the habit more common among men](https://www.cdc.gov/alcohol/fact-sheets/binge-drinking.html). Not only does drinking heavily take a toll on boozersвҖҷ wallets, but it can also lead to negative health consequences and tragedies.

[Research shows](https://www.dailymail.co.uk/health/article-15359383/drinking-pill-Ozempic-alcohol-brakes-bingeing.html) that 80% of users who took naltrexone, a prescription-only med, an hour before drinking drastically reduced or eliminated their alcohol intake altogether.

Nausea and headaches are reported side effects of naltrexone, but experts say the nausea subsides as participants get used to the treatment.

This sets naltrexone apart from acamprosate, which is also approved to treat alcohol use disorder, but necessitates monitoring from a health care professional since potential side effects include suicidal thoughts and depression.

And unlike disulfiram вҖ” another drug approved to treat alcohol use disorder, which causes unpleasant, life-threatening side effects if taken alongside alcohol вҖ” naltrexone doesnвҖҷt require strict alcohol abstinence.
