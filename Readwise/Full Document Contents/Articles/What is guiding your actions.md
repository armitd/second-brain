## Full Document
The north star is the brightest star in the sky and is said to have been used for navigation since the 5th century. Have you considered how you plan your route through life? I was wondering if there is an equivalent to the north star for our life. 5 “v” words came to mind and thought you might find them useful.

1. Values – We all need to find the things that we value in our life. Personal health, family and friendships are values that we should prioritise. However, they are taken for granted whilst we prioritise other activities such as work. Ask yourself if you understand what you value and if they are guiding your actions.
2. Vitality – Maintaining and increasing your energy levels should guide your actions. I am sure we can all do a little more to choose the right foods, adjust your exercise or improve the your sleep quality.
3. Vision – A future vision can be a compelling force to guide our actions. Do something for the first time. Gaining a qualification, learning to drive, or winning a medal are examples.
4. Variety – “Variety is the spice of life” is a proverb from the 18th century. It is as true today as it was then. Look for new things that ignite you curiosity.
5. Virtuosity – Acheiving a level of virtuosity can be the ultimate goal. The path may not be a direct and may require many changes in direction as we learn what viruotisty means to you. The nature of your virtuosity may not become evident at the start of the journey. Focus and determiantion are critical.

What are your thoughts of the 5 “v” that can guide your life? How do they align with what you use to prioritise your activities? Please do share your perspectives.
