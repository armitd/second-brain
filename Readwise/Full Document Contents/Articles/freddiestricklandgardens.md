We have had a few busy days painting the inside of our garden summerhouse . We moved here 6 years ago and it’s more or less been a bit of a dumping ground since. Anyway given we have had such a lovely warm dry spell we made a decision to take everything out (no mean feat as we had hundreds of books) and paint it. It gave us a chance to have a good old sort out too and we managed to take several bags of books and craft items to the charity shops.   

Today has been a day of pottering in the garden and I’m just looking at our shady border and thinking how pretty it looks at this time of the year. I initially used predominantly ferns and hostas but it was all a bit samey and I wanted some flowers, hence the dicentras, tiarellas, and brunnera. In a few weeks there will be thalictrums, astrantias and aquilegias along with lots of lovely foliage plants. I’ve had to get the hose out and give everything a good water but would you believe after the wettest winter for donkeys years, yorkshire water have started putting out radio adverts to use watering cans and recycle veg preparation water! Hey ho here we go again!
