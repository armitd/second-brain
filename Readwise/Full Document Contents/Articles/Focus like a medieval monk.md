You might think that first-millennium monks suffered no distractions as they contemplated and scribed in a candlelit and smartphone-free environment. However, like everyone else, [medieval monks had to use various tactics to concentrate](https://www.wired.co.uk/article/medieval-monks-distraction) on their studies and prayers, a new book explains. Monks would inspire and imitate one another by sharing tales of extreme concentration, like the ascetic Symeon the Stylite, who lived for 37 years on a platform atop a pillar. Such stories helped them remain humble and compassionate when slacking and encouraged them to search for productivity tactics, says Jamie Kreiner, author of *The Wandering Mind: What Medieval Monks Tell Us About Distraction.* [The takeaway for 21st century you?](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/time_the-medieval-monks-guide-to-new-years-resolutions-activity-7017459829220737024-EmSy?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop) Acknowledge and plan for distractions – the new season of a hit show or your kids raising a ruckus – instead of ignoring them.
