##### The report goes onto to conclude

##### *"So what about training?*

***Marketers are participating in training. But too often, it's not helping them improve business results.**  

70% of organizations report their teams participated in B2B marketing training. But **only 30% say their current training is helping them meet their business goals.***

***Managers don't know what skills their team needs to learn.**  

**Only 15% of managers report their training includes an assessment to see which skills their team needs to learn next.*** 

I would also add, that if only 19% of marketers feel they have the skills to meet their future needs, they may not know what training they need.

***Very few organizations are proactive about B2B marketing training.**  

Most B2B organizations do not have a process to decide what training is needed; only 13% have a process in place.* 

***Marketers struggle with B2B training because it's too focused on theory.**  

The biggest frustration marketers have with B2B marketing training is its focus on theory.* 

***There's a gap between the type of training marketers are using and the type they actually want to use.***

**There are three problems with this sort of training**

1. As people move into digital, **knowing and doing are two different things**. What looks easy in the classroom, you get back to your desk and realise you don't understand.

2. This training is about passing on facts, I tell you something and you remember it or you can refer to the workbook. Digital requires us to change, change our mindsets and change our habits, this often requires us to challenge our belief systems, **the answer to this is not [PowerPoint](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_PowerPoint) it's coaching.**

3. People need a safe environment in which to experiment and this means a business needs to give people the time and culture in which to try.

##### What is social selling?

Here at [DLA Ignite](https://dlaignite.com/), we define social selling as

**"Using your presence and behavior on Social Media to build influence,  

make connections, grow relationships and trust, which leads to  

conversation and commercial interaction."**

It's not [witchcraft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Witchcraft), it's **enabling your salespeople to work from [home](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Home), (or the [office](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Office)) and create conversations with prospects and customers. Conversations on social media and conversations that convert.** 

**The problem with the name "social selling" is that people think that this is selling on social.** All these pitches that you get on social are not social selling, they are [spam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spamming).

The other thing you need to know about **social selling** is that this isn't about "putting out some videos" or "putting flowers on your profile" or "going viral" this **is about** [**revenue**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Revenue)**,** [**EBITDA**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earnings_before_interest,_taxes,_depreciation_and_amortization)**, you winning business from the competition and having a competitive advantage.** 

With anything you do on social there are two questions you need to ask

**1. How many** [**leads**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lead_generation) **/ meetings / conversations will I get from this post /** [**blog**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blog) **/ activity?**

**2. How much revenue / EBITDA am I getting as a business?**
