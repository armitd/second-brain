## Full Document
**Paul Michael Newman** [CBE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commander_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire) [FREng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Academy_of_Engineering) (born 1973[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-ingenia-1)) is a British engineer and academic, the BP Professor of Information Engineering at the [University of Oxford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Oxford), and a Fellow of [Keble College, Oxford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keble_College,_Oxford).[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-autogenerated1-2) He is head of the Oxford Mobile Robotics Group (MRG)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-autogenerated1-2) and CTO at [Oxa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxa).

In 2014, he co-founded [Oxa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxa) with [Ingmar Posner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ingmar_Posner).[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-Ori.ox.ac.uk-3)

#### Education

Newman received an MEng in Engineering Science from [Balliol College, Oxford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balliol_College,_Oxford) in 1995, followed by a PhD in autonomous navigation from the [Australian Centre for Field Robotics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australian_Centre_for_Field_Robotics), [University of Sydney](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Sydney), Australia.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-Ori.ox.ac.uk-3)

#### Career and research

Newman’s work on autonomous vehicle technology has led him to author 200 papers and garner over 15,000 citations.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-Ori.ox.ac.uk-3) He has an [h-index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H-index) of 72 according to [Google Scholar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Scholar).[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-4) In his doctoral dissertation at the [University of Sydney](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Sydney), Paul set out the fundamentals of the large-scale navigation problem [SLAM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simultaneous_localization_and_mapping), which would later become one of the most cited papers in the field at over 3,000 citations.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-Ori.ox.ac.uk-3)

Following his PhD in 1999 under [Hugh F. Durrant-Whyte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugh_F._Durrant-Whyte),[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-ingenia-1)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-5) Newman worked as a Navigation Engineer at Sonardyne International, UK, in 1999 and 2000, where he wrote the navigation algorithms which underpinned operation of autonomous sub-sea vehicles dealing with the [Deepwater Horizon oil spill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deepwater_Horizon_oil_spill).[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-6)

In 2000, Newman left industry for [MIT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology), where he was a postdoctorate research scientist, working with Professor [John J. Leonard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_J._Leonard) on large-scale field robotics both on land and in the ocean.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-Ori.ox.ac.uk-3)

He became a Departmental Lecturer in Engineering Science at the [University of Oxford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Oxford) in 2003,[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-Ori.ox.ac.uk-3) and set up the Mobile Robotics Group, a sub-division of the [Oxford Robotics Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxford_Robotics_Institute), where he developed partnerships with [BAE Systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BAE_Systems) and [Nissan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nissan).[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-:0-7) In 2005, he was appointed to a University Lectureship in Information Engineering and elected a fellow of [New College Oxford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_College,_Oxford) where he was a Tutorial Fellow until 2012.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-:1-8) He became Professor of Engineering Science in 2010 and BP Professor of Information Engineering and Fellow of Keble College in 2012.

In 2010, Newman was awarded an [EPSRC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Engineering_and_Physical_Sciences_Research_Council) Leadership Fellowship.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-:1-8) The flagship output was the “Robotcar”, which in 2013 became the first [autonomous vehicle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vehicular_automation) permitted on public roads in the UK.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-:0-7)

He founded the Oxford Robotics Institute in 2016 and served as director until 2022.

In 2014, Newman leveraged the newly developed technology and, with the Robotcar team, co-founded [Oxa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxa).[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-9)

#### Honours, advisory roles and fellowships

Newman was elected a Fellow of the [Royal Academy of Engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Engineering) and Fellow of the [IEEE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IEEE) in 2014, both with citations for "outstanding contributions to robot navigation".[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-Ori.ox.ac.uk-3)

He served on the [UK Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UK_Government)’s [Department for Transport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Department_for_Transport) Scientific Advisory Council from 2016 to 2020.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-Ori.ox.ac.uk-3)

From 2020 Newman has served as a member of the Prime Minister’s Council for Science and Technology.

He was appointed [Commander of the Order of the British Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commander_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire) (CBE) in the [2023 Birthday Honours](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Birthday_Honours) for services to technology and engineering.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Newman_(engineer)#cite_note-10)
