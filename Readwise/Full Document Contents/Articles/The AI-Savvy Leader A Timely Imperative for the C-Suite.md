The global business leadership landscape is undergoing a seismic shift. Artificial intelligence (AI) is no longer a science fiction storyline; it's rapidly weaving itself into the fabric of every industry.

This presents a unique challenge for CEOs and executive teams – mastering AI is not just the next big opportunity, it's the next great leadership challenge. 

Here's why "[The AI-Savvy Leader](https://www.amazon.com/AI-Savvy-Leader-Nine-Ways-Control/dp/1647826233?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block)" by David De Cremer is a must-read for any senior executive navigating this emerging business transformation frontier.

##### Why Now? A Perfect Storm of Change

The urgency for AI literacy stems from several factors. Generative AI (GenAI) tools are rapidly advancing in capability, offering all organizations the potential to reimagine everything from product development to customer service.

Yet, without proper guidance, these powerful tools can lead to ethical quagmires and missed opportunities. De Cremer emphasizes that business purpose, not just technological prowess, must be the north star guiding AI implementation.

##### Human-Plus-AI: A Collaborative Future

The book debunks the myth of AI as a job-stealing menace. Instead, De Cremer advocates for human-plus-AI collaborations, where human expertise and intuition are amplified by AI's data-crunching abilities.

This approach fosters a more inclusive work environment, leveraging the strengths of both humans and machines.

##### Communication is Key: Leading the Change

De Cremer highlights the crucial role of superior communication skills in driving AI adoption. Leaders must effectively articulate the vision, assuage anxieties, and build trust with employees at all levels.

After all, successful AI integration hinges on a willing and engaged workforce.

##### The Strategic Visionary: Beyond the Tech

The most AI-savvy leaders are not just tech enthusiasts; they are strategic visionaries. De Cremer emphasizes the importance of considering all stakeholders – employees, customers, and society as a whole – when deploying AI solutions.

This holistic mindset ensures responsible and sustainable AI practices.

##### Human-Centered Design: Beyond Automation

The book champions a human-centered approach to AI adoption. De Cremer argues for employee augmentation, where AI assists humans in performing tasks, rather than simply automating them.

This frees up people's innate potential for higher-order thinking and innovation, driving the development of more strategic AI use cases.

##### The Soft Skills Imperative

De Cremer reminds us that AI is not just about the technology; it's about people. 

Leaders must hone their soft skills – empathy, persuasion, and active listening – to navigate the change management storm that inevitably accompanies AI integration.

##### A Practical Roadmap for AI Innovation

"The AI-Savvy Leader" is a timely and thought-provoking read, offering a clear roadmap for senior executives navigating the uncharted territory of AI.

By prioritizing human-centered design, fostering human-plus-AI collaboration, and wielding clear communication as a leadership weapon, CEOs can ensure their organizations not only survive but thrive in this era of intelligent machines. 

As [Harvard Business Review](https://hbr.org/2024/05/for-success-with-ai-bring-everyone-on-board?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) aptly states, for success with AI, "bringing everyone on board" is paramount. De Cremer's book provides the essential insights to make that happen.

##### Polymathic Leadership: How to Unlock AI's Potential

I believe Generative AI tools require a new kind of leadership charter: for the [Polymathic Leader](https://www.geoactivegroup.com/about-us/polymathic-leadership?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block). As outlined in my framework, these savvy leaders possess a blend of skills aligned with the themes in David De Cremer's book.

I hypothesize that by fostering Polymathic Leadership development alongside strategic AI implementation, organizations can unlock the full potential of their GenAI investments. Here's how:

* Alignment with Business Purpose: Polymathic leaders, with their inherent ability to connect seemingly disparate concepts, excel at aligning AI initiatives with the organization's core purpose. This ensures AI serves as a strategic tool, not just a technological marvel, as emphasized by De Cremer.
* Human-Plus-AI Collaboration: Polymathic leaders, with their diverse skill sets, are natural bridge-builders between Technical and Business teams. This fosters the collaborative spirit De Cremer champions, maximizing the combined power of human and GenAI capabilities.
* Superior Communication and Change Management: Polymathic leaders, with their grasp of the bigger picture, can effectively communicate the vision and benefits of GenAI to all stakeholders. This aligns with De Cremer's emphasis on clear narratives as a critical driver of AI adoption.
* Strategic Vision with a Human Touch: Polymathic leaders, with their well-rounded perspective, consider the human element alongside the technological. This aligns with De Cremer's call for responsible AI leadership that factors in the impact on employees, customers, and society as a whole.

In essence, Polymathic Leadership provides the skills criteria for De Cremer's AI-Savvy Leader vision. By cultivating these leaders, organizations can navigate the complexities of GenAI integration while maximizing its positive impact.

I'm an advocate for the change management agenda De Cremer underscores. By prioritizing [Polymathic Leadership](https://www.geoactivegroup.com/about-us/polymathic-leadership?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) alongside strategic AI investments, organizations can ensure a smooth transition, gain employee buy-in, and ultimately, a successful journey towards their desired business outcomes.
