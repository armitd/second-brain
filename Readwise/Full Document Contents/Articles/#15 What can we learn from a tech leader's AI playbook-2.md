### #15 What can we learn from a tech leader's AI playbook?

##### It may feel different but maybe it's just digital transformation

Hello,

It's been a month since I last sent out a newsletter. Because I've launched an AI unicorn? Unfortunately not (yet). But I have been diligently conducting market research to refine my AI-related proposition for launch. I've been engaging with a cross-section of business leaders across functions, sizes, and sectors. My first insight is their eagerness to share progress and challenges, which has led to surprising access to diaries.

A similar tone was evident at this week's AI Safety Summit in the UK, where multiple countries convened to address critical issues. Initially, I viewed the event as potentially politically motivated. However, the [feedback](https://twitter.com/soundboy/status/1720118410040737940?s=46&t=DnZGwH6Ch6vKUPZx7wlS5A) from the two-day event has been positive: significant global participation, robust debate, and a consensus on collaboration, with future sessions planned in South Korea and France. Oh, and Elon Musk was there, though his views on AI seem as changeable as the weather…[welcome Grok](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/nov/05/elon-musk-unveils-grok-an-ai-chatbot-with-a-rebellious-streak)!

In the spirit of sharing, one conversation I had with a senior digital leader at a global private equity firm was particularly enlightening. They recognised the value creation potential of AI early on and have not been playing catch-up. The discussion had an 'AI adoption playbook' feel to it, providing valuable insights for anyone at any stage of their AI journey. Here are the key takeaways.

**Setting the Stage**

The firm didn't stumble upon AI; they charted a deliberate course towards it, identifying companies with 'game-changer' potential. Their strategic focus on technology was underpinned by engaging external experts at an early stage, encouraging a curious and innovative culture. Whilst advancements in Generative AI are relatively new, they have been able to take advantage sooner down to preparing their people and systems.

*“Digital transformation is all about having the foresight and the strategy. It's about orchestrating change and it's about the resources that you are using. If you're not developing those at a fast enough pace, you're going to be held back.*

**Being Prepared**

Adopting AI comes with its hurdles. For this firm, it's about discerning where, what, and when to invest. They are highly confident in their strategy for areas with clear use cases. However, in less clear domains, determining the extent and pace of investment is challenging. Investments must be justified—similar tech advances, such as augmented reality, have proved un-profitable investments.

They are keenly aware that the AI landscape is ever-changing and staying ahead requires a culture of constant learning and adaptability. They regularly assess whether their businesses might be disrupted and ensure they are tuned into the strategic benefits of technology to deliver competitive value.

The company maintains a 'continuous preparation' approach with tailored training sessions, encouraging cross-portfolio learning and ongoing board-level discussions to make timely investments. From a more day to day perspective it’s about staying agile, looking at business tasks from start to finish, finding opportunities to implement technologies.

*"How swiftly you adopt new technology equates to how quickly you acquire new knowledge. It's inherently challenging. We're not experts, nor are we perfect; we're always learning and surrounding ourselves with experts to truly grasp it."*

*"In any digital transformation, having the right skills at every organisational level is crucial—think of it as reinforcing your 'digital spine'. You must be strong throughout."*

**A Catalyst for Change**

Generative AI may not be the answer to all problems (unless you believe the hype). Yet, this firm leverages it to encourage teams to think differently about their businesses challenges and explore broader technology solutions such as automation and machine learning, which are further along in their evolution and have more readily identifiable use cases.

*"We had a workshop about Generative AI in one of our companies. Although we didn't define any projects specifically for Generative AI, we ended up outlining four AI projects, because it opened our eyes to more immediate opportunities elsewhere."*

Unlike digital transformations gone by, AI is evolving at an unprecedented pace. Whilst being aware of the pace of change, this technology leader saw no reason to approach it any differently to previous transformations like digital marketing 15 years ago. He sees it as exactly the same, they have to be on their feet: know that a business case is on the horizon, prioritise it, identify strength and weaknesses, draw upon expertise, provide training and ensure the management team are engaged.

With AI, the journey is becoming as important as the destination. The conversations and insights shared by others offer a map of sorts. Whether you're taking your first steps or are well on your way, the shared experience from those one or two strides ahead of you will be invaluable.

That's exactly why I've started a series of punchy, 30-minute sessions on Friday’s, designed to cut through the noise and get down to the practical reality of AI. Picture this: every Friday, we come together for a rapid-fire exchange of wisdom, steering clear of the fluff that's all too common elsewhere. We're talking brisk, yet brilliantly honest demos of tools/prompts, behind-the-scenes look at product development, and hands-on workshops that tackle AI use cases head-on.
