#####  Natalie Turner

######  Award Award-winning author of Yes, You Can Innovate. Inventor of The 6 'I's® of Innovation. Keynote Speaker. TEDx Speaker. Founder of Women Who Lead. Executive Advisory for Purpose-Driven Organisations. Fiction Author.

From the acceleration of AI to the visible rollback of progress in women’s rights and diversity, it’s no wonder so many of us feel unsteady. For women in leadership, the path forward can feel both full of possibility and fraught with uncertainty. Lately, I’ve been asking myself: How do we rise — not just in title or responsibility — but in voice, presence, and purpose — in a world that’s constantly shifting?

This week, I had the opportunity to explore that very question with a room of 30 extraordinary women at [Nova School of Business and Economics](https://www.linkedin.com/school/nova-school-of-business-and-economics/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) — senior leaders from 19 of Portugal’s top companies — as they began, in partnership with [CIP - Confederação Empresarial de Portugal](https://pt.linkedin.com/company/cip-confedera-o-empresarial-de-portugal?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-mention) their [Projeto Promova](https://www.linkedin.com/company/projeto-promova/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) leadership journey.

I was invited to open the programme with a keynote: Powerfully You — Leading with Authenticity, Influence and Voice in a Changing World.

Rather than delivering a traditional talk, I created space for dialogue, inviting the women to reflect and share with one another after each theme. Their conversations were honest, energising, and deeply needed. Because so many women are ready to lead in ways that feel more true to who they are.

Here are the three themes we explored together.

##### 1. Authenticity: Power from Within

We’re often taught to associate power with hierarchy and control. But authentic power is different. It’s not about position — it’s about presence. It stems from being rooted in your values and purpose, allowing you to lead with integrity, regardless of the environment.

Many women are conditioned to “fit in” to succeed. To tone it down. To be the “nice girl.” But authentic leadership doesn’t come from fitting in — it comes from standing out with alignment and courage. Authenticity isn’t about being the same everywhere. It’s about being true to yourself — even when it’s uncomfortable. Especially then.

##### 2. Influence: The Power of Whom You Know — and Who Knows You

Traditional leadership, rooted in hierarchy and authority, continues to shape many systems. But there's growing recognition that connection and trust, not just control, are what truly drive change. Influence today isn’t only about titles. It’s about credibility, visibility, and relationships.

And for women, that’s often a natural strength.

We tend to lead through collaboration and community. However, we can also undervalue our influence, feel uncomfortable using it intentionally, or view it as part of our leadership role. Yet our impact — and often our progress — depends on it.

Because in a fast-changing world, our survival exists in our networks. 

It’s not just who you know. It’s who sees you. Who advocates for you when you’re not in the room. Who reminds you of your strength when self-doubt creeps in. Leadership doesn’t have to be lonely. Let’s stop doing it alone.

##### 3. Confidence & Voice: The Courage to Be Heard

Confidence rarely comes first. More often, it follows the act of speaking — even when we don’t feel ready. But many brilliant women still hold back. We wait to be asked. Wait for the perfect moment. Wait until we’re “ready enough.”

The truth is that leadership doesn’t wait. It shows up with clarity, curiosity, and courage. It’s not about being the loudest in the room. It’s about trusting that your voice has value — and using it. Because when you speak from who you truly are, you don’t need to prove yourself. You’re simply being yourself. And that’s where your real power lives.
