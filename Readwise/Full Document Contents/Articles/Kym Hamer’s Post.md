Are you paying attention?

For today's Mindfeed Monday, I've chosen Stolen Focus: Why you can't pay attention, by Johann Hari.

I closed the last page of this book with so many more questions than when I started. In a completely good way, and no doubt that was the author's intention. We have created a world where our attention and focus being hacked at every turn - and when I say we I mean WE, that's me typing this and you reading this and everyone else.

Because what became even clearer to me the more I read was that we have gone with the flow, becoming and raising human doings vs human beings and living in a society of joy-sapping worst case scenarios, FOMO and comparison-itis.

So how can we begin the road back to paying attention? Is it more than intention, discipline, sheer willpower individually? Hari posits yes it is more than those things, it's a collective revolt that will start us on the path to reclaiming our focus and rebuilding a society that can pay attention to what matters to them most.

Riveting, provocative and uncomfortable reading.

I hope you enjoy it.
