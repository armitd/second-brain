I’ve put together some learnings from my use of AI and how I’m applying it to searching for jobs. Alas, this isn’t a “*complete guide to getting your perfect job*”, but hopefully contains a few pointers and a few well crafted prompts to help you.

### Finding jobs

Firstly, not so much about using AI, but I recommend you apply for any job you might be interested in — you don’t need to accept it — any interviews you get are excellent practice — and practice is really important — and if you are lucky enough to be in a position of having more than 1 offer on the table, you’re in an excellent negotiating position.

Don’t be afraid to negotiate hard if you do receive an offer — in the US it’s increasingly common to employ someone to negotiate on your behalf — that’s now available in the UK too — [here](https://www.levels.fyi/services/). One tip is to not disclose your current salary if you can avoid it — it gives you more of a lever to negotiate.

#### Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS)

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) is software used by companies to screen and sort job applications. These are commonly used, so ensure your CV is reasonably parseable to avoid being screened out. There’s lots of information out there about these systems, including sites where you can check your CV — here’s one <https://enhancv.com> (I can’t vouch for its capabilities though)

#### LinkedIn

Upgrade to LinkedIn Premium — it’s easier for recruiters to find and contact you and you get better insight into who’s checked you out — this isn’t a plug for LinkedIn, but just reality.

**Recommendations** — apparently more helpful than I’d thought — [source](https://softwareoasis.com/linkedin-recommendations-impact/) — so by all means recommend each other, but follow the guidelines [here](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/do-linkedin-recommendations-matter-deep-dive-social-proof-emon-grruc/) (Feel free to recommend me!😅)

**Skills** — these help your ‘SEO’ on LinkedIn — the more endorsements, the better — [here](https://nealschaffer.com/linkedin-endorsements/)

#### Network

Your network is probably your best source of job leads. Don’t be afraid to ask for referrals for jobs you see advertised where someone you know works there.

### Using AI

The next few sections are on using AI. The trick is not to get the AI to actually *write* stuff for you — that’s easy to spot nowadays, but use it to give feedback so you can update it in your style.

I once got a job based on my cover letter, so I’d encourage you to always include a cover letter.

Areas covered:

* CV Review Prompt
* Cover Letter Review Prompt
* Hiring Company Analysis
* Prompt for Interview Questions
* Introducing yourself
* Writing a case study

#### Which AI?

They will likely perform similarly, so use whichever AI you have access to. For the cover letter, you’ll want one which can access the internet to read the Job Description (or alternatively, you can download it and upload it to the AI)

**Disclaimer: Hopefully it’s obvious, but these prompts are a starting point and you should customise them to suit your situation**

### CV Review Prompt

This is an optimised prompt. Fill in the blanks and then upload your CV — PDF works best

> *I am seeking a comprehensive review of my attached CV. My goal is to secure a high-impact role in [****Specify your target area(s), e.g., Machine Learning Engineering, Data Science (NLP focused), Senior Data Analytics, AI Research, etc.****] within [****Specify your target industry or type of company, if any, e.g., a fast-paced tech startup, a large financial institution, a healthcare organisation, etc.****].*
> 
> *Please review my CV from the perspective of a hiring manager or a senior technical recruiter in this field. I am looking for both* ***positive feedback*** *(what stands out, what is done well) and* ***constructive criticism*** *(areas for improvement, what might cause concern or lead to rejection).*
> 
> *Specifically, please consider and provide feedback on the following aspects:*
> 
> ***Overall Impact & First Impression:***
> 
> *Does the CV immediately convey a strong candidate for a high-impact role in my target field?*
> 
> *Is it engaging and easy to read? Is the formatting clear and (applicant tracking systems) ATS-friendly?*
> 
> *Is the length appropriate?*
> 
> ***Clarity of Career Narrative & Objective (Implicit or Explicit):***
> 
> *Is it clear what kind of role I am targeting and what my key value proposition is?*
> 
> *Does my experience tell a coherent story of growth and expertise in Data/AI/ML/Analytics?*
> 
> ***Keywords & Technical Skills:***
> 
> *Are the most relevant and current technical skills, programming languages (e.g., Python, R, SQL), libraries/frameworks (e.g., TensorFlow, PyTorch, Scikit-learn, Pandas, Spark), tools (e.g., AWS, Azure, GCP, Docker, Kubernetes, Tableau, Power BI), and methodologies (e.g., Deep Learning, NLP, Reinforcement Learning, Statistical Modeling, A/B testing, MLOps) adequately represented for my target roles?*
> 
> *Are there any critical skills missing or underrepresented given my experience and goals?*
> 
> *Is the skills section effectively presented?*
> 
> ***Quantifiable Achievements & Impact (CRITICAL):***
> 
> *Does my experience section effectively demonstrate the* ***impact*** *of my work using quantifiable metrics (e.g., “Increased model accuracy by X%,” “Reduced data processing time by Y hours,” “Contributed to Z% revenue growth,” “Managed datasets of X size”)?*
> 
> *Are my contributions clearly articulated using strong action verbs?*
> 
> *Where could I better highlight or quantify my achievements to showcase business value or technical innovation?*
> 
> ***Project Portfolio (if applicable, or integrated into experience):***
> 
> *Are my projects relevant and impressive for my target roles?*
> 
> *Do I clearly explain the problem, the technologies/methodologies used, my specific role, and the outcome/impact of each project?*
> 
> *Could the project descriptions be improved for clarity or impact?*
> 
> ***Experience Section:***
> 
> *Is my work experience described in a way that is compelling and results-oriented, rather than just a list of duties?*
> 
> *Is the progression of my career clear?*
> 
> *Is there a good balance between technical detail and business context?*
> 
> ***Education & Certifications:***
> 
> *Is my educational background and any relevant certifications presented effectively?*
> 
> *Are these aligned with the expectations for high-impact roles in this field?*
> 
> ***Potential Red Flags or Weaknesses:***
> 
> *Are there any aspects of my CV that might raise concerns for a hiring manager (e.g., gaps in employment (if so, how to address), lack of specific experience, unclear contributions, too much jargon, or not enough technical depth)?*
> 
> *What are the weakest parts of my CV, and how can I strengthen them?*
> 
> ***Tailoring for High-Impact Roles:***
> 
> *Does the CV effectively position me for high-impact roles, suggesting leadership, innovation, significant contributions, and the ability to solve complex problems?*
> 
> *What could I add or change to better convey my suitability for such roles?*
> 
> ***Overall Readability and Professionalism:***
> 
> *Are there any grammatical errors, typos, or inconsistencies?*
> 
> *Is the language professional and confident?*
> 
> *Please be as candid and specific as possible in your feedback. Examples of how to rephrase certain points or suggestions for additional content would be highly appreciated. My aim is to make this CV as compelling as possible.*
> 
> *Thank you for your time and expertise!*
> 
> 

How to Use This Prompt Effectively:

* **Provide Context:** The bracketed sections […] are crucial. Fill them in with your specific details. The more context you give the reviewer, the better the feedback will be.

### Cover Letter Review Prompt

Write the cover letter yourself — it needs to be in your style and tone of voice. Then upload/ paste it into the AI along with this prompt

> *I am seeking a thorough review of my attached cover letter. I am applying for the [****Insert Job Title Here****] position at [****Insert Company Name Here****].*
> 
> *You can find the job description at this link: [****Paste the direct link to the job description here****]*
> 
> *My goal is for this cover letter to powerfully complement my CV, specifically highlighting my suitability for this high-impact role in [Specify your target area(s) if different from job title, e.g., applied Machine Learning, strategic Data Analytics, AI product development, etc.].*
> 
> *Please review my cover letter from the perspective of a hiring manager or a senior technical recruiter for this specific role and company. I am looking for both* ***positive feedback*** *(what resonates, what is persuasive) and* ***constructive criticism*** *(areas for improvement, what might be unconvincing or generic).*
> 
> *Specifically, please consider and provide feedback on the following aspects:*
> 
> ***Direct Response to Job Description & Company:***
> 
> *How effectively does the cover letter address the specific requirements, skills, and experiences mentioned in the provided job description?*
> 
> *Does it show genuine interest in this specific role and this particular company (mentioning company values, projects, or mission if appropriate and genuinely researched)?*
> 
> *Does it avoid sounding like a generic template?*
> 
> ***Opening Impact & Clarity:***
> 
> *Does the opening immediately grab attention and clearly state the position I am applying for?*
> 
> *Does it effectively introduce my core value proposition for this role?*
> 
> ***Highlighting Key Skills & Experience Alignment:***
> 
> *Does the letter convincingly connect my most relevant skills and experiences (from my CV) to the needs outlined in the job description?*
> 
> *Am I successfully showcasing specific achievements or projects that directly relate to the challenges or opportunities of this role?*
> 
> *Am I using keywords from the job description naturally and effectively?*
> 
> ***Demonstrating “High-Impact” Potential:***
> 
> *Does the letter convey my ability to make significant contributions, drive results, innovate, or take on complex challenges, as expected in a high-impact role?*
> 
> *Does it suggest qualities like leadership, strategic thinking, problem-solving aptitude, or a proactive approach, relevant to the role?*
> 
> ***Storytelling & Engagement:***
> 
> *Does the letter tell a compelling (but concise) story about why I am a strong fit, rather than just reiterating my CV?*
> 
> *Is it engaging and persuasive? Does it make the reader want to learn more about me?*
> 
> ***Enthusiasm & Cultural Fit (Implicit or Explicit):***
> 
> *Does the letter convey genuine enthusiasm for the role and the company?*
> 
> *Does my tone and language align with the likely culture of the company (e.g., innovative, analytical, collaborative)?*
> 
> ***Structure, Flow, and Conciseness:***
> 
> *Is the letter well-structured with a clear introduction, body, and conclusion?*
> 
> *Does it flow logically?*
> 
> *Is it concise and to the point (ideally no more than one page)? Are there any parts that could be cut or condensed?*
> 
> ***Call to Action & Closing:***
> 
> *Is the closing professional and confident?*
> 
> *Does it include a clear call to action (e.g., expressing eagerness for an interview)?*
> 
> ***Professionalism & Tone:***
> 
> *Is the tone appropriate for a professional cover letter in the Data/AI/ML/Analytics field?*
> 
> *Are there any grammatical errors, typos, or awkward phrasing?*
> 
> *Is the language clear, precise, and impactful?*
> 
> ***Overall Persuasiveness:***
> 
> *How convincing is the letter overall? Does it significantly strengthen my application for this specific high-impact role?*
> 
> *What is the single biggest improvement I could make to this cover letter?*
> 
> *What are its strongest points?*
> 
> *Please be as candid and specific as possible in your feedback. Examples of how to rephrase certain sentences or suggestions for aspects to emphasize further would be highly valuable.*
> 
> *Thank you for your time and expertise!*
> 
> 

**How to Use This Prompt Effectively:**

* **Provide ALL Information:** Make sure to insert your target job title, company name, and critically, the **link to the job description**. Without the job ad, the reviewer cannot effectively assess alignment.
* **Attach Your Cover Letter and CV:** The reviewer will likely want to see how the cover letter complements your CV.
* **Specify Your Target Field Clearly:** This helps the reviewer understand the lens through which to evaluate your letter.
* **Be Open and Act:** This is about making your application as strong as possible. Take the feedback seriously and revise.

### Hiring Company Analysis

Do you research into the company you’re applying to

**For this I’d strongly advise using a Deep Research AI**

> *I need to conduct thorough research on* ***[Insert Company Name Here]*** *to prepare for an interview for a Data/AI/ML/Analytics role. Please help me outline the key areas to investigate and suggest search terms or resources for each.*
> 
> *My main areas of interest are:*
> 
> ***Company Growth & Strategy:***
> 
> *Recent growth trajectory (revenue, employee count, market share).*
> 
> *Key strategic initiatives, new products/services, or market expansions, especially related to data, AI, ML, or analytics.*
> 
> *Future outlook and vision.*
> 
> ***Funding & Financial Health (if applicable, especially for startups/private companies):***
> 
> *Recent funding rounds (e.g., Series A, B, C), amounts, and key investors.*
> 
> *Overall financial stability and performance.*
> 
> ***Company Culture:***
> 
> *Stated company values and mission.*
> 
> *Work environment (e.g., collaborative, innovative, fast-paced).*
> 
> *Approach to work-life balance, diversity, and inclusion.*
> 
> *How they use or plan to use Data/AI/ML internally and in their products/services.*
> 
> ***Employee Feedback & Sentiment:***
> 
> *Common themes in employee reviews (e.g., on Glassdoor, Comparably, LinkedIn).*
> 
> *Pros and cons mentioned by current/former employees.*
> 
> *Insights into management style and career development opportunities.*
> 
> ***News & Recent Developments:***
> 
> *Any significant recent news, press releases, or media coverage.*
> 
> *Partnerships, acquisitions, or significant leadership changes.*
>

### Prompt for Interview Questions

It’s always important to have a few questions to ask during the interview

> *Please generate a comprehensive list of insightful questions I can ask my interviewer for this role: [Paste the direct link to the job description here].*
> 
> *I’m looking for questions that will help me:*
> 
> *Deeply understand the strategic priorities, key challenges, and success indicators for this role and its broader impact on the business.*
> 
> *Gauge the leadership style, team culture, cross-functional collaboration, and decision-making processes within the team and department.*
> 
> *Gain significant insight into the company’s long-term vision, strategic direction, and how this role contributes to broader organizational goals, particularly within [mention relevant broader domain if applicable, e.g., technological innovation, data-driven initiatives, business transformation].*
> 
> *Explore opportunities for strategic influence, leadership development, and career trajectory within the company.*
> 
> *Demonstrate my critical thinking, genuine interest in the role’s broader context, and alignment with the company’s strategic objectives.*
> 
> *Please provide questions suitable for different stages of the interview process (e.g., initial screen, technical/peer interview, hiring manager/leadership interview, executive interview). Wherever possible, categorize them (e.g., About the Role’s Strategic Impact, About Leadership & Team Dynamics, About Company Vision & Future, About Growth & Influence, Technical Acumen Questions, Business Acumen Questions).*
> 
> 

**Why this prompt is effective:**

* **Specificity:** Clearly states the type of role, which helps tailor the question relevance.
* **Clear Objectives:** Outlines *why* you want the questions, guiding the AI to provide questions that meet those needs.
* **Action-Oriented:** Focuses on understanding, learning, gaining insights, and demonstrating interest.
* **Practical Consideration:** Asks for questions suitable for different interview stages.
* **Optional Categorization:** Suggests a helpful way to organize the output.

### Introducing yourself

Typically at the start of an interview you will be asked to introduce yourself and your background. You can use a prompt such as the one below, with your CV attached. Vary the tone prompt according to your own personal style. This helps ensure that your verbal introduction covers the basics of what you curated into your CV — presumably the most important points.

> *Please generate an approximately 2-minute verbal introduction for me ([****Your Name****] — I’ll provide my name along with the CV).*
> 
> *This introduction should be based on my attached CV. When crafting it, please:*
> 
> ***Prioritise highlighting my recent experience through the lens of leadership, strategic impact, and broader business contributions****, rather than solely focusing on specific product features or highly granular technical execution. Emphasise how my work has influenced organisational or cross-functional outcomes.*
> 
> *Provide a concise indication of my overall career background, illustrating a progression towards strategic roles.*
> 
> *Ensure the tone is* ***straightforward, conversational, and reflective****, avoiding overly formal language.*
> 
> *Convey a* ***clear sense of direction****, articulating my aspiration for senior, strategic roles where I can drive significant organisational change, influence key business outcomes, or lead complex initiatives at scale.*
> 
> ***Infer and emphasise senior-level attributes, strategic contributions, and leadership experiences from my CV****, even if they are not explicitly stated with traditional senior titles.*
> 
> 

Note: this kind of prompt can also be useful for cover letters but should be combined with the specific target JD in that case.

### Writing a case study

#### **How to Use NotebookLM for Case Studies**

**Start a Focused Notebook:**

* Create a **new, dedicated notebook** for each major case study type or interview you’re preparing for. This helps maintain focus and prevents information overload. Name it clearly (e.g., “Leadership Case Study: [**Company Name/Problem Type**]”).

**Curate Your Sources:**

* **Upload the case study itself!**
* **Upload your core documents:** Your CV, the specific job description, and the company’s website (or key sections/annual reports).
* **Add company-specific context:** Upload recent news articles, investor calls transcripts, competitor analysis, relevant industry reports, or specific press releases related to the company’s challenges or strategic goals.
* **Include foundational knowledge:** Integrate your “personal reference bibles” — your favourite articles, frameworks, or methodologies on data mesh, agentic architecture, leadership principles, organisational change management, etc. This helps ground the AI’s responses in your preferred approaches.
* **Diversity of formats:** Leverage NotebookLM’s ability to process PDFs, Google Docs/Slides, pasted text, web URLs, and even YouTube video transcripts.

**Strategic Source Selection (Before Interaction):**

* Use the “Discover” feature initially, but treat its suggestions as a starting point.
* **Critically review and select sources:** Before interacting with the AI, quickly skim potential sources identified via “Discover” or your own research. Ensure they align with your experience, principles, and the strategic/technical challenges common in your target roles. Only include sources that you trust and find relevant to shaping your case study response. Toggle sources on/off as needed for focused queries.

**Iterative Q&A for Deep Understanding & Synthesis:**

* Instead of single questions, use a **conversational and iterative approach** with the Q&A feature.
* **Explore facets:** Start broad (“What are the key strategic challenges this company faces relevant to [role] and [domain]?”).
* **Drill down:** “Given these challenges, what are the primary technical/leadership considerations for solving X?”
* **Identify constraints:** “What are the common constraints (e.g., budget, team size, legacy systems, time) often associated with [type of problem] at [company type]?”
* **Synthesise information:** Ask it to “Summarise the key differences in [approach A] vs. [approach B] based on my uploaded sources.”
* **Test your hypotheses:** “Based on my CV and the job description, how might my experience in [X] be particularly relevant to [challenge Y] described in the company website?”

**Leverage Notes for Structure & Outline Building:**

* Use the “Notes” feature to **save key AI-generated answers, insights, and direct quotes**.
* Ask NotebookLM to **generate notes or outlines** directly from your Q&A sessions. For example, “Summarise the key aspects of [topic] from our conversation into a concise note for my case study outline.”
* Manually add your own thoughts and connections to these notes.

**Review and Refine:**

* Always **verify AI-generated information** against your original sources using the citations provided by NotebookLM.
* Use NotebookLM to help refine your wording, clarify complex points, or even generate alternative ways to explain concepts from your notes.

#### **Expert-Level Prompt for Generating a Case Study Skeleton**

This prompt is designed to be given *after* you’ve uploaded your relevant sources into NotebookLM. It instructs NotebookLM to act as a strategic advisor, drawing on all available information to create a comprehensive, interview-ready case study outline.

> ***Role:*** *You are an experienced Chief Technology Officer (CTO) / Head of Data & AI / Senior Engineering Leader / Strategic Business Consultant (choose the most relevant based on the job description). Your task is to help me, the candidate, prepare for a [Leadership/Technical] case study interview for the [Specific Role Title from Job Description] position at [Company Name].*
> 
> ***Context:*** *I have uploaded my CV, the job description, the company website, recent news/reports, and my personal “bibles” on [mention relevant topics like ‘data strategy’, ‘scaling AI systems’, ‘organizational leadership’, ‘agile transformation’]. My goal is to demonstrate strategic thinking, deep technical acumen (where applicable), strong leadership capabilities, and a results-oriented approach.*
> 
> ***Case Study:*** *[***describe the case study***]*
> 
> ***Task:*** *Generate a comprehensive* ***skeleton/outline*** *for a typical 45–60 minute interview on this case study*
> 
> ***Structure the skeleton with the following sections, ensuring each section includes guiding questions or prompts for my detailed content:***
> 
> ***Problem Definition & Strategic Context (5–7 mins):***
> 
> *How to clearly articulate the problem statement and its* ***business impact/strategic urgency*** *from the company’s perspective (drawing from JD, company website, recent news)?*
> 
> *What key assumptions need to be stated? What* ***critical clarifying questions*** *would I ask the interviewer?*
> 
> *How does this problem align with the company’s overall vision and my target role’s responsibilities?*
> 
> ***Analysis & Root Cause (10–12 mins):***
> 
> *What are the potential* ***root causes*** *(technical, organizational, process, people, market, data-related) based on the provided context?*
> 
> *What relevant* ***frameworks or mental models*** *(e.g., SWOT, Porter’s Five Forces, 5 Whys, Systems Thinking, Wardley Maps, etc.) could be used for analysis?*
> 
> *What* ***data or evidence*** *would I seek to validate these causes? (Connect to company’s current state/data practices).*
> 
> ***Solution & Strategic Options (15–20 mins):***
> 
> *What are 2–3* ***distinct strategic or technical solution options*** *to address the identified problem, considering scale, resources, and company context?*
> 
> *For each option, what are the* ***key pros, cons, and potential trade-offs*** *(e.g., cost, time, complexity, cultural impact, technical debt)?*
> 
> *What* ***decision criteria*** *would I use to select the optimal solution? (Emphasize business impact, feasibility, risk).*
> 
> *How does my specific experience (from CV) align with the proposed solutions, demonstrating* ***relevant leadership or technical expertise****?*
> 
> ***Implementation Plan & Execution (10–12 mins):***
> 
> *Outline the* ***key phases, milestones, and high-level steps*** *for the chosen solution.*
> 
> *What* ***resources (people, budget, technology)*** *would be required? How would I secure buy-in?*
> 
> *Identify potential* ***risks (technical, organizational, market)*** *and proactive* ***mitigation strategies****.*
> 
> *How would I* ***lead and empower the team(s)*** *responsible for execution? (Connect to leadership styles/principles from my “bibles”).*
> 
> *What would be the* ***stakeholder management and communication strategy*** *throughout implementation?*
> 
> ***Measuring Success & Iteration (5–7 mins):***
> 
> *What are the* ***key performance indicators (KPIs) and success metrics*** *for the chosen solution? (Focus on business outcomes and impact).*
> 
> *How would I* ***monitor progress and iterate****? What’s the process for feedback and adjustment?*
> 
> *What are the* ***long-term implications or next steps*** *after initial success?*
> 
> ***Personal Reflection & Learnings (Optional, integrate into other sections or concluding remarks):***
> 
> *What personal insights or learnings from my past experience would I apply here?*
> 
> *What broader principles guide my approach to problems like this?*
> 
> ***Output Format:*** *Provide a structured outline with clear headings, bullet points, and specific questions under each section to guide my thinking and content development. Ensure the language used in the outline is appropriate for a senior-level discussion.*
> 
>
