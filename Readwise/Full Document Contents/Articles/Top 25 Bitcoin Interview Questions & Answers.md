##### **Top Bitcoin Interview Questions**

Enterprise wants to test the breadth of knowledge of candidates required for addressing the roles and responsibilities of enterprise blockchain professionals. The top Bitcoin interview questions are generally the most commonly asked questions in almost any interview. The top questions basically focus on testing the basic knowledge of candidates regarding Bitcoin. Here are some of the notable additions among some of the common interview questions on Bitcoin.

######  **1. What is a Bitcoin?**

The definition of Bitcoin is undoubtedly one of the most basic Bitcoin questions you would come across in an interview. Bitcoin is basically a variant of digital currency which could be purchased, sold, and exchanged between two parties on the internet with high security. Bitcoin is an intangible asset that can be traded electronically only, and it could store value just like gold, silver, and securities.

######  **2. Who created Bitcoin?**

One of the top Bitcoin interview questions would undoubtedly focus on the creator of the unique cryptocurrency. The creator of Bitcoin, [Satoshi Nakamoto](https://101blockchains.com/who-is-satoshi-nakamoto-bitcoin/), introduced the cryptocurrency anonymously through a whitepaper in 2008. Subsequently, Bitcoin was released as [open-source](https://101blockchains.com/blockchain-open-source/) software and was the first successful virtual currency tailored with trust.

######  **3. What is Bitcoin mining?**

[Bitcoin mining](https://101blockchains.com/bitcoin-mining-guide) is one of the core concepts in the world of Bitcoin, with a specific group of individuals, known as miners, taking on the task. The process of Bitcoin mining involves the use of computers specialized with unique hardware for solving complex algorithmic equations.

Miners conduct Bitcoin mining by solving computational problems for creating a chain of connected transaction blocks. The specialized computers help in authentication of transaction blocks in the Bitcoin network. Miners receive rewards for addition of a new block to the blockchain in the form of Bitcoin alongside transaction fees.

######  **4. What is a Bitcoin wallet?**

A bitcoin wallet is basically a digital wallet that you can use for storing Bitcoins. In the technical sense, it is a software program that stores a secret number for each Bitcoin address. Furthermore, the wallet owner could send, receive as well as exchange Bitcoins with the help of Bitcoin wallet. Bitcoin wallet is available in different forms such as desktop wallet, hardware wallet, web wallet, and mobile wallet.

######  **5. What do you mean by a Bitcoin address?**

The Bitcoin address is the unique identifier that includes almost 26 to 35 alphanumeric characters. In most cases, the identifier starts with the digits 1 or 3, and it represents the location where the Bitcoin has to be sent. Bitcoin users could generate a Bitcoin address without any additional payment. On the other hand, the Bitcoin address is temporary and could change for each new transaction. As of now, you can find three common address formats as the standard. The three address formats include P2PKH, Bech32, and P2SH.

######  **6. Which agency takes care of Bitcoin governance?**

The governance of Bitcoin is also one of the common topics for top Bitcoin interview questions. It is important to understand that Bitcoin is not an organization, thereby excluding it from the limits of governance. Bitcoin is basically decentralized digital money, issued and managed without the involvement of any centralized authorities. Miners are responsible for verifying and generating new Bitcoins with their specialized computers, alongside maintaining [security](https://101blockchains.com/blockchain-security/) of the Bitcoin network.

######  **7. What is the reason for fluctuating Bitcoin prices?**

The fluctuating prices of Bitcoin could also be one of the crucial topics in Bitcoin questions. Bitcoin prices are fluctuating because of their highly volatile nature. With a limited number of Bitcoins in circulation, the creation of new Bitcoins slows down. Therefore, demand should follow the inflation level to maintain price stability. At the same time, Bitcoin is comparatively smaller in comparison to other industries. As a result, the market price of Bitcoin could move up or down without even significant amounts of money.

######  **8. What are the reasons to trust Bitcoin?**

Many interview questions on Bitcoin would also focus on the factors for which people should showcase their trust in Bitcoin. The trust factor in Bitcoin basically relies on the basic tenets of human faith in numbers, mathematical algorithms, and encryption. Bitcoin is a network-operated system where all participants are involved in the governance. Most importantly, the elements of [decentralization](https://101blockchains.com/decentralization-in-blockchain/) and technical freedom with open-source code and true [peer-to-peer](https://101blockchains.com/peer-to-peer-network/) technology also elevate trust in Bitcoin.

######  **9. Is double-spending possible with Bitcoin?**

One of the foundational tenets of Bitcoin focuses on addressing the issue of double-spending. Double spending basically refers to the instances where users attempt to use the same Bitcoin twice. However, there is a set limit of 21 million Bitcoins in the protocol, thereby ensuring restrictions on further creation of Bitcoins. Furthermore, each existing Bitcoin has unique traits which offer legitimacy to the transactions. Any user attempting duplicate transactions would receive notification about illegitimacy of the transaction.

######  **10. What are the benefits of Bitcoin?**

The important benefits of Bitcoin include payment flexibility, lower transaction fees, and reduced risks for merchants. In addition, Bitcoin also offers the advantages of improved security and control alongside ensuring improved transparency. Furthermore, decentralization ensures that Bitcoin is free from the control of any organization or individual, thereby verifying that it is completely neutral.

######  **11. What is a hard limit?**

The hard limit in the case of Bitcoin refers to the number of total Bitcoins in circulation. The concept of a hard limit suggests that Bitcoin could not be printed or stocked. It is basically a deflationary currency with growth in value based solely on its exchange value.

######  **12. What are the potential use cases of Bitcoin?**

The best Bitcoin interview questions would test the knowledge of candidates regarding the feasible applications of Bitcoin. First of all, Bitcoin presents promising scope for revolutionizing the existing global financial landscape. In addition, it could also serve other use cases such as voting mechanisms, wills, and trusts, future markets, initiating trusts, crowdfunding, and decentralized domain names.

######  **13. How can you purchase Bitcoin?**

You can purchase Bitcoin from different sources. It is possible to purchase Bitcoin online by using credit cards or e-wallets such as PayPal. Users can also purchase Bitcoin with the help of Bitcoin Teller Machines and LocalBitcoins. Furthermore, you can also find many verified online exchanges for selling and purchasing Bitcoins.

######  **14. How can you sell Bitcoin?**

You can sell Bitcoin in different types of ways, such as selling online to an exchange or other people living nearby. It is possible to sell Bitcoin in the same way as you purchase it. In addition, you should also note that Bitcoin prices fluctuate according to demand and supply. Furthermore, you can also use Bitcoin ATMs for selling Bitcoins.

######  **15. What type of products can you purchase with Bitcoins?**

Interestingly, Bitcoin helps in purchasing anything, starting from electronics, art, and food to clothing. Furthermore, you can use Bitcoin for purchasing [real estate](https://101blockchains.com/blockchain-for-real-estate/), cars as well as investment instruments like precious metals.

######  **16. What do you know about the legal aspects of Bitcoin?**

The legality of Bitcoin is also one of the top additions to the most popular Bitcoin interview questions. As of now, Bitcoin has achieved legal status in different countries across the world. On the other hand, there are many countries that have imposed restrictions on the use of Bitcoin. With varying cryptocurrency regulations from one country to another, it is reasonable to review regulatory policies for Bitcoin across different countries in the world.

######  **17. What is an unconfirmed transaction?**

An unconfirmed transaction in Bitcoin refers to a transaction that has not been completed or included in the block. Each transaction in Bitcoin needs one confirmation at the least for completing a transaction.

######  **18. What are the general reasons for unconfirmed transactions?**

The first common reason for unconfirmed transactions would be the delay in the verification of a transaction. Even if a user makes a transfer, the Bitcoin network would require a minimum of 10 minutes to include the transaction in the block. In addition, a low blockchain fee could also lead to unconfirmed transactions. Lower transaction fees automatically push down a transaction’s priority, thereby causing a delay in verification.

######  **19. What is the basic working of Bitcoin transactions?**

All Bitcoin transactions include an amount, which includes the sending address as an input. The receiver’s address is the output of the transaction, and private keys help in debiting Bitcoin from a user’s account. The Bitcoin network basically works as a database that maintains a detailed transaction history since its beginning.

######  **20. What are Full Nodes?**

Full nodes serve as the second layer in the security system of the Bitcoin network. The full nodes are basically a group of people working in an altruistic manner without any expectations for reward. The work of full nodes focuses on maintaining the security system in a completely clean state. At the same time, full nodes also ensure that Bitcoin does not garner any dubious reputation.

######  **21. What are the costs associated with Bitcoin transactions?**

The costs associated with Bitcoin transactions are also one of the notable topics for most popular Bitcoin interview questions. Bitcoin transactions generally include the miner fees which you pay to the miners for authenticating the transaction and adding to the network.

######  **22. What is a public key in Bitcoin?**

All Bitcoin addresses include a [public key and a private key](https://101blockchains.com/private-key-vs-public-key/). The public key is useful in scenarios where an individual sends money to another user while the private key helps in spending one’s own Bitcoins. The public key is an important part of the authentication process of Bitcoin.

######  **23. Is it possible to trade Bitcoin directly without selling at an exchange?**

Bitcoin offers the advantage of security and trust, thereby making it possible to trade Bitcoins directly. In addition, trading Bitcoin directly also relieves the burden of KYC requirements followed at the exchanges.

######  **24. How does blockchain resolve security threats for Bitcoin?**

Blockchain leverages some crucial principles for resolving security threats for Bitcoin. The principles include auditing, database security, application security, continuity planning, digital workforce training, and security testing.

######  **25. What are the setbacks of Bitcoin?**

The best Bitcoin interview questions would also draw the limelight towards the setbacks associated with Bitcoin. The commonly perceived setbacks with Bitcoin include the highly volatile nature of blockchain alongside the limited extent of acceptance.
