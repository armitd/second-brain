![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*B3i3Y4jbdReW7d0EKBGVjQ.jpeg)
The worlds of VR and AR can be very confusing. There’s a wide variety of products with wildly different functions. So here’s a chronological map for beginners of the topic, showing a casual look at where we’ve come from and where we’re going.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*DBjaZrZblP_H8uqw5XpHxg.jpeg)
Personal Computer:   

A desktop computer or a laptop computer. It consists of a 2D screen with a keyboard and usually a mouse or trackpad.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*C_nrJfMpVG6z9kEM75-q5A.jpeg)
VR Headset:  

Using motion detection, we can strap screens to our faces (one for each eye)that have changing images depending on where your head is and where it’s pointing, in order to create the illusion of a 3D environment all around you no matter where you look.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*Ulo2iTWtaIKx1JcrVDOCqQ.jpeg)
Full Body Motion Tracking:  

With cameras, we can detect where your body is and what position it’s in. In doing so, your body can be recreated by an avatar in a 3D environment that you can view on a screen.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*MO2T18ZlwGpqs9vjkJt9dg.jpeg)
Telepresence:

With a camera, microphone, and an internet connection, we can send a live footage video of you to be viewed on a tv almost anywhere else in the world.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*i7RBslP5YSXePRR44SwBpQ.jpeg)
Telepresence Robot:

By attaching the receiving screen onto remote controlled wheels, you can move your vantage point around.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*vBzbf5IQkPmnUEXLAoPtMw.jpeg)
3D Image Capture Cameras:

By capturing images of you from multiple angles, a 3D visual of you can be recreated in a computer.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*e_dmQ7MvjcT1amjkGe-YZA.jpeg)
Holoportation:

Using 3D image capturing cameras, your voice and body can appear anywhere in front of anyone with a AR or VR headset, anywhere in the world.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*fbF9YhGVFLaH46neevNZnQ.jpeg)
Mobile Device:  

A computer that you can hold in the palm of your hand and potentially connect to the internet anywhere you go. Typically the screen screen can be touched with your fingers in various different ways for various different actions.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*zjFJ-N1QVv6DpuJr15Q2IA.jpeg)
AR Headset:   

A pair of glasses with small projectors to overlay images on the lenses, creating transparent display screens, and combining them with motion tracking, we can see virtual objects overlayed over our physical reality wherever we go and interact with them by poking our fingers in the empty space where the object appears to be.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*eN7fuLwUtIxFp0n-qWhgCw.jpeg)
AR Contact Lenses:

Contact lenses with screens on them, creating transparent display screens, and combining them with motion tracking, we can see virtual objects overlayed over our physical reality wherever we go and interact with them by poking our fingers in the empty space where the object appears to be.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*zyUJwwfFFtoVO_BLlXrkNA.jpeg)
Haptic Feedback Glove:  

A glove with the capacity for physical stimulation when your phyical hand touches the empty space where a virtual object is supposed to be, to make it slightly more lifelike.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*AcQsgmZBZ-_KVRayGpSEMQ.jpeg)
Full Body Haptic Feedback Suit:   

An entire suit with the capacity for physical stimulation when your physical hand touches the empty space where a virtual object is supposed to be, to make it slightly more lifelike.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*z87T8PbJrYLx_P5jdPJOmg.jpeg)
Haptic Resistance Arms Rig:

Robotic arms that you can wear that only allow movement in the space outside of virtual objects, so that you can feel the full physical weight and hardness the objects.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*nWPVMfSgVMfoSwOLQW1-BA.jpeg)
Mobile Haptic Resistance Arms Rig:  

A glove held by robotic arms attached to a backpack, so that you can feel the full physical weight and hardness of virtual objects even while you’re on the go.   

This type of device is not yet being publicly developed by any company.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*t22B2yg1tYshl5WUfr4V6Q.jpeg)
Omnidirectional Treadmill:  

A slippery bowl with a harness, so that you can walk in place, in any direction, without actually going anywhere in the physical world.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*ZbaKDio9_OoS77zeVpzaYg.jpeg)
Flying Simulator Rig:  

A rig to move you around while laid out horizontally on your chest, to give the sensation of flying.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*O1kfdGNaBHvFTYUVzPHp3A.jpeg)
Driving Simulator Rig:  

A rig to move you around in your chair to give the sensation of driving.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*C-xWW-uryN1JmlhlPc2aLA.jpeg)
Suspended Motion-Simulator:  

A platform suspended by wires that pull and release in order to create large amounts of inertia.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*zKfB3RUqEnJVFwihEMIPWw.jpeg)
Full Body Haptic Resistance Rig:  

A robotic suit suspended in air that you can wear in order to only allow movement in the space outside of virtual objects, so that you can feel the full physical weight and hardness the objects on any part of your body.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*EuaQnf8ohKZd5Qmpev50UQ.jpeg)
Mobile Full Body Haptic Resistance Rig:  

A robotic suit suspended in air that you can wear in order to only allow movement in the space outside of virtual objects, so that you can feel the full physical weight and hardness the objects on any part of your body while on the go.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*Wz1CCREgZ7JBk116wu387g.jpeg)
Full Body Haptic Resistance Rig in Suspended Motion-Simulator:   

A robotic suit suspended in air that you can wear in order to only allow movement in the space outside of virtual objects, so that you can feel the full physical weight and hardness the objects on any part of your body, on a platform suspended by wires that pull and release in order to create large amounts of inertia.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*aBZGQGKlGQ7TwCukoBjc-g.jpeg)
Full Body Haptic Resistance Rig in *Mobile* Suspended Motion-Simulator:   

A robotic suit suspended in air that you can wear in order to only allow movement in the space outside of virtual objects, so that you can feel the full physical weight and hardness the objects on any part of your body, on a platform suspended by wires that pull and release in order to create large amounts of inertia, but held in a room on wheels or treads so that you can do so on the go.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*Bqnis0QQ7XaNaJzpGRz4aQ.jpeg)
Surround Sound System:

With sound speakers all around you with different inputs, you can have the perception of physical activity all around you.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*-BHzZdA2NbvCGFERbYZ-QQ.jpeg)
Headphones:

By attaching speakers to your ears, you can hear audio outputs while on the go.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*xGDLkaHsQZeZGEzmxvTYCg.jpeg)
3D Headphones:

By attaching multiple speakers at different locations around each ear, you can have the perception of physical activity all around you while on the go.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*qkPDUcsDy8deqdiGqGLqFw.jpeg)
Digital Scent Technology:  

Any smell is recreated for you on command.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*xkOqvv2y4bsFjhnO_KGxAQ.jpeg)
Digital Taste Interface:

Any taste is recreated for you on command.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*XVM4H4abpZSazsWs0jvQmw.jpeg)
You-know-what:   

Ask your parents about this one if you don’t know what I’m getting at here.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*ZX4lJizfAQUhbNZUvA4i1Q.jpeg)
Direct Brain Interface:  

A device for detecting brain activity, so that you can interact with the computer using thoughts alone.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*Gal2BA6SQdRpxcqnZxSYhQ.jpeg)
Your Brain in a Jar:  

The illusion of life is all you have anymore. But you can still use the internet.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*cxceG2id_bgeOxe7NfZcGA.jpeg)
“3D” Screen:   

A screen with two images, each only seen by one eye, in order to indicate the differences in depth of content. This is typically done by using glasses that filter out each of the two images to be seen by one eye each.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*ukuBndndk4AhNkptfsjRtw.jpeg)
Holographic Display:   

A screen with two projectors pointed at it, to create a 3D virtual objects without glasses.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*JMMKlx3HNxbIFS9CtkyqwA.jpeg)
Touch-able Air Blast Hologram:  

A gun that shoots air at your fingers whenever they touch a virtual object to give the illusion of touching said object.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*dEtKseh0fMMVe3kXRiCBBw.jpeg)
Free Space Projection Hologram:  

A 3D virtual object made by pointing a projector wherever you want it to appear.   

This type of device is not yet being publicly developed.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*-J7WgJoslWyo7PsNGlFklQ.jpeg)
Mobile Full Body Haptic Resistance Rig with Touch-able Air Blast Free Space Projection Hologram:

A robotic suit suspended in air that you can wear in order to only allow movement in the space outside of virtual objects that also includes 3D virtual objects made by pointing a projector wherever you want it to appear and a gun that shoots air at the fingers of anyone else whenever they touch a virtual object, so that you can feel the full physical weight and hardness the objects on any part of your body while on the go and give the illusion of touching said object to anyone around you.

This type of device is not yet being publicly developed.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*wkF8dlPj2YuYgZTF5Ibs-g.jpeg)
Robotically Self-Assembling Structures:  

Building blocks that have mechanical parts for the purpose of moving themselves around and even forming structures by themselves when programmed with a goal.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*LMDErs1RgidtTu4wnNO0Ow.jpeg)
Claytronics:  

Tiny building blocks that have mechanical parts for the purpose of moving themselves around and even forming structures by themselves when programmed with a goal.

This type of device is not yet being publicly developed.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*SkQ8360yHVu37bhiPCaK0g.jpeg)
UAV Drone:   

Remotely controlled helicopters.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*weIr6uuZ4tvj7lrsBz9S0Q.jpeg)
Coordinated UAV Drones:   

Helicopters that are programmed to fly in formations.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*cAqyilpcjBuo-_3s4Ez4Lg.jpeg)
“Bladeless” Fan:   

A fan with the blades tucked in so that they don’t get caught in anything.

This product is currently made by Dyson.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*JHwL3W0phxrtvKvP013yZQ.jpeg)
“Bladeless” UAV Drones:  

Robotically controlled helicopters with the blades tucked in so that they don’t get caught in anything.

This type of device is not yet being publicly developed.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*8C9Wiy3xMkMl0EEOHQjTGw.jpeg)
Flying Claytronics:   

Tiny robotically controlled helicopters with the blades tucked in so that they don’t get caught in anything as they form physical objects that can be touched by a user.

This type of device is not yet being publicly developed.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*3y1l5vcb0DhMKxqT8uFzHA.jpeg)
Mind Simulation:  

You don’t have a body or even a brain anymore. You only have the thought patterns. But you can still use the internet.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*AK13-U3Vd9Eug-OmLN6iMg.jpeg)
Magnetic Full Body Haptic Resistance Rig:  

Cuffs that attach to parts of your body with magnets in order to only allow movement in the space outside of virtual objects, so that you can feel the full physical weight and hardness the objects on any part of your body without being held in a robotic exoskeleton.

This type of device is not yet being publicly developed.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*b1A5LXKuyK1kCU7n5Mltvg.jpeg)
Implanted Full Body Internal Haptic Resistance Rig:  

Implants in parts of your body with magnets in order to only allow movement in the space outside of virtual objects, so that you can feel the full physical weight and hardness the objects on any part of your body without being held in a robotic exoskeleton or having to wear bulky cuffs outside of your body.

This type of device is not yet being publicly developed.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*LzFnZAANq8ZC-do0VQBu4w.jpeg)
Full Body Internal Removable Nanotech Haptic Resistance Rig:  

Implants in parts of your body with magnets in order to only allow movement in the space outside of virtual objects, so that you can feel the full physical weight and hardness the objects on any part of your body without being held in a robotic exoskeleton or having to wear bulky cuffs outside of your body or inserting permanent implants into .

This type of device is not yet being publicly developed.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*W-yRjt0JoCTVFkEXvUnZrg.jpeg)
Magnetic Full Body Internal Removable Nanotech Haptic Resistance Rig:  

Implants in parts of your body so that you can feel the physical push of virtual objects without being held in a robotic exoskeleton or having to wear bulky cuffs outside of your body or even being held at the waist.

This type of device is not yet being publicly developed.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*eqDKBisaC4IQrbyYZ_7VeA.jpeg)
Flying Magnetic Full Body Internal Removable Nano-tech Haptic Resistance Rig:  

Implants in parts of your body with magnets above a platform with helicopter blades attached to it so that you can feel the physical push of virtual objects without being held in a robotic exoskeleton or having to wear bulky cuffs outside of your body or even being held at the waist while being transported to places inaccessible by land vehicles as a result of rough or wet terrain.

This type of device is not yet being publicly developed.
