### ⚠️ Older item, included with disclosure: Genesys acquires Pinkfish, adding 25,000 MCP-tool integrations to Genesys Cloud AI
**Publication date:** 2026-06-30 — 18 days old, well outside the standard 7-day window. Flagged anyway because it's new to your vault, dated and confirmed (not a rumour), and directly intersects two of your named active projects (MCP Governance and Contact Centre of the Future) via a vendor already on your Competitive Watchlist.

**Relevance:** Genesys is on your Competitive Watchlist as a CCaaS vendor under evaluation for Contact Centre of the Future. Pinkfish is an agentic-orchestration company built on MCP — its acquisition is a concrete example of MCP adoption inside a live CCaaS platform, not a hypothetical.

On 30 June 2026, Genesys announced its acquisition of Pinkfish (terms undisclosed) to accelerate "autonomous customer experiences." Pinkfish brings MCP-based tool integration and workflow automation spanning more than 500 integrations and roughly 25,000 MCP tools across CRM, ERP, IT, HR, order management, and billing systems. Genesys plans to make Pinkfish capabilities available via its AppFoundry marketplace by end of July 2026, with native embedding into Genesys Cloud by end of January 2027. The stated goal is a "Genesys Cloud Agentic Virtual Agent" that can resolve complex customer requests end-to-end without human hand-off.

**Strategic Implications:**
- This is a live, dated example of an MCP-server ecosystem (25,000 tools) being absorbed wholesale into a CCaaS platform Belron may evaluate — the MCP Governance framework needs a concrete answer for "what happens when a vendor's agent can reach 25,000 MCP tools by default," not just Belron-authored MCP servers
- For CCOTF, this sharpens the Genesys evaluation: the vendor is moving fast on agentic/MCP capability, which should be weighed alongside the EBC observation already on your watchlist that Genesys's core CX problems (routing, agent productivity) remain unsolved underneath the AI layer
- Worth cross-referencing against the MCP Enterprise-Managed Authorization stable release (covered in your 15 July brief) — Pinkfish's 25,000-tool surface is exactly the kind of estate EMA-style connection governance is meant to control
