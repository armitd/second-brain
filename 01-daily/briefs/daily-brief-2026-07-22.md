## High Impact News

### Gemini 3.5 Pro misses third launch target; Google scrapped and restarted the base model
**Relevance:** Directly affects the AI Damage Assessment PoC, which has been tracking a three-model benchmark set (Claude Fable 5, GPT-5.6, Gemini 3.5 Pro) — Gemini remains the missing piece.

Bloomberg reported (16 July) that Google is months behind schedule on Gemini 3.5 Pro, its flagship model, after enterprise testers flagged coding performance gaps, token-efficiency issues, and weak long-task reasoning. Google reportedly scrapped the original base model in June and restarted pretraining; the model missed its internally targeted July 17 ship date — the third missed date this cycle. Gemini 3.5 Flash is already shipping; Google is reportedly weighing a stopgap "Gemini 3.5 Flash" release while Pro remains in internal testing and limited enterprise preview, with no official model card, pricing, or benchmarks published.

**Impact Assessment:**
- **Projects Affected:** AI Damage Assessment PoC (three-model benchmark set), Belron AI advocacy narrative (comparative capability positioning)
- **Potential Effects:** The PoC's benchmark comparison is effectively a two-model comparison (Claude Fable 5 vs. GPT-5.6) until Gemini 3.5 Pro ships or a decision is made to substitute Gemini 3.5 Flash. If Google's own enterprise testers are flagging coding/reasoning gaps, that's a data point worth citing in any "why Claude" advocacy conversation — a direct competitor's own QA is surfacing capability gaps publicly.
- **Action Suggested:** Update the PoC benchmark plan to note Gemini 3.5 Pro's continued unavailability; decide whether to proceed with a two-model comparison now or continue waiting.
