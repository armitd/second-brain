## High Impact

### A2A Protocol Turns One — 150+ Organisations, Microsoft Ships Agent Framework 1.0
**Relevance:** You own the MCP/A2A governance question for Belron EA. This is the most concrete signal yet that the architecture has crossed from experimental to enterprise-production. Your governance position just got both stronger (more justification) and more urgent (more pressure to have an answer).

On 9 April 2026, the A2A Protocol project (hosted under the Linux Foundation, alongside MCP) announced its one-year milestone:

- **150+ supporting organisations**, up from 50 at launch, including AWS, Cisco, Google, IBM, Microsoft, Salesforce, SAP, and ServiceNow
- **22,000+ GitHub stars** and SDKs now available in Python, JavaScript, Java, Go, and .NET
- **Active production deployments** confirmed across multiple industries (not just pilots)
- **Microsoft Agent Framework 1.0** shipped this week with full MCP tool support and A2A 1.0 landing imminently — this is the clearest enterprise stamp of approval yet

The architecture is now settled: **MCP = resource layer** (agents connect to tools, APIs, data sources); **A2A = networking layer** (agents coordinate across frameworks and org boundaries). Any enterprise building multi-agent systems this year is building on these two protocols.

**Impact Assessment:**
- **EA Governance:** The question is no longer "will MCP/A2A win?" — it has. The question is "who owns this inside Belron, and what does the governance model look like?" This lands squarely in EA territory.
- **Vendor Lock-in Risk:** 150 orgs including all major cloud providers means no single vendor controls the stack — this is structurally good for Belron's independence
- **Action Needed:** If you haven't already, the one-year milestone is a natural trigger to bring a short MCP/A2A briefing to EA leadership — "here's what just standardised, here's what it means for us"

### Anthropic Overtakes OpenAI Revenue — $30B ARR, IPO as Early as October
**Relevance:** You're planning a damage assessment PoC and tracking foundation models. The vendor you choose is about to go through a major liquidity event. This is the context in which to make that decision.

A significant shift in the foundation model landscape this week:

**Anthropic** reported a $30 billion annual revenue run rate in March 2026 — a 58% single-month surge — surpassing OpenAI's reported $25 billion ARR for the first time. Anthropic is now evaluating an IPO as early as October 2026 that could raise $60B+ at a $380B valuation. Supporting this: Google and Broadcom have committed 3.5 gigawatts of compute capacity by 2027 (conditional on commercial milestones), addressing the constraint that has been limiting Claude's availability.

Anthropic also announced **Project Glasswing** — a cybersecurity coalition testing the unreleased Mythos model to identify zero-day vulnerabilities before they're exploited. The coalition includes major tech and security firms given early access in exchange for responsible disclosure. (Note: Mythos itself will NOT be publicly released due to its cybersecurity risk profile — it's too capable in the wrong hands.)

Meanwhile **OpenAI** is dealing with accumulating internal turbulence: a New Yorker investigative profile questioning Sam Altman's trustworthiness, reported CFO tensions over IPO timing, and a media acquisition (TBPN) that reads as a narrative control move rather than a strategic product decision. OpenAI is still enormously capitalised ($852B valuation, $122B raise closed March 31), but the drama creates an interesting opening for Anthropic.

**Impact Assessment:**
- **PoC model choice:** Claude's improved availability (backed by Google/Broadcom compute) reduces the reliability concerns that made GPT-4o/Azure the safer enterprise default

### Ravin AI Pilots US Collision Market Entry — Watchlist Update
**Relevance:** Direct competitor/reference point for the damage assessment PoC. Ravin is doing exactly what you're exploring — AI-based vehicle damage detection from photos — and they just expanded into the US repair shop market.

Ravin AI (Israeli-founded, Austin HQ, $35M raised) is actively piloting with an unnamed US multi-shop collision operator (MSO) after establishing deployments in Australia and New Zealand. Key differentiators from others in the field:

- **Hardware-agnostic**: uses standard CCTV cameras and mobile photos — no proprietary hardware installation required
- **Scale**: 2 billion vehicle images processed on their proprietary system
- **Capability**: flags secondary issues (e.g., "check suspension") from damage patterns, not just repair estimate generation
- **Investors**: Shell Ventures and Firemark Ventures (IAG's VC arm) — insurer money is behind this

Ravin enters a field that now includes Tractable, Claim Genius, Proov Station, UVeye, Wenn, and BayWatch Technologies — all pursuing AI-based intake and estimation for body shops. The market is crowding fast.

**Competitive Implications:**
- Ravin's hardware-agnostic model is the most relevant architectural reference for a Belron PoC — no bespoke camera rigs, just existing CCTV and customer phones
- Firemark Ventures (IAG's VC arm) investing signals insurers are backing their own vendor here — worth knowing which of Belron's insurance partners have relationships with these vendors
- The US collision market is ahead of UK/Europe on AI adoption — patterns there tend to reach here 12-18 months later

### OpenAI + Anthropic + Google Form Anti-China AI Coalition
**Relevance:** Geopolitical signal that affects how long-term AI vendor dependencies should be evaluated — particularly if Belron's European opcos face different regulatory constraints on US AI vendors.

On April 6-7, OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google announced coordination through the **Frontier Model Forum** to share intelligence and prevent Chinese AI companies from stealing their models via adversarial distillation (the practice of training a cheaper model to mimic the outputs of a more capable one without paying for access or using proprietary data).

This is the first public instance of the three main foundation model competitors cooperating formally on a competitive threat. The framing is IP protection, but the practical effect is tighter controls on API access, more aggressive rate limiting, and potentially stricter enterprise KYC requirements.

**Strategic Implications:**
- API access to frontier models may become more restricted over time — another argument for hybrid/open-source model strategy (LLaMA, Mistral) for sensitive or high-volume use cases
- EU AI Act + US export controls + this coalition = a tightening regulatory-commercial environment around frontier AI — worth tracking for Belron's EU opcos specifically
- Mistral's positioning as a European, non-aligned model provider becomes more strategically interesting in this context

**Relevance:** You're an EA at a large enterprise navigating AI adoption. This data validates what you're probably already sensing — the technology is ahead of the organisation's ability to absorb it.

The Deloitte State of AI in the Enterprise 2026 report and WEF workforce research this week paint a consistent picture:

- **84%** of organisations have not redesigned roles or workflows around AI capabilities
- **120 million workers** are at medium-term redundancy risk because they're unlikely to receive reskilling
- **80% of the engineering workforce** needs upskilling by 2027 just to keep pace with generative AI evolution (Gartner)
- Workers with AI skills command a **56% wage premium** over peers without (PwC)

The gap isn't AI capability — it's organisational readiness. Companies are buying tools faster than they're changing how work actually gets done.

## Technology Watch

### Integration Is the New Enterprise Architecture — Agentic AI Makes Data Plumbing Foundational
**Relevance:** Directly reshapes the EA mandate. As agentic AI moves into production, real-time data integration becomes the critical dependency — and the EA who owns integration strategy becomes the EA who owns AI operations.

Key insight from this week's analysis: "Over 80% of digital initiatives now require seamless system-to-system connectivity" and AI-driven enterprises with real-time data flows achieve revenue growth up to 2× faster. But the deeper point is structural — agentic AI systems degrade rapidly when fed stale or inconsistent data. The EU AI Act additionally mandates full auditability and data traceability for AI systems.

Four shifts redefining enterprise architecture in 2026:

1. **Real-time data orchestration** is no longer optional for AI — batch pipelines break agentic workflows
2. **80% of integration users** will be outside formal IT by end of 2026 (Gartner) — EA governance needs to scale without bottlenecking
3. **Hybrid readiness** is critical: legacy systems without APIs become AI blockers, not just technical debt
4. **AI Act compliance** requires end-to-end lineage and explainability baked into the integration layer — this is an EA design concern, not a data team bolt-on
