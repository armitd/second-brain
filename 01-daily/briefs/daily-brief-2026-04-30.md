### White House Blocks Anthropic Mythos Expansion — Government AI Governance Escalates
**Relevance:** Signals that Anthropic's security posture and government relationships carry enterprise risk — relevant context for any Anthropic-heavy AI strategy at Belron.

The White House has formally opposed Anthropic's plan to give Mythos access to ~70 companies, citing security concerns. The background is complex: the Pentagon had earlier designated Anthropic as a supply chain risk after Anthropic refused to allow its models to be used in domestic surveillance and fully autonomous weapons. Simultaneously, the White House is drafting separate guidance to *restore* federal access to Anthropic tools for controlled cybersecurity use.

What makes Mythos sensitive: during its preview period it identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS and web browser. Axios reports (29 Apr) that Trump officials are actively negotiating a path to restore access despite the current standoff.

**Strategic Implications:**
- The supply chain risk designation is a government-specific issue; it does not currently affect commercial enterprise deployments
- However, it signals that powerful AI models are now subject to government oversight in ways that could expand — worth monitoring for enterprise governance
- Anthropic's refusal of surveillance/autonomous weapons use cases demonstrates it will defend its acceptable use policy even against government pressure — generally a positive signal for enterprise customers

### MCP Lazy Loading Identified as Key Next Optimisation
**Relevance:** Direct input for the MCP Governance project — lazy loading is the next performance and cost lever for MCP deployments.

A developer has identified lazy loading as the critical next optimisation for MCP deployments: loading only the tools needed for each specific task rather than initialising all tools upfront. This directly addresses the "MCP tools always loaded = most expensive context" problem covered in previous sessions, and would significantly reduce per-interaction token costs.

**Technology Implications:**
- Relevant architecture decision for Belron's MCP Governance standards — mandate lazy loading patterns in MCP server design
- Reduces the cost gap between MCP-based and skill-based agent approaches
