## Strategic Developments

### California SB 988 — Industry Pushes Back on Safelite-Advantaging Provision
**Relevance:** This is a live US regulatory story directly affecting the competitive dynamics between Safelite (Belron's US brand) and independent auto glass shops. The AGSC's language makes the competitive implication explicit.

California SB 988 — the **California Motor Vehicle Glass Act** — is advancing through the State Senate after amendment on March 23. Industry opposition is now active.

**The bill's key provisions:**
- **Claim number requirement:** Auto glass shops must obtain an insurance claim number from the customer *before* they can formally accept a job
- Bans assignment of benefits
- Requires ADAS recalibration disclosure
- Mandates itemized invoices
- Prohibits improper inducements

**Why the industry objects — and who it helps:**
The AGSC's core argument is pointed: the claim number requirement forces customers to go through a **third-party administrator (TPA)** before a shop can begin work. Safelite runs **Safelite Solutions**, one of the largest auto glass TPAs in the US. Requiring a TPA-issued claim number before service acceptance structurally channels customers toward Safelite's ecosystem and "places small and independent glass shops at a distinct competitive disadvantage."

Independent shop owner Jimmy Saldana (Showcase Auto Glass): *"That can be confusing for customers and limits consumer choice... it also slows the process down."*

**Current status:**
- Amended March 23 from insurance code to civil code (broader scope, AG enforcement)
- Progressing through State Senate
- Penalties: $500 first violation, $2,000 subsequent — civil enforcement by city/district attorneys or CA AG
- AGSC has filed formal opposition; coalition of California shops submitted opposition letters

**For Belron/Safelite context:** If passed, SB 988 would create a structural advantage for Safelite in California — one of the largest US auto glass markets. Belron/Safelite have obvious incentive to see this legislation pass; the AGRR independent sector has equally strong incentive to see it fail or amended. Watch for similar model legislation spreading to other states (NCOIL model bills typically do).
