### ⚠️ Older item, included with disclosure: Virginia enacts ADAS recalibration disclosure law for auto glass shops
**Relevance:** Direct operational relevance — this is exactly the kind of legislative pattern flagged on your Competitive Watchlist as "originating in the US, tends to reach UK/Europe." First mention of this specific law in your vault.

**Publication date:** 2026-07-02 (law effective 2026-07-01) — outside the standard 7-day window, flagged per COG verification rules rather than silently included.

Virginia's HB 312 took effect on 1 July 2026, requiring auto glass repair and replacement providers to: (1) inform customers whether their vehicle has ADAS requiring recalibration after windshield replacement, (2) disclose whether the shop will perform that recalibration itself, (3) notify customers in writing if recalibration can't be done in-house and advise them to seek it elsewhere, and (4) provide written confirmation of recalibration success or failure after the work is done. Industry groups and Virginia auto glass companies were involved in shaping the bill, which ended up narrower than an originally-proposed broader fraud-prevention measure. Separately, the federal "ADAS Functionality and Integrity Act" (now folded into H.R. 7389, the Motor Vehicle Modernization Act of 2026) passed the House Energy & Commerce Committee on 21 May and is heading toward a full House floor vote — it would direct NHTSA to set standardised calibration guidelines starting with MY2028 vehicles.

**Impact Assessment:**
- **Projects Affected:** None of your four active named projects directly, but squarely in the "Belron/auto glass" and "ADAS/windscreen industry" watch areas, and relevant background for any Belron US operations (Safelite) and future UK/EU consumer-disclosure regulation
- **Potential Effects:** State-by-state ADAS disclosure mandates are proliferating (this follows earlier bills flagged in your vault); Safelite's US operations will need per-state compliance tracking, and this is a plausible template for future UK/EU consumer-protection rules on calibration transparency
- **Action Suggested:** Flag to whoever tracks US regulatory compliance for Safelite whether HB 312's disclosure requirements are already operationalised; monitor H.R. 7389's House floor vote as the more consequential federal-level development 📅 2026-07-20

**Publication date:** 2026-07-01 (restoration announcement) — outside the standard 7-day window; the underlying incident ran 9 June–1 July 2026.

Anthropic released Fable 5 and Mythos 5 on 9 June 2026. On 12 June, the US Commerce Department ordered Anthropic to suspend both models worldwide (including for US users, since nationality couldn't be verified in real time) after Amazon researchers found a jailbreak that could get Fable 5 to identify software vulnerabilities and, in one case, write exploit code. Anthropic worked with the government for roughly two and a half weeks, trained a new safety classifier that blocks the specific jailbreak technique with "more than 99% effectiveness," and had Fable 5 restored across Claude.ai, the Claude Platform, Claude Code, and Claude Cowork on 1 July after Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick approved the reversal. Mythos 5 remains restricted to ~100 vetted US companies and federal agencies. Anthropic also announced it is working with Amazon, Microsoft, Google, and other "Glasswing" partners on a shared industry framework for evaluating and responding to model jailbreaks.
