## Strategic Developments

### **Update — EU AI Act:** Digital Omnibus Moves August 2 Deadline to December 2027
*First covered 12 June 2026 — material update: the August 2 enforcement date has effectively moved.*

**Relevance:** Yesterday's brief flagged August 2, 2026 as "7 weeks away." That date still stands — but a provisional political agreement now defers the actual enforcement obligations for most high-risk AI systems by 16 months.

On 7 May 2026, negotiators from the European Council, Parliament, and Commission reached provisional agreement on the **Digital Omnibus on AI**, amending the EU AI Act's implementation timeline:

- **Annex III high-risk AI systems** (including AI used in employment, education, credit) → obligations deferred to **2 December 2027**
- **Annex I systems** (AI embedded in regulated products) → deferred to **2 August 2028**

Formal adoption and publication in the Official Journal is expected in the coming weeks, before August 2. The agreement still needs Council and Parliament endorsement but is effectively agreed at political level — organisations that were racing to comply for August 2 now have significantly more runway.

**Strategic Implications:**
- The extension applies to Annex III systems. Contact centre AI (if making employment-related decisions) and damage assessment AI (if used in insurance decisions) could fall under this category — worth a formal classification exercise even with the extension
- The extension does **not** remove the obligation — it moves the deadline. Organisations that use the extension purely as a pause risk compressing compliance work into a harder 2027 deadline
- Prohibited practices (August 2025) and GPAI model obligations remain on their original timelines — only Annex III/I high-risk obligations moved
