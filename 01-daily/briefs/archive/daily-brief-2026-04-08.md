### Anthropic Research: AI Already Does a "Huge Portion" of White-Collar Jobs — But Adoption Lags
**Relevance:** This is the most authoritative data yet on the gap between what AI *can* do and what it *is* doing — directly shapes how you should frame AI literacy investment internally.

Fortune (April 7) published an interview with Peter McCrory, Anthropic's head of economics, alongside new research from Anthropic measuring "observed" vs. "theoretical" AI exposure by occupation. Key numbers: coding roles have ~94% theoretical AI exposure but only ~30% actual observed task automation; finance and market research sit similarly — very high ceiling, much lower floor today. McCrory's framing is significant: the trajectory will be shaped "not just by capabilities as they advance, but by the choices society makes" and his team explicitly wants to ensure transition costs are "not unequally borne."

**Impact Assessment:**
- **Relevance for AI literacy case:** The gap between theoretical and actual exposure is the strongest argument for investing in AI literacy *now* — companies that close that gap fastest will capture the largest ROI differential
- **Framing shift:** The debate isn't "will AI replace us?" — it's "which organisations learn to deploy AI on the 94% ceiling vs. staying at the 30% floor?" Your EA role can drive that trajectory
- **Entry-level jobs signal:** A separate data point from Anthropic confirms businesses are leaning into automation for entry-level roles specifically — affects your graduate/junior hiring pipeline planning

### Mercedes Commits $4B to Alabama Plant — GLC Production Moving to U.S. in 2027
**Relevance:** Tariff-driven production onshoring by a major OEM signals the composition of the U.S. vehicle fleet will shift — with implications for parts sourcing and service volumes in your sector.

Mercedes-Benz announced a $4 billion investment at its Alabama SUV plant through 2030, with capacity to rise from ~300,000 to ~340,000 units annually. The headline move: its best-selling model globally, the GLC compact crossover, will begin U.S. assembly in Alabama in late 2027. The driver is explicit — tariffs wiped out $1.2B in Mercedes profits, contributing to a 57% profit plunge. Mercedes joins Honda and Hyundai in announcing major U.S. production expansions in direct response to Trump tariff pressure. Canada has until April 13 to submit comments on counter-tariff structure.

**Impact Assessment:**
- **Fleet composition:** More U.S.-built Mercedes models means a stronger pipeline of ADAS-equipped premium vehicles in the domestic fleet — the GLC is heavily sensor-fitted, relevant for calibration demand
- **Parts sourcing:** OEM onshoring *can* affect aftermarket glass supply chains — watch for any Mercedes announcement on component localisation
- **Broader signal:** This is tariff policy working as intended; don't expect reversal. The domestic vehicle fleet will continue to be shaped by which OEMs have U.S. plants

## Industry Watch — Windscreen & Auto Glass

### HLDI Study: ADAS Bundles Cut Crash Claims Up to 39% — But Surviving Claims Cost More
**Relevance:** This is the most significant piece of ADAS claims data published this year — it directly defines your industry's dual opportunity: fewer incidents but higher-value repairs.

The Highway Loss Data Institute (HLDI) published findings from a study of 2018–2023 Mazda vehicles with stacked ADAS features. The full bundle (AEB, FCW, pedestrian detection, adaptive cruise, lane departure, rear AEB, driver attention alert) was associated with a **39% reduction in property damage liability claims** and a **21% reduction in bodily injury claims**. However, claim severity for crashes that do occur increased ~4% — because ADAS eliminates low-speed, low-cost incidents while the remaining claims involve more complex vehicle damage, including sensor-mounted windshields.

**Industry Implications:**
- **Good news for windscreen specialists:** Fewer total glass jobs, but each replacement on an ADAS-equipped vehicle commands a calibration premium — this study validates that ADAS vehicles are entering the fleet at scale
- **Pricing justification:** The severity data supports premium pricing for ADAS-equipped glass work; insurers now have hard data on why these repairs cost more
- **Technician training urgency:** As ADAS penetration deepens, calibration competency becomes a survival requirement, not a differentiator — accelerate workforce upskilling timelines

### Federal Bill Targets ADAS Calibration Standards for 2028+ Vehicles
**Relevance:** This legislation, if enacted, will set the baseline expectation for calibration quality across your industry.

H.R. 6688 — the ADAS Functionality and Integrity Act — is a bipartisan bill (sponsored by Rep. Diana Harshbarger, R-TN) advancing through the House Energy and Commerce Committee. It would require NHTSA to publish calibration *guidelines* (not mandatory regulations) for any ADAS system on vehicles model year 2028 and later that have undergone aftermarket repair or modification. AGSC's attorney issued a March 2026 update noting the bill's scope is deliberately narrow — it does not impose hard compliance obligations but creates an official guideline baseline. Industry bodies including SEMA back it.

**Strategic Implications:**
- **Prepare now:** 2028 vehicles are already in product development. When guidelines land, companies that have already built calibration processes to a high standard will need minimal adjustment — laggards will scramble
- **Competitive moat:** Positioning your company as "NHTSA-guideline ready" before the standards are final is a genuine differentiator for fleet accounts and insurance partnerships
- **EA implication:** If your company runs a service management system, now is the time to design calibration workflow tracking that can surface compliance documentation — future audits will ask for it

### White House National AI Legislative Framework — Federal Preemption of State AI Laws
**Relevance:** Sets the regulatory backdrop for any AI tooling your company deploys — and simplifies the compliance picture considerably.

⚠️ *Note: Framework released March 24, 2026 — slightly outside 7-day window. Included due to strategic relevance and sustained analysis activity in early April.*

The Trump White House released a National Policy Framework for AI (March 24) that does something unusual: it explicitly recommends **federal preemption of state AI laws**, arguing a patchwork of state rules would create compliance burdens that inhibit AI deployment. Key workforce components: integrate AI training into existing federal workforce programs (not create new ones), study AI-driven labour market impacts, and support land-grant institutions in building AI curriculum. The framework is legislative recommendations to Congress, not executive orders — it requires Congressional action to become binding.

**Strategic Implications:**
- **Simplified compliance horizon:** If federal preemption passes, your company needs one AI governance framework, not jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction state law tracking — a significant EA simplification
- **Workforce training investment:** The framework's approach (integrate into existing programs vs. new spend) signals government won't do the heavy lifting on workforce AI upskilling — enterprises must self-fund
- **Watch for:** Congressional response to preemption provisions — tech industry broadly supports, civil liberties groups opposed. Timeline uncertain

**Sources:**

## Market Intelligence

### EV Tax Credit Expired + 25% U.S. Sales Drop Projected for H1 2026
**Relevance:** Slower EV fleet penetration extends the life of ICE vehicles and delays ADAS-heavy EV calibration volume — relevant for your demand forecasting.

The $7,500 U.S. federal EV tax credit has expired. Without the subsidy, EV sales are projected to drop up to 25% in H1 2026 — compounding the GM Factory Zero layoffs flagged in yesterday's brief. Global EV forecasts have been revised down as a result. BYD is bypassing tariffs by establishing production in Turkey and Hungary; Western OEMs are doubling down on U.S. onshoring. The net effect: EV fleet growth continues, but slower than the 2024–2025 trajectory assumed.

**Market Impact:**
- The mix of vehicles needing windscreen replacement skews back toward ICE/hybrid longer than expected — plan your parts inventory and technician skill mix accordingly
- ICE vehicles age longer when replacement costs rise (tariff pass-through) — continued tailwind for repair demand
- The ADAS transition still happens, just stretched over a longer timeline — no change to strategic direction, adjust pace of investment
