## Strategic Developments

### EU AI Act: High-Risk AI Deadline Remains 2 August 2026 Despite Streamlining Deal
**Relevance:** Belron's IPO is targeted for H2 2026. The August 2 date falls squarely inside your IPO preparation window. Any AI system in scope for high-risk classification could become a material disclosure item in the prospectus.

On May 7, the EU Council and Parliament reached a provisional agreement to streamline certain AI Act rules — postponing AI regulatory sandbox establishment to August 2027 and reducing the grace period for generated-content transparency to 3 months. However, the **August 2, 2026 enforcement date for Annex III high-risk AI systems** (AI used in employment, credit decisions, education) is unchanged. Penalties for non-compliance reach €35M or 7% of worldwide annual turnover. The Commission is currently consulting on draft guidelines for both high-risk system classification and AI transparency obligations.

**Strategic Implications:**
- The streamlining agreement is mostly about sandboxes and supervision competence — the enterprise compliance deadlines are untouched.
- If any Belron AI use case (e.g. AI in technician scheduling, automated insurance processing, AI Damage Assessment) touches employment or financial decisions, it may be in-scope for August 2.
- The 7% of worldwide turnover penalty scale is material enough to require prospectus disclosure if there is any compliance uncertainty.
- Belron's EA team should confirm classification of active AI initiatives against the Annex III list before July — ideally with legal sign-off.

**Sources:**

### Safelite Solutions: Logo'd Glass Parts Now Face OEM-Level Claims Scrutiny
**Relevance:** Safelite Solutions (Belron's US claims administrator arm) changed its parts approval policy on May 1, affecting how independent shops in the Belron network get paid for logo'd glass.

Effective May 1, 2026, Safelite Solutions began processing NAGS logo'd parts under the same requirements as OEM parts. Shops must obtain prior insurer approval before fitting a logo'd part, provide proof of purchase documentation with the invoice, and follow OEM documentation guidelines. Invoices without prior approval and proof of purchase will be paid down to non-logo'd NAGS rates. Independent glass shops have raised concerns about extended processing times and distributors' ability to supply next-day proof of purchase. The change has been described as increasing Safelite's leverage over the independent shop network.

**Strategic Implications:**
- This increases administrative complexity for Belron's non-Safelite opco network if they are processing US claims through Safelite Solutions.
- For Autoglass/Carglass in the UK/EU, this may signal a future direction for parts quality control policy — watch for similar moves from UK insurers.
- The "logo'd parts face OEM scrutiny" dynamic also touches the AI Damage Assessment PoC — if the assessment system needs to recommend specific glass types, part classification complexity will matter.

**Sources:**
- [glassBYTEs.com: Logo'd Parts Face OEM-Level Scrutiny](https://glassbytes.com/2026/05/logod-parts-face-oem-level-scrutiny/) (Tier 2) — May 2026

### Salesforce Agentforce Contact Center Now in General Availability
**Relevance:** Contact Centre of the Future project — Salesforce has entered the native CCaaS market with an agentic-first platform. This reshapes the vendor landscape you are evaluating.

Salesforce launched Agentforce Contact Center (AGCC) into general availability, building a native CCaaS platform on the Salesforce platform that integrates voice, automation, CRM data, AI agents, and digital channels into a single system. The architecture is "agentic-first": AI agents handle simple cases autonomously and escalate to human agents for complex or emotionally sensitive interactions. Gartner predicts agentic AI will autonomously resolve 80% of common customer service issues by 2029, reducing operational costs by 30%. Microsoft's Dynamics 365 Contact Center also added real-time voice AI with ultra-low latency reasoning in April 2026. The CCaaS market is stratifying between platforms with deep contact-centre architecture (Genesys, NICE, Parloa) and CRM-first platforms adding CCaaS (Salesforce, Microsoft).

**Market Impact:**
- If Belron uses Salesforce CRM at any opco, AGCC becomes the default CCaaS extension path — lowest integration friction, single vendor.
- The "agentic AI as first line of service" model is now the market consensus, not a fringe position. Any evaluation of Zoom's CCaaS pitch or legacy telephony should be measured against this standard.
- The Zoom EBC observation (from May 2026 watchlist) — that AI is being layered on structural CCaaS problems rather than solving them — applies more to Zoom's position than to Salesforce or Microsoft, which own the CRM data layer.
