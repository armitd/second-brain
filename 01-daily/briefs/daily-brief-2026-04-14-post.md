### SAIL — Shared AI License Foundation Launched (Anthropic, Meta, IBM, Microsoft)
**Relevance:** Open-source AI IP governance. Affects how Belron should think about AI model licensing risk.

On 8 April, a coalition of AI leaders launched the Shared AI License Foundation (SAIL) — the first organisation dedicated to AI innovation protection through a collaborative patent network. Founding members: Anthropic, Genentech, IBM, Meta, and Microsoft. The intent is to create a shared defensive patent pool that prevents open-source AI models from being ambushed by aggressive patent claims. This mirrors what the Open Invention Network did for Linux. For Belron, this reduces the legal risk of building on open-weight models like Gemma or LLaMA for a production damage assessment system.

## Auto Glass & Industry Intelligence

### Update: Illinois HB 4373 Clears House Insurance Committee Unanimously
**Update:** _First covered 2026-04-09_

**Relevance:** Watchlist: Safelite / legislative landscape. Material update — bill has passed its first vote.

Illinois HB 4373, the ADAS calibration disclosure bill, has passed the House Insurance Committee with a unanimous vote. The bill requires auto glass shops to notify customers about ADAS systems and confirm whether calibration is needed before windshield work proceeds. This moves it from "consideration" to "on track to pass." If signed into law, it sets a US template. Previous brief noted that Safelite/insurer interests are the driving force. The unanimous vote removes the most likely obstacle (insurance lobby resistance) — suggesting the insurer side is now aligned.

**Competitive Implications:**
- Belron's US opcos (Safelite is the competitor, not the operator here — Belron's US brand is Safelite's rival) need to track whether compliance with calibration disclosure becomes a competitive differentiator or a cost burden.
- In the UK and Europe, this legislative trajectory is a leading indicator — ADAS calibration mandates are likely to arrive via Consumer Duty or DVSA guidance within 12-18 months.
- Strengthens the case for Belron investing in a standardised ADAS calibration data layer (part of the EA roadmap argument).
