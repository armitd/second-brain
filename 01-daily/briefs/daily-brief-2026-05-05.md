### AI Coverage Insurance Launches as Enterprise Liability Category
**Relevance:** A new insurance product category for enterprise AI liability is now commercially available — this is the risk management infrastructure that unlocks AI deployment at scale for regulated or risk-averse organisations like Belron.

A new AI Coverage Insurance product has launched targeting enterprise AI liability — covering model output failures, data leakage from agentic systems, and third-party harm from autonomous AI decisions. The emergence of this product category follows the EU AI Act's liability provisions (in force since August 2025) and signals that the insurance market now views enterprise AI risk as quantifiable and insurable rather than unquantifiable.

**Market Impact:**

## Technology Watch

### Teams Are Replacing Slack with Markdown Files, Prompts, and MCP Servers
**Relevance:** This is the clearest real-world signal yet that agentic workflows are crossing from experiment to practice — directly relevant to MCP Governance and how Belron should think about the collaboration layer under an agentic AI operating model.

Developer community discussion (high engagement, multiple threads) documents teams actively replacing Slack channels with a combination of: shared markdown files (as async communication medium), structured prompts (as team protocols), and MCP servers (as the integration and action layer). The pattern: instead of a Slack channel for "engineering updates," a markdown file that agents can read and write; instead of DMs for requests, a prompt template that triggers an agent action via MCP. swyx (Latent Space, Tier 2) characterised this as "the agentic team topology emerging in practice."

**Technology Implications:**
- For MCP Governance: this is the adoption signal that moves MCP from developer curiosity to enterprise governance requirement. If teams are building workflows where MCP is the action layer, governance standards need to be in place before, not after, this pattern reaches Belron's engineering teams
- The markdown-as-communication pattern is a natural fit for Second Brain / COG-style knowledge structures — worth tracking as a workflow complement
- Risk: if Belron's collaboration tooling strategy assumes Slack-as-primary indefinitely, the next 12–18 months may require a re-evaluation
